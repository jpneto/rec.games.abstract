What you describe might be called "Stavropol Hex". There are some obvious differences with Stravropol Checkers:

- Checkers can end in a draw

- In Checkers you can find opponent's moves that are actually bad for him

- Stavropol requires you to either move your own side or the opponent's, not both in one turn



Hex cannot end in a draw, so your Stavropol Hex will eventually lead to a decision. But the moves you make for your opponent cannot (!) be bad, so you have to minimize their 'goodness'. You have to place them as inefficiently as possible. This goes against my wishes as a player. For the record, I don't like Stavropol Checkers for the same reason, but at least you can employ some interesting tactics. That doesn't seem to be the case here.
