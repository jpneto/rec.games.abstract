bailey@sun.lclark.edu (James Bailey) writes:


	I have heard that while some very good chess programs have been
written for computers up to the Grandmaster level, no one has been able to 
write a program that enables a computer to play at such a level. The
implication being that checkers is far more difficult at the professional
level than is chess. Has anyone else heard of this?


There is a checker program ("Chinook") which has beaten all but one
of the world's best checkers players.  This makes it the second best
player in the world.  There is a tournament proceeding at the Computer
Museum in Boston now between Chinook and Marian Tinsley (the champion),
who hasn't lost a match since 1955 (! -- not a typo).  There has been
a long discussion of this on rec.games.chess, with running match reports.
