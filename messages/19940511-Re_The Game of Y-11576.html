Just to follow up in a small number of ways: 

"Y", which I believe is the same game as "Poly Y", is basically Piet
Hein's classic game of Hex on a modified board. The Games reviewer (Wayne
Schmittberger) argues that "Y" is actually in some ways an improvement
over Hex because of the modification of the board. 

Most of the vertices of the board are six-pointed-- that is, each vertex
connects to six other vertices. However, three (symmetrically placed)
vertices near the center are five-pointed. This give the board its
characteristic "bowed" effect, and creates an interesting dynamic. In Hex,
and in most similar games, plays at the center of the board are
overwhelmingly more significant than plays at the edges. In "Y", this is
not as true, because of the "star points". These points connect to fewer
points than the others do, and this counterbalances their positional
importance.

I have not played "Y"; alignment games are not among my favorites.
However, Wayne Schmittberger is a big fan of them, and if he says Y is a
superior game, he's probably right.

It's also worth pointing out that Kadon Enterprises makes _first-class_
games. Without exception, Kadon's games are very well-constructed, and are
usually beautiful to boot.
