Basic principle: When you lose excessively, you tend to blame the game.  It's the game's fault, in this case Hex Oust. 

  http://www.marksteeregames.com/Oust_rules.pdf

Correction.  After Raph, and after Corey suffered a huge losing streak, one day I was out of sorts (if you can possibly imagine) and playing terribly all day.  Corey won once and played again immediately.  Not the slightest hesitation.  He won the second game and then played again later in the day, breaking his self imposed limit of two HO games per day.  He won the third game, noting that his limit of two was really a limit of two losses - **totally proving my point**.  Don't blame the game.

After that, Corey embarked on another withering losing streak and has now abandoned HO because it no longer has "asceticism", lol.

Basically it's time to move up to a larger board, from base 7 to base 8, since a game can now essentially end in about 35 moves (though it can still go well over 100).  A larger board was long anticipated, at least by me, and will only enhance the tactical array.

I'll need some new recruits though.  I keep a board open every day at iggc while I practice the sax.  I can't watch the board continually, so if you stop in to play, don't say "hi" and run off after 30 seconds.  Give me ten minutes to notice your arrival.
