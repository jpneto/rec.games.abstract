How does the Blocker move in La Trel?  I want to try the game with
my own equipment before I drop $30 on it (that's the _least_
expensive set of what is essentially a chess variant), and the rules 
on the Net are somewhat vague.

A Usenet post from 1994, paraphrased from the _Encyclopedia of 
Chess Variants_, outlines the basic rules of the game but 
describes the Blocker's move thusly:  "Moves up to 3 squares 
any direction except sideways and can change direction(s) 
during a move... (I realize the description of the movement of the 
blocker is ambiguous.  I really can't guess what moves might 
be allowed.)"

  http://groups.google.com/groups?&selm=ZYz2neE.ejackman%40delphi.com

A review of La Trel from The Game Cabinet is somewhat more 
explicit, saying, "[Blockers] are given an enhanced knight's move 
wherein each piece can move up to three squares in any direction."

  http://www.gamecabinet.com/sumo/Issue16.17.18/LaTrel.html

My question: is the Blocker a "slider" or a "leaper"?  That is,
can it be blocked by other pieces in its way, like a Rook in
ortho-Chess, or does it leap over pieces in its way, like an
ortho-Chess Knight?  

Any other clarifications you can make would also be welcome.
