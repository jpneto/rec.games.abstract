I played checkers for the first time in twenty-some years the other day.
The end-game resulted in two kings versus one king. There seem to be
situations where this is a draw. Can anyone confirm this? Is there a

I haven't played in years either, but [pulling out my handy chess
board] I do find one draw.  Or rather, two symmetric ones.  I don't
remember if the board should be set up as in chess, but I will assume
that pieces can sit on a1, b2, c1, etc.   Then the following is a
draw:  R:  Ka3, Kc1;  B: Kc3; R to move.  There, and in the other
corner are the only draws I see.

The trickiest win is with B: Kc5, Kd6;  R: Ka7.  If Red is to move,
just make the moves symmetric about a8-h1.  1 d6-c7 a7-b8  2 c5-d6
b8-a7  3 c7-b8 a7-b6  4 b8-a7 b6-a5  5 d6-e5 a5-b4  6 e5-d4 b4-a5 or
b4-a3 7 d4-c3 winning next move.

Anyway, that's my take.  But, like I said, I haven't played in years.
