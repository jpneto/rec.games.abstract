Here's a few other European checkers/draughts variants named for various countries just to confuse matters further. Although I'm not sure if they actually originated in these countries, or are merely named for them. I like to say that games are like languages -they have dialects and change as they go from place to place.

Italian Checkers
    Exceptions from standard British, or American Checkers:
    Players must capture opposing pieces whenever possible, or forfeit the game.
    Only kings can capture kings.
    If there is a choice of possible jumps, the player must take a king rather than a single piece.
    Players must also jump the maximum number of pieces possible, as well as the most powerful pieces (again, jumping kings rather than men).
---------

Spanish Checkers

    Played as Italian checkers, with these exceptions:
    If there is a choice of captures, the player must take the option which takes the maximum number of opposing pieces.
    Kings may move any number of spaces along open diagonals, and they can capture by jumping an opposing piece to land any number of diagonal spaces away.


German Checkers
    Played as Spanish Checkers, except:
    Men can make jumps backwards, or forwards, but otherwise can only move forward as in standard Checkers.
    A piece is crowned only if the move lands and stops on the opponents edge row of squares. If there are additional jumps that are possible that would take the piece away from the edge, it must take the jumps immediately -without- being crowned.
    Kings may move any number of spaces along open diagonals, and they can capture by jumping an opposing piece to land any number of diagonal spaces away.
    Players must capture opposing pieces whenever possible, or forfeit.
    Only kings can capture kings.
    If there is a choice of possible jumps, the player must take a king rather than a single piece.
    Players must jump the maximum number of pieces possible, as well as the most powerful pieces (again, jumping kings rather than men).


Russian Checkers
    This variant is like German checkers in that men can jump opposing pieces either forwards, or backwards, however:
    A player with a choice of jumps is not required to take the larger number of pieces.
    A man reaching the far row is crowned, and then may continue to make any additional possible jumps as part of the same move.


Irish Draughts
    Roll a single die and you must move a single piece the number of spaces indcated by the dice. If you don't have enough room to move that far you lose a turn.
