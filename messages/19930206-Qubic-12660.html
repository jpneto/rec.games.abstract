Qubic is three dimensional four-on-a-side tic-tac-toe.  Players take
turns placing their pieces on a 4 by 4 by 4 cube.  First to get four
in a row (straight or diagonally) wins.  I believe it was recently
proven to be an assured win for the first player in a two player game.
But this isn't at all obvious in regular play.  It's not a trivial game.

Some company (which probably hold a trademark on "Qubic") markets a board.
But it's easily played on paper.

I discovered two transforms, which transform a game position into an
equivalent position (e.g. wins remain wins) which looks completely
different.

The four (or higher) dimensional variant is also interesting.
