The purpose of your P(s) is to find a value for the original
poster's p(x), correct?

p(x) is the probability of B winning from the given initial
position. You are calculating this probability, in terms of
the probabilites of winning from all the possible positions that
can result after one bid.

Strictly from a practical point of view, how are all those
probabilities to be calculated? It would seem, the same
process would have to be applied to most of those positions
as well, which would imply that most of the (uncountably infinite?)
2-ply positions have to be evaluated the same way, etc.
