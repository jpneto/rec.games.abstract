matt wrote:
Could someone clarify this query please?
In the game of Pool, if the cue ball is potted by the opponent, where
can you place the cue ball, in the D or behind the line?

Since american pool tables lack a "D" (that is a snooker-ism) it'd have to be behind the line (aka "in the kitchen").

Exceptions exist depending on the variant of pool, however.  In 9-ball, it becomes "ball in hand" anywhere on the table.  In 8-ball, it's "in the kitchen".
