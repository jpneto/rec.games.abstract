OK. You haven't stated the rules of Twixt, so I'm going to have to
guess. Play alternates, and a turn consists of placing one of your
pieces on an empty square. You win if you get a chain of your pieces
between the opposite sides of the square board that have been designated
as yours. Pieces that are diagonally adjacent are considered to be next
to each other when we define "chain". Is that right?

In that case, the game cannot end in a draw because if one player never 
gets a chain between his sides of the board then the other player must 
have a chain between his.

It now follows that the game is a first-player win, because one or other
player must have a winning strategy, and if it were the second player the
first player could play his first move at random and then play the second
player's winning strategy with an extra piece.

If I was wrong about the connectivity (if diagonals don't count, say) then
it might be possible to get a draw (say, if the players place pieecs in a
checkerboard pattern).

If HexMeister is a game like the above but played on a board of hexagons,
then again the game cannot end in a draw, and again it is therefore a win
for the first player.

None of this gives much help in finding a winning strategy, unfortunately.
