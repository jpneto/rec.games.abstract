Tuomas T Korppi <korppi@cc.helsinki.fi> writes:

|>  I do not have to consider pros and cons of getting a
|> corner since there are only pros.

This sounds wonderfully obvious, so much so that I feel challenged to query it.

Of course, one can easily believe there would be positions where you have
the chance to take a corner, but it is better not to because something else
is more urgent.  One could even believe that this might happen even when it
means allowing the opponent to take the corner in question.

BUT:  is it possible to go even further.   I, and the poster excerpted above,
believe not, but a proof or counter-example would be nice.   That is:-


  Can you construct a position where, with optimal play by both, player A
  would win; but that if one corner stone were artificially changed from
  a B to an A, and no other change made, then with optimal play B would win?
  =========================================================================


If one could do this, that would be VERY interesting indeed!
