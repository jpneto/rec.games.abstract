An alternative method for assigning scores to each group, which I
haven't tested, but which nonetheless intrigues me greatly:

Each of Player 1's red groups (even a single red stone is considered a
"group") gets a score.  A red group's score is the total number of
blue stones in the blue groups that the red group touches minus the
total number of pink groups on the board.

Each of Player 2's green groups (even a single green stone is
considered a "group") gets a score.  A green group's score is the
total number of pink stones in the pink groups that the green group
touches, minus the total number of blue groups on the board.
