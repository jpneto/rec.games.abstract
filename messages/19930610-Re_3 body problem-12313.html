For zero sum games with 3 or more players, one may also need a further
restriction, namely one prohibiting coalition formation. Consider, for
example, the usual attitude towards cooperation amongst players in
poker, or the like.

Anatol Rapoport, in chapter 18 of his _N-person Game Theory_,
describes a four person zero sum game called ``So Long Sucker'' in
which coalitions are allowed. [The game was invented by Hausner, Nash,
Shapley and Shubik.] After describing some of the double-crosses that
are possible in the game, Rapoport remarks:

   ``We gather what Martin Shubik meant when, in describing the
     game, he wrote `The four inventors of the game still occasionally
     talk to each other'.''
