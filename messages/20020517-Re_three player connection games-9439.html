Not only that, but an interesting game would probably be played by
interesting players; in other words, it may not be useful to assume
the moves could be made in any random fashion. Just because a random
fill of the board might result in zero or three winning paths, doesn't
mean an interesting and balanced game does not exist. From a mathematical
standpoint, it might have a certain appeal, but "interesting game"
is really a very different goal.

Anyway, not all games that have been classified as "connection type"
satisfy this mathematical criterion. In Havannah, for example, you
can win by making a loop around a vacant or opponent-occupied cell
or group of cells. Kaliko is for two to four players; points are awarded
for extending paths and making loops. See http://gamepuzzles.com/
