As many of you probably know, I keep The World of Abstract Games website
[www.di.fc.ul.pt/~jpn/gv] where are, at this moment, 3 different sections,
namely,

i) Go Variations;
ii) Games of Soldiers (i.e., games played with Go stones)


Your set-up for Tibetan Go (Mig Mang) is wrong. You show the set-up for
Sun Chang Patuk (traditional Korean Go). There are no stones in the center
of the board in Tibetan Go, only those around the edge. It is weird to find
Tibetan Go in the "Games of Soldiers" section. The rules of the game
are very close to the rules of Go / Wei-Xi and both games are historically
related to each other.
