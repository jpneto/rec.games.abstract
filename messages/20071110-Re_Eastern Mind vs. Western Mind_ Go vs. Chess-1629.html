went east and west, and as it went west, the Muslims removed the dice

There's no record of dice being used in Indian Chess - at least, not in the two-player game. The first mention of four-handed dice Chess occurs in an early 12th-century Sanskrit document, where it is treated as a minor variant. See also Murray, A History of Chess, p.48. (Eales, in Chess: The History of a Game, is more ambivalent, but offers no evidence for the existence of a two-handed dice Chess.)
