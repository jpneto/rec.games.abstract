On Tuesday, December 18, 2012 12:12:02 AM UTC+1, Luis Bolaños Mures wrote:

Losing the game by being forced to play on some cell when passing would win instead is zugzwang, but that's impossible in Hex because an extra stone can never harm you.


It's interesting to note that zugzwang is possible in Slither, though. For instance:

5 | o x x o x
4 | x x . o o
3 | o o . x x
2 | x x . o o
1 | o x o o x
..   ---------
..   a b c d e

(x goes top-bottom)

This is even zugzwang regardless of who plays first: Black d3-c2 c4 is met by White e4-d3 c3, while White b3-c2 c4 is met by Black b3.

Of course, mutual zugzwang implies the possibility of cold wars. They could happen on bigger boards where players would routinely fill the rest of the board in an attempt to avoid being the first to make a move in the central area of this pattern. Of course, the probability of this happening in an actual game is exceedingly low.
