Barney Pell (bdp@cl.cam.ac.uk) wrote:
: My METAGAME WORKBENCH contains an implementation of chinese-chess as
: an instance of symmetric chess-like games.  
: However, this implementation does not capture *all* the rules,
: the most significant limitation being that horses and elephants
: leap directly to the final square, whereas in the complete rules
: they must travel through an intermediate square which must be
: empty.  
: 
: I am not a very good chinese chess player, but have been told that
: this dramatically alters the game.  Can any experienced players tell
: me how much they think this difference should affect the overall
: strategy of the game?
: 

This is a very important rule in the game of Xiangqi (Chinese Chess).
In International Chess, the Knights' movements and powers are restricted by
the pawn structure in the opening and middle game.

However in Xiangqi, without such rule, the soldiers are not 
able to restrict the movements of the Horse the same way as the pawns do.

The Horse will become very active very early in the game.
And all the known tactics and strategies of the game will have to be
redefined. This becomes a new game altogether. See what happen if you
implement such an 'obstacle' rule for Knights in International Chess.
