Unlike tic-tac-toe, you get a point each time you make the scoring pattern
and the game continues.


 my hexagonal N-in-a-row game, "4's", with 3 directions to build, gives
 you 1 point for each set of 4-in-a-row you can make without an intervening
 stone of the other color.  so you can use the 5th diagonal down Pascal's
 triangle to lookup the value of N in a row:

           N             points
      ------------------------------
          <4                0
           4                1
           5                5
           6               15
           7               35
           8               70
           9              126
          10              210
          11              330

 since my wooden board was 11 dots on a side, it made for
 a total of 331 dots.  the fact that a row along it's edge
 would be 11 dots long for 330 points gave me a smile.


I know it's not the same as a four player version.  I believe a four playuer
version of the game would have all the issues that four player abstract
games have.  For example, one player's situation becomes hopeless but she
gets to decide which of the other three players wins.


 you could always play partners, 1st & 3rd against 2nd & 4th,
 perhaps with a team goal.  of course, this is really just a
 fancy 2-player variant.
