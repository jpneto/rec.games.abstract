A reasonable mnemonic, but I liked the one I invented better, (naturally!)

Conway has another in "Winning Ways", involving renaming the I and L as O and Q,
(very dodgy!), and renaming F as R (it looks a little like a small cursive r);
and renaming N as S, which is actually better than N, IMHO.  Then the names are
OPQRSTUVWXYZ,   which is very easy to remember!  

Unfortunately that O/Q stuff is unacceptable.  

My version uses S for N, but otherwise as above...

|>            _
|>           |_|     _                  
|>    _ _    |_|    |_|       _ _      _ _ _    _ _ _
|>  _|_|_|   |_|    |_|      |_|_|   _|_|_|_|  |_|_|_|
|> |_|_|     |_|    |_|_     |_|_|  |_|_|        |_|
|>   |_|     |_|    |_|_|    |_|                 |_|
|> 
|>    F       I      L        P        S          T
|>                                        _
|>            _       _          _      _|_|    _ _
|>  _   _    |_|     |_|_      _|_|_   |_|_|   |_|_|
|> |_|_|_|   |_|_ _  |_|_|_   |_|_|_|    |_|     |_|_
|> |_|_|_|   |_|_|_|   |_|_|    |_|      |_|     |_|_|
|> 
|>    U         V       W        X        Y      Z


Then the mnemonic is   "FLIP  S to Z" , (a natural geometric operation!)
.                       ~~~~~~~~~~~~
In other words...       FLIP  STUVWXYZ.

Ta-dah!!!
