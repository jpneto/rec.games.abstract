ISAAC@utaphy.ph.utexas.edu (ISAAC M. MANDELBERG) writes:


I played checkers for the first time in twenty-some years the other day.
The end-game resulted in two kings versus one king. There seem to be
situations where this is a draw. Can anyone confirm this?


It depends which version of checkers you are refering to (there are over
100 known variations):


For 8X8 draughts, "checkers" (popular in North America and Great Britian).
	In this variant, kings can only move one square at a time.

	Two kings against one is always a win, except for one special conditon.
	Using chess notation, put a white king on A1 and C1 (or A3) and a black
	king on C3.  White can never get kis kings free from the side of the
	board.

For 10x10 draughts, "international checkers" (popular in the former Soviet
	Union and the Netheralnds).  In this variant kings can move many
	squares at a time.

	Two kings against one is a draw.
