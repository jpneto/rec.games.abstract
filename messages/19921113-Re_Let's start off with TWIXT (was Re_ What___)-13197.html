Well, Jerry, if the game board were only 10x10 I would agree with you that red
could win in either of the ways you show. However, on the full 24x24 board
black may be able to prevail, depending on just where on the board this
position is located. For example, in your first variation black could answer
j5 with k6, linking to h5. If red then plays g4, double linking to j5 and f6,
black comes back with j4, linking to k6. This would be particularly strong for
black if the "diagonal" j4-k2-l1... eventually reaches black's border instead
of red's. In the second variation, black might try responding to g5 with k4,
linking to h5. If red then plays j6 linking to g5, black could "fall back" with
n7. Red's second move of the game, f6 linking to d5, violates the opening
principle that you should not start a fistfight too soon. The opening phase
is a time to "stake out territory." For example, red might have played h1
instead of f6. Black might then make some distant move between red's d5 peg
and red's upper border. A well-played game frequently fills up most of the
board. I hope this makes sense.
