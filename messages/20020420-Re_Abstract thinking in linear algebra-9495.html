This is extremely helpful to me.  I'm glad I "vented."  The following
two comments especially help me see what my goals should be as I learn
more and more engineering.  

I tend to favor the proof-lecturers when I choose a professor.  But
I'd like to go from proof to manipulation, to proof, in a continuous
loop.  I find it hard to learn either one in the absence of the other.
When I was a high schooler, I would write "proofs" of my algebra
problems ("by the Distributive Law"...).  I wasn't taught that way,
but I was always interested in why I was "allowed" to do this or that
manipulation.  I was fascinated by the process of writing a string of
true statements that all depend on each other.  This kind of learning
style takes a lot of time, and I'm lucky if I get through even 1/2 of
the problems (hw).  But I also tend to go for the hard ones.  They're
more fun anyway.  

Everyone's comments helped in big or little ways.  I appreciate them.
