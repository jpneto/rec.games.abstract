I've played more Monkey Queen than anybody at this point, and the current
balancing mechanism, the pie rule, is more than adequate to balance the game
for the current range of skill levels.  What determines the winner in Monkey
Queen is the ability to detect checkmate opportunities and effective
follow-through, not who moved first.  Gamerman beats me every time
regardless of who goes first, regardless of whether the pie rule is in
effect, and I'm quite certain, regardless of any new balancing mechanism we
might try - simply because he's a more skilled player.

If players become so skilled at Monkey Queen in the coming years that the
pie rule is no longer deemed adequate, then said skilled players can figure
out a more sophisticated balancing mechanism.  There's no way I'm going to
kludge out the rule sheet with some unnecessary, misguided "balancing"
mechanism designed by clueless, beginner Monkey Queen players that
accomplishes anything but.

Ready, fire, aim.  Let's develop a body of Monkey Queen strategy before
embarking on the quest for its perfect balancing mechanism.
