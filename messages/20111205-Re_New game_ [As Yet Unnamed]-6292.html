There's an important difference.  In Byte, you move your stack closer to the
nearest stack, of either owner.  In Ayu, you move a unit closer to the
nearest *friendly* unit.  I think that would have been a problem in Byte,
looking back, because an enemy stack could stand between two friendly
stacks, creating equidistant paths.  I don't remember why that would have
caused a problem, but...   It was many years ago.


It seemed a problem at first, but it was a false alarm. It can happen
that you move a unit closer to the closest friendly unit and your
opponent places a unit in between, thus changing the distance between
your units and forcing you to move in the oppossite direction. Still,
this doesn't cause a cycle. For example:
