Query: If a move would take you onto a cleared square or off the board,
do you move as far as possible without entering a cleared square? Or is
the move prohibited?


The move is prohibited in this case.

Actually, there is one situation that I am unsure of. At the end of
the game, when the player lands in a position with no legal moves,
it may be possible to make one last move in any direction that travels,
as you said, as far as possible without entering a cleared square.

This is the method used by the Unix version of greed that I have. The
OS/2 version just ends the game upon entering a situation with no legal
moves.


Also I assume two diagonal clearing paths may cross
as long as they are on "opposite color checkered" squares, i.e. they
do not clear the same square. Yes? Thanks....


Yes, as long as no square is cleared twice the move is legal.
