In article 9946@laas.laas.fr, doucet@ripolin.laas.fr (Jean-Etienne Doucet) writes:


Rules are roughly as follow:

- at his turn, each player moves one of his dice by the number indicated
  on it; the king only moves by 1 place;



I forgot to say you can make as many turns you want (or you can);
this is the point of the game: depending of the way you'll move your die,
it will end with different numbers on it for next turns...


No no! you make only maximal one (right or left)turn per move. 
So a die, showing the 'four' has 16 fields it could reach. 
(Not off the board and over/through other dice of course)
Except for the straight forward/backward/sideward moves, you have two ways
to reach the desired place.

tactical hint:
If your King is in danger of beeing hit next round, you can either hit
the threatening die or
move the king out of danger or
place an intervening die in the line (or two lines) of striking.
