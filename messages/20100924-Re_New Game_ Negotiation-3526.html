I haven't put a huge amount of thought into this, but I don't think
Ecalper would be as complex as Oust on the same size board.  In
Ecalper, you can move and flip wherever you want, unlike Oust with its
move restrictions.  However, you're limited to 127 stone placements
and a third as many flips in Ecalper.  In Oust, because of stone
recycling, cooperating players could keep a game going for hundreds if
not thousands of moves on a 127 cell board, the game tree fucking
flaring the whole way along.  Just a theory....
