On Nov 28, 11:20 am, an...@nowhere.org wrote:


(And regardless of what Mark will say -- this IS a generalization of Hex.)


Alternative tessellations for connection games have long been
established. An alternative tessellation does not a Hex generalization
make.

There is precisely one generalization of Hex: a combination of
generalized Atoll and generalized Loophole.  Hex is the trivial,
special case of "Atoll-Loophole" where the playing surface has only
one edge, and that edge is divided into the minimum number of
segments, four.  And if it makes you happy you can play Atoll-Loophole
on an unlimited variety of tessellations, although that does nothing
to further generalize what was already a tessellation independent
game.
