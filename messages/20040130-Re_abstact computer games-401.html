Tetris could work without a computer.  Draw a piece at random from a bag,
place it on the board, following the rules of placement that the computer
version would allow.  About the only aspects I can think of that would be
difficult are (a) removing a filled-in line, and (b) enforcing the speed
limitations (e.g. when you have a move that would be legal, but can't make
it in the computer game because you can't move the piece fast enough to
avoid other obstacles).

Somewhere on the web there are some research math articles about tetris as
an abstract game.  Seems like University of Alberta had one.
