Well, the backgammon clock is used probably as an adjustment for
television to keep the game moving, and add another stress element to

You say you don't play backgammon.  It's a fast game in most cases.  The
problem with a clock is that no matter how fast a player moves, it's
theoretically possible for him throw bad dice and be blocked again and again
from coming in when he's off the board.  He will be eating up clock time in
that case, when the fault is not his own.  No such situation can arise in
chess.


They way they implement the clock in backgammon is that you get 9
seconds to roll and make a move before you eat into your clock, so it
accounts for being blocked again and again.
