In article <MJD.92Nov10124318@saul.cis.upenn.edu>, mjd@saul.cis.upenn.edu ("[*] The One True Cross") writes:


This is probably the most abstract psychology game I've ever met. 
I heard it from Ranjit Bhatnagar (ranjit@gradient.cis.upenn.edu).

The rule is:

	Players take turns naming stations from the London Underground.
	Whomever is first to name the station `Mornington Crescent', wins.


Since mjd only heard this from a friend, he seems to have not heard of the
best part of the game.

Originally it was devised as part of the BBC Radio 4 programme ``I'm Sorry
I Haven't A Clue'' which is a spoof panel game. The two teams of panellist
would take it in turns to name stations, and along the way invoke
archaic (and, naturally, completely ficticious) rules and tactics. The
audience, always excellent, would Ooh and Ahh at all the clever plays and
this all made it an excellent listen. Particularly if you were listening
with someone who didn't know the rules...

For further information, look in the groups alt.games.mornington.cresent
(unfortunate spelling mistake in the group name, there) and
alt.comedy.british. Funny? Yes. Psychologically complex? No chance :-).
