   In nim, there are piles of matches.  Players take turns removing as many
matches as they want, but only from one pile per turn.  The player whe removes
the last match wins.

******** HOW TO WIN **********

   So long as, after the end of your turn, all pile counts XOR to zero,
you will win.  e.g.,

Pile 1: 11 matiches ||||| ||||| |
Pile 2:  6 matiches ||||| |
Pile 3:  3 matiches |||

11 ^ 6 ^ 3 = 0b1011 ^ 0b110 ^ 0b11 = 0b1110

So we need to toggle the two, 4, and 8 bits of any pile.  Try pile 1.
0b1011 ^ 0b1110 = 0b101 = 5 so we want 5 matches in pile 1.  So we remove
6 matches from pile 1 for our turn and have 5/6/3.  5^6^3 = 0.

   Now notice that NO MATTER WHAT YOUR OPPONENT DOES, the piles will not
XOR to 0 because they're going to toggle some bits, somewhere, and since
the bits XOR to 0 now these toggled bits will no longer be 0.  So then you
can readjust to bring the XOR to 0 again, then they move, etc.  Since all
piles of size 0 (the winning position) also XORs to 0, you HAVE to win
eventually if you play this way.
