I would recommend that anyone interested in this group check out the
two volume set titled "Winning Ways" by Berlekamp, Conway, and Guy.
(Conway is the fellow who invented the cellular automata game Life.)

The first volume is devoted to modeling and solving various general
games, like nim and a bunch of graph based games.  It is based on a
non-standard model of numbers (see Knuth's Surreal Numbers for a light
hearted approach to these unusual numbers).

The second volume is particular games and includes Twixt and Mankala,
and also a wide variety of solitare games, including an amazing
analysis of Rubik's cube.  

All the games seem to fall in the category of abstract games and are
devoid of chance.
