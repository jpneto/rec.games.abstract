Under certain circumstances, you can beat tit-for-tat.  The 
not-quite-trivial such circumstance is a tournament with a finite 
number of unchanging competitors.  If you introduce only one new 
competitor, and it can have arbitrary complexity, it can be 
fine-tuned to use a different strategy against each separate opponent. 

Forgiving tit-for-tat is probably a variant that will sometimes be
nice (even after being betrayed) as a method of error-recovery.

It wins in noisy situations, because if tit-for-tat is playing itself
and one (mistakenly) thinks that the other defected, they'll take
turns defecting forever, while a forgiving version can get back
to cooperation.
