In designing Quadrature I first invented a "squaring" mechanism, whereby if
you form a rectangle with three of your pieces and one of your opponent's
(as the 4 corners of the orthogonal rectangle), the enemy piece converts to
one of your own.  This can initiate a chain reaction of such squarings.

This was my first game mechanism ever, and it was kind of a eureka moment,
so what did I do?  I slapped it into a game.  I modeled it after Checkers,
with opposite armies on opposite sides, charging forward and through each
other.  Why?  It was all I really knew about games at the time.  I had no
notions of finite game and such.

One day, a couple years after designing Quadrature, I experienced a draw -
an endless aggressive exchange.  Yuck!

Quadrature continues to be one of my most fun games to play, oddly.  I just
finished a very satisfying game of Quadrature on Scott Nesin's server,
GamesByEmail (http://www.gamesbyemail.com/).  So in a sense, if it works,
don't fix it.  I only experienced a draw once in hundreds of plays, but it
was such a sucky experience, I vowed to henceforth only design finite games.

Which kind of leads me back to my point.  I've considered coming up with a
finite, omnidirectional (as opposed to bidirectional, like unkinged
checkers) game using the squaring mechanism but just not Quadrature.  The
only thing stopping me is I'm too lazy.  Plus maybe it isn't even possible.
