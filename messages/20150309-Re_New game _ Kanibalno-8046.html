So far, I've only designed games that I would like to play. I don't see that changing either. To me, it's an interesting game. But I agree that most people are scared off by "4D" - so when I designed a trading game I set in space, I never told the players it, too, was a 4D board. They enjoyed it, but it could run for a couple days, so it's back to the re-design stage.

I'm decent at chess, but have a lousy memory, so I appreciated the variants.. And that's why I do higher-end abstracts - it essentially eliminates the memory issues I have. I can honestly say I have the memory of a multiple stroke victim, based on my losing roughly half the memory games I play with the one person I know who had 3 strokes within a few hours... and that explains why I don't like "simple" abstracts - there's too much of a memory issue there for me. I've rarely gotten 6 turns ahead in chess, and generally only get a few moves ahead before I lose the whole thing.

The "hard" games I design are occasionally unable to be played by computer, or played well by computer, but are aimed at humans, who do beat me. The Command and Maneuver series is a good example of this. Computers don't yet play wargames well. People do. This is why I said in the war chess thread that I have not designed a much more difficult chess game, I've designed a "different" game. Agreed, not many play it now, and not many play chess variants. Fwiw, I gained some notoriety in the world of chess variants, but that only makes me obscurely famous, known by tens worldwide. 

Given the direction my designs have gone, I jumped from the tiny ghetto of chess variants to the somewhat larger ghetto of abstracts. And I haven't gained much traction here, either. So I'm going further, and designing non-abstracts, which are beginning to gain traction. Shoot 'Em Up Spaceships did quite well at Unpub 5 in Baltimore MD a month ago, and I'm working on that game now, as it actually does have commercial potential. However, That's only my second-best design. The Battle of Macysburg is my best design so far, and I'm trying to get a little traction for it, and the game system it's a part of. If I can't, I can't. C'est la vie. But because I'm neither a well-known nor published designer is pretty irrelevant to the kinds of games I design. I will always design games that I would like to play when I've designed them. I'm not looking for novelty, per se. I'm looking for good games that people will play and enjoy. And I'll go where my designs take me. Enjoy!
