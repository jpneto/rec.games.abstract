First thing about Mixtour: I love the game set graphic!  Very nice.

Reading the Mixtour rule sheet I was on the lookout for aesthetic Hiroshimas
and surprisingly didn't find any.  But I also didn't find a lot of wow
factor, an admittedly subjective measure.  Grabber has the grab.  Kinda
cool, though I'm a little biased since the same mechanism occurred to me
independently.

I have a game with a similar mechanism to Mixtour's.  Call it annihilation
or evacuation or whatever.  In Byte, all of the checkers start out on the
board.  None are added during play.  Stacks of exactly eight checkers are
formed and removed from the board, awarding a single point to the player
with the top checker.

Byte rule sheet:
  http://www.marksteeregames.com/Byte_rules.pdf

With 24 checkers, exactly three stacks of eight will form.  With 40 checkers
(on a 10x10), exactly five stacks of eight will form, both three and five
being odd numbers.  So Byte is naturally finite-conclusive.  There's no
arbitrary cutoff at having earned X number of points with an infinite
checker supply.

Mixtour didn't take me to new heights architecturally, though it does have
that receiving stack range feature which is interesting.

Now, all that negativity out of the way, Byte has been criticized for poor
clarity, and Mixtour undoubtedly does not have that problem.  So I'll
certainly have positive things to say about Mixtour's gameplay when I do
play it.
