I'm just out of date.  Backgammon reminds me of a time in the 1770s, when gentlemen of discernment and taste played backgammon and whist. Not that I'm talking about personal memories here, of course.

And besides what you've mentioned, chess is one of the best-looking games in the world.

I'd put it behind hnefatafl in that respect.  While chess usually has the more finely sculptured pieces, one thing that attracted me to hnefatafl was the pleasing radial symmetry of the pieces, as initially set out for play on one of the bigger boards.

Some people will buy an expensive chess set just to decorate a room, even if they don't play.

I must admit, I've done that with a lot of board games.  I've had to start putting them away again as the plants and "real" ornaments take over.

When a game doesn't seem to be a metaphor or stylistic representation
of anything, it starts to seem like just a mathematical exercise to me.
That's when it starts to seem dry and boring--all work and no play.

Hence my attitude to "contrived" mechanisms in another posting.
