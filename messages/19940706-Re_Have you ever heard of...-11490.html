You're not thinking of Nomic are you? The legal moves in Nomic, at least
according to the initial rules, are (roughly) to change the rules.


There are at least two other games I know of which revolve around making 
up rules for subsequent moves: the board-game LEMMA and the word game 
WORD LEMMA. 

Lemma consisted of a board with a complex geometric design (consisting of 
imbedded squares and probably other shapes-- more complex than the 
Kennsington board) in various colors and three sets of wooden tokens, 
colored on one side and blank on the other. Each player in turn 
formulated a rule for the placement or movement of tokens on the board, 
and then placed or moved a token, obeying that rule and all previous 
rules. The last player to be able to make a move won the game.

Word Lemma is very similar, except played without a board. The rules 
dictate the formulation of rules; the last person to state a rule which 
obeys itself and all previous rules wins. This gets very difficult very 
quickly; in the one game I played of it, rule #4 dictated that all rules 
must rhyme, and rule #5 was something to the effect of:

	"That which does not kill us makes us stronger
	 So to make this game last not much longer
	 I think that it would be just peachy
	 If every rule had to quote from Nietzsche."

It went downhill fast from there.
