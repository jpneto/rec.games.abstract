Glenn C. Rhoads <gcrhoads@yahoo.com> wrote:

: I have to disagree with your opinion on reversi/othello.
: It is extremely difficult to visualize future board positions
: and hence you generally can't play tactically and must play
: almost purely strategically.  I would put the strategy % up
: around 90.

I am not an experienced othello player, but your statements seems
strange to me. Because it's hard to visualize future boards, all I can
do in othello is to try to read the sequence a couple of moves ahead,
and try to maximize the number of my own pieces. This makes my play of
othello very tactical. For me, the strategy element is just trying to
get edges and corners, and this kind of strategy is quite superficial.

If I have missed some fine strategies in othello, I'd like to hear about
them.
