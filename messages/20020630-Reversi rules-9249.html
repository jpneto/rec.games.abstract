When my father taught me how to play Reversi, I was told that when I
placed a piece, I could "revert" exactly _one_ line of my opponent's
pieces, until I reached another one of my pieces behind them. So, if
there were several lines leading away from the new piece I just played,
I would have to choose one of them to capture. Only later I discovered
the other variant (where you capture _all_ the possible lines of enemy
pieces).

Unfortunately we have misplaced the original rules insert to our Reversi
game, so I can't check there. What I would like to know is this: Does
the first rules variant I mentioned above exist at all, or is that way
to play Reversi I mentioned above just wrong?
