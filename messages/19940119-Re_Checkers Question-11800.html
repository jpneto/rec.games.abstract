ISAAC M. MANDELBERG <ISAAC@utaphy.ph.utexas.edu> wrote:

I played checkers for the first time in twenty-some years the other day.
The end-game resulted in two kings versus one king. There seem to be
situations where this is a draw. Can anyone confirm this?


Nope.  One-vs-one is a draw if the defensive player can get to the appropriate
corner, but two-vs-one is always a win (except in trivial situations where a
king gets captured immediately).

(This is assuming USA checkers.  In France, even three-vs-one is a draw under
certain conditions.)
