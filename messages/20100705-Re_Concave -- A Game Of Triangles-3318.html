Leroy Quet wrote:

...
In the first part of the game, players take turns placing n dots on
the paper. No 3 dots are to be co-linear. n is determined ahead of
time amongst the players and is a multiple of the number of players.
....


Here, the dots may go anywhere on the paper the players choose except
upon dots already drawn.


....
After a player moves, she gets a point for each concave angle along
the perimeter of the bounding polygon, the polygon containing all the
triangles drawn so far.
....


The number of points in this move is the number of concave angles in
the perimeter of the bounding polygon AT THE CONCLUSION of a player's
move (plus the extra point, if applicable). The same angles can count
again towards a players score in different moves if the angles remain
along the perimeter of the bounding polygon.
