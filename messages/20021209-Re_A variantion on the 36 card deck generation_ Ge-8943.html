Chris Byler writes:

What does a d5 look like?


A triangular prism.  If it's too long and narrow, you don't roll the
ends enough, and if it's too short and wide, you don't roll the sides
enough; but there's an aspect ratio where all 5 sides are equally
likely.


Not really.  For such non-symmetrical objects, the relative odds are
going to depend on how you throw it, how elastic is the surface it's
rolled on, etc.


Alternatively, a pentagonal prism with rounded or pointed ends so that
they can't be rolled at all.


Yes, this is a better way to make a fair d5.  Or, of course, use a d10
or d20 with repeated numbers.
