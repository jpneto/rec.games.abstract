CHARTER

  rec.games.chinese-chess (unmoderated) will be a common forum for discussion
  of all aspects of the game of Chinese chess. Chinese chess (also known as
  "xiangqi" or "co tuong") is a game similar to western chess & shogi, but
  with its own unique pieces, board, rules, and strategy. It is estimated that
  Chinese chess is played by about a billion people. Many books have been
  written on this game, but most of them are in Chinese & Vietnamese. There
  are some available in English & western European languages. A short list of 
  topics which could be covered in this group:

  --      Proper play in the opening, middle game, and end game
  --      Tournament announcements & results
  --      Software
  --      Chinese chess in the arts
  --      Club locations & creations
  --      Chinese chess sets
  --      Famous personalities
  --      Books, magazines, & related materials
  --      Rating systems
  --      Organizations


RATIONALE

  Chinese chess is played primarily in the Far East & Southeast Asia, and is
  growing in popularity in Europe & North America. Although a few of the
  pieces are similar to those in western chess, the two games are
  substantially different. At present, serious and sustained discussion 
  related to Chinese chess is severely hampered by the lack of a newsgroup 
  specifically devoted to it. Discussion appears sporadically on the 
  above-mentioned Crosspost Groups, but due to this fragmentation, many 
  enthusiasts cannot add to or benefit from these short bursts of activity. 
  A newsgroup devoted to Chinese chess will play an important part in bringing 
  together enthusiasts who can share their knowledge and improve the current 
  level of interest, understanding, and organization.
