Well, there may be some truth to that, but, I've found that 
Backgammon players are usually fairly enthusiastic. They also 
have a slight advantage as a beginner, since they are already 
used to playing the odds and other basic concepts that make up 
the backbone of Chebache.


I think chess could use some mild randomization so that one can have "rollouts" as in backgammon (or Scrabble, though
the latter are suspect because of hidden information) and objectively 

valid statements about the relative strengths of

moves, the way one can have in games of skill and chance. I think this 

would make chess more mathematical. However, Ed

Formanek (an IM and mathematician) didn't seem to like the idea.


There are several games that introduce chance into a Chess game.
One of these is Nightmare Chess. It adds cards to the game that 
allow special powers or constraints to a piece. They also create
bazaar situations. One which I remember is a card played said 
that we had to turn the board 90 degrees, but still play as you 
would normally, moving across towards your opponent. A bit of a 
twister. The use of cards and how they are played are also
strategic since you need to know when it's best to play a card
and when it's best to hold it for later in the game, or try to get 
rid of it. There are at least 2 sets of cards available...unless 
they are out of print already. I think they titled it accurately.
