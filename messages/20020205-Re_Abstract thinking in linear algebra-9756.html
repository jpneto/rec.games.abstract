I like (though I'm sure that not all of my students like that I like)
to give, as a good family of examples of bases of vectorspaces, the 
bases of the vectorspace Pn of degree-at-most-n polynomial functions
on R produced by applying Lagrange interpolation to (the Dirac deltas
at elements of) a set of n+1 points of R.  That of course is only
(what should be) high school algebra, not calculus.  But then one
can modify the construction to get bases of Pn determined by 
information about a polynomial *and some of its derivatives*
at fewer than n+1 points.  The popular-for-graphics cubic splines
then arise naturally.  Of course this is still (at most) differential
algebra, not genuine calculus.  But the students no doubt think it
is all calculus--after all, it involves *functions*.
