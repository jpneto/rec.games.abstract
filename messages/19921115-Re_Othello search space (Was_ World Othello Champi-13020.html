There's no precise way of calculating the total number of nodes tractably.
The numbers I cited were estimates from a paper by Peter Frey, and he didn't say
how he derived them.  If you look at Rosenbloom's AI Journal paper ("A World
Championship-level Othello Program") you can calculate it yourself from his
graph of average mobility.  I think it comes out to 10^50, so maybe
Frey was being generous.
