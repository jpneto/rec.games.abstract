Sygo on hexhex would be very interesting I think, on a reasonably large board (assuming hexhex is a la Havannah). The reason I like the idea in principle is I think growth feels more organic to me on a hex board, and I think the capture dynamics will be neater. In our game, captures occur almost implicitly of separation of non-large groups, so I think draw rates will be low as there will be a lot of mutual separation going on. It also realistically takes 7 stones to create life on either board (without a crazy loss of momentum from a single stone move, which is unlikely to be worth it for a single small group early on). Territories will be smaller, but the inter-group dynamics will be more fluid I think - in our game, once I'd marked out my initial interests I just expanded without much you could do about it. Hex movement allows for a greater variety of interruptions on expansions.

I don't know, I just think there's a certain clumsy albeit unstoppable factor over the orthogonal growth that wouldn't be present on a hexhex board.

I have a suspicion that this game will be a "reasonable" Go variant, but an "excellent" go/hex hybrid, prior to actually playing the latter of course :D
