(You omitted a coordinate system). I would play diagonally next to the
upper left corner, yielding:

   . o o o . x . .
   . o o x o x . .
   . x o o x o x .
   x x x o x x x .
   x x x x o x o o
   x x o o x o o .
   x . x o o x . .
   . x x x x x x . 

Black can not immediately play in the top left corner, so white's next
move will be two spaces below the top left corner. This way (most of)
the left edge and (all of) the bottom edge will be white. If black
should play on the top row, this threat may be handled in the obvious
way, and if he plays on the right edge to turn over a piece on the
diagonal, a move on the end of the diagonal takes care of this threat.
But note that giving black accss to the top left corner doesn't change
the fact that almost two full edges become white.
