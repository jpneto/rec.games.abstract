Do you mean in how to balance the sides?


Yes. I was specifically referring to the pie rule (i.e. there has to be
a fine
variation of how good a move/rearrangement is) but it applies to any
balancing mechanism relying on players to try to fine tune it by
adjusting the balancing weight until it is just right (for them).


What I saw, when I thought about using progression as a balancing
mechanism for chess, is that after 3-4 moves (white gets multiple
moves before black) it breaks down easy, and it is an exponential
worse disadvantage for the black player if you do that.


I personally would suggest
going with the adjusting of the clocks in tournament play, shaving
time off one side or the other.


Time is very finely divisible, so that works.


And this is probably the best place to balance Chess960.  One may
argue that the issue then is that neither player fully can evaluate
correctly, but is that the problem with the play mechanics?  If you
want to expose people to a tournament of games they saw for the first
time at the tournament, then something has to be done for balance.
