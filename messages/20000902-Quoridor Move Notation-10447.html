Has anyone come up with a Quoridor move notation for transcribing game moves?
 
My stab at it would go something like this.  Using an algebraic notation, similar to chess, the squares of the board are labeled a1 through i9 (that "i" is unfortunate because of the confusion with "1").
 
Moves of a "pawn" can be denoted by the square label being moved to.  For example, "white's" first move from the home row to the next row would be:
 
    1.    e2
 
Wall placement is more problematic.  One has to denote orientation AND location.  The most concise notation I could come up with, is an indication of horizontal (h) vs. vertical (v) and then use the label of the square immediately above and to the right of the "center" of the wall.
 
For example, if "black" places a wall in front of the white pawn in the second row we'd have:
 
    1.    e2    h-e3
 
It's a bit cryptic.  A more verbose notation for wall placement, would allow for explicitly naming the squares that are "blocked" by the wall.
 
    1.    e2    d2-e2 x d3-e3
 
This notation makes it very clear that moves from the d2-e2 side of the wall are blocked from the d3-e3 side.  I think I like the latter notation a bit better.
 
The last wrinkle is handling "jumps" over the opponents piece.  For brevity, one could just use the standard pawn move and list the destination square.  But I think it would be good to explicitly call out that a jump was taken.  I'd borrow from the en passant notation in chess - and add a "pass" annotation after the move.  If white jumps over the back pawn from g4 to g6 you could write:
 
    7.    g6 p.
