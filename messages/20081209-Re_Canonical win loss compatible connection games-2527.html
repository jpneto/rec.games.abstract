Bill, your claim to have a canonical game is preposterous.  In Y, you
connect the three sides of a triangle with a Y.  I'll say it again.
In Y, you connect the three sides of a triangle with a Y.  In Free Y,
by stark comparison, you connect three border cells of a hexagon with
side lengths alternating between n and n+1 (or another shape with an
odd number of border cells) such that the largest perimeter distance
between the two most distant of said cells is less than half the
perimeter length.  Free Y isn't even in the same solar system as Y!  I
dont' care how poetically you frame the partially stated rules of Free
Y or how much you torture the definition of canonical.  Your claim
that "[Free Y] may turn out to be the MOST CANONICAL of all the planar
connection games" is ridiculous.  It's absurd!  I've never seen such
profound egocentrism.  It takes a lot of balls to make a the claim you
have with full knowledge of Hex, Y, and Lariat.  Nobody wants to
massage your bloated ego. Rein it in!
