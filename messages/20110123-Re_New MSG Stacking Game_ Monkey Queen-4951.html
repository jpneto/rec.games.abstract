You do need a checker management system, but nothing that sophisticated.  I
added a line to the MQ rule sheet recently:

"For convenience, some of the checkers can be kept off the board initially,
and added to the stacks during play as needed."

I generally use stacks of about six checkers in play testing.  When they
start running a little low, I add about four more.  Just keep the yet to be
played checkers separate from the captured checkers.
