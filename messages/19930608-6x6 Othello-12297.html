The following line is an optimal line in 6x6 Othello.
The notation I use is a little strange! I have labelled the columns
b to g from left to right, and the rows 2 to 7 from top to bottom.
The standard starting position is then: black pieces at d5 and e4,
white pieces at d4 and e5. Black begins. An optimal sequence is:

d3 c5 d6 e3 f5 f4 e2 d2 c2 e6 e7 g5 c4 c3 g4 g3 f3 c7 b5 d7 b7 
b3 c6 b6 f7 f6 b4 b2 g6 g7 ps f2 g2 

White wins 20-16.

Here ps means "pass" (black has no moves, so white plays again).

2.c3 instead is a loss. I don't yet know what 2.e3 is, but it does not win
by more than 4.

There are two reasons for the strange notation. One is that it meant I had
to make less changes to my 8x8 program. The other is that strong Othello
players will recognise the beginning of this sequence as the well-known
Rose opening in 8x8.
