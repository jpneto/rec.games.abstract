The difference is difficult to make clear in words.  The playing area
is an order-6 hexagon, but the actual physical board that came with
the game extended the hexagonal grid on two opposite sides.  This area
(the "Base") was just to hold the pieces that had not yet been entered
onto the board in a convenient and attractive way.  The adjacent side
through which the pieces were entered was the "Base Entry Row".  The
rule referring to the Base was trying to make clear that that area was
just a buffer, and not part of the playing surface.   With the
original equipment and illustrations it is clear, but did not get
transcribed correctly.  Unfortunately, I have neither scanner nor
digital camera, so Joao was working from my summary of the rules
without a good diagram.
