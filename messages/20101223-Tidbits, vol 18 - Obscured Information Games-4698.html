What's neither perfect information nor hidden information?  Obscured
information, the villain of the trio.  It's neither here nor there.  In
Lasca for example, you have a stacking game in which the faces of the pieces
are obscured in the stack, so you don't know what they are, unless you
remember.

In Mancala games with large numbers of stones in the pits, the stone count
is obscured.  Decorum prohibits scooping out a pit and counting the stones,
so you just guess.  Most traditional Mancala games are primitive and
imprecise both in the estimation of stone count and in the actual
rapid-fire, error strewn placement of the stones.

Traditionally, Mancala stones are of one, uniform color and the physical
manipulation is a one handed, fluid scoop-and-distribute action.  Modern
"Mancala" games sometimes employ various stone types and colors, abandoning
the one handed, fluid scoop-and-distribute action for a two handed,
non-fluid, scoop-sort-pick-and-distribute action.

Just for reference, here's a lovely example of a modern Mancala game which
is finite, perfect information (due to a limit of an easily countable five
stones or less per pit)... and which celebrates the true spirit of Mancala -
with a one handed scoop-and-distribute of a single color of stones.
