JDerksen <jderksen@aol.com> wrote:

Can anyone help me out with some good basic stategies?  


It's been a while, but I recall the theorm that you should plan for the
game to "turn over" once.  Basically, you should plan to almost lose
during the first half.  The ideal position should be you backed up 
against one edge, your opponent without any pieces on edges, and your
opponent surrounding you on as much of your three sides as possible.


Specifically, when should you take an edge?  


I'd say (but I am not ranked or played in any tournaments) take one
edge piece, defend it if possible, try to prevent your opponent from
landing any other pieces on that edge.  I'd say take one edge and
then take no further until the middle of the game.


How important are the central diagonals?  


I've never found them to be of any importance except for the ones
diagonally adjacent to the corners.
