Steve Lopez <baayszhore@intrepid.net> writes:

Not possible. There are more possible chess positions than there are
atoms in the universe....


Where did you get this theory?  Unless you allow large numbers of
underpromotions (which would be irrelevant in an attempt to simply find
a forced win or draw from the start position), it's easy to show that
there are fewer than 10^50 chess positions.  A published estimate of the
information content of positions from real play estimates only about
10^21 positions.  By contrast, our Sun alone has about 10^57 atoms.


This topic's been done to death in rec.games.chess.computer (and
elsewhere) and numerous computer scientists (Dr. Robert Hyatt,
programmer of Cray Blitz and Crafty) have opined that chess will
*never* be solved by computers (at least not digital computers as we
now know them).


Robert Hyatt may have enough to say to be equated to "several computer
scientists", but that doesn't make his opinions worth a whole lot.
