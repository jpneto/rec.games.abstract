Not a partners game - truly multilateral.

The n-player card game Toepen (see http://www.pagat.com/last/toepen.html) uses something a bit like a doubling cube.

Each hand of this game ends by one player winning the last trick, each other player then loses one or more lives.  Initially the hand is being played for one life.  At any time, any player who is not the last player to have knocked may knock.  Each other player must then chose whether to fold, paying whatever the current stake is;  or to remain in, playing for one life higher than previously.

Ok, it's not a doubling cube, it's a +1 cube.  You could use a doubling cube in the same way.
