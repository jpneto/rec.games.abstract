  (Define your terms. How do I play checkers "randomly"?  Perhaps the
word you're looking for is "perfectly", in which case checkers is even

If just one player chooses to play randomly, then it becomes a sort of asymmetrical game - one player is rewarded by skill in pattern matching, while the other is rewarded by skill in generating true randomness which, as Chris pointed out, isn't as easy as it sounds.

  Nope. Suppose Alice plays randomly, while Bob is still trying to play
the metagame. Alice isn't rewarded by her skill in /anything/, since her
job could easily be performed by a computer or some even simpler inanimate
object (such as a six-sided die). I assert as an axiom the statement that inanimate objects have no "skills."
  Now, what about Bob? Is he rewarded by his skill in pattern matching?
No. No matter how good Bob is at recognizing patterns, there aren't any
patterns to Alice's play --- she's playing randomly. So Bob may have
mad pattern-recognition skillz, but they avail him not. Once Alice starts
playing randomly, Bob might as well throw "rock" every time --- the only
reason he doesn't is because he secretly fears that Alice may still be
playing the metagame. :) [1]

Not that any of this makes me think of it as a classy game.
