I played Soar for the first time today with my flatmate... and since
'Pommel' got some flack for sounding strange in Dutch.  I'll just say
that after mentioning the name Soar in conversation a bit it starts to
sound a bit Herpes-ish :D

I found it really (really) cool that rules can emphasize the landscape
texture of a common checkerboard (in this case referring to stack
height).  ...salting the soup so to speak.

The explosions are very entertaining.  I've never seen this before.
I liked this game more than the new racing game.

For anyone who hasn't made it to the rule sheet I'll summarize:
It's played on a 6x6 checker board.
Each player gets 12 pieces of their color.
The object is to capture your opponent's 12 pieces.
Either place a piece on an empty space or move a piece.
Capture is by replacement.
When a stack reaches a certain height a capture happens
If enough of your stones are present for the capture then there's an
explosion!
The explosion is mostly harmless.  It pretty much just makes a mess
for your opponent to have to deal with.
Movement is based on stack height, but because there's a textural
rooftop on the checkerboard the counting doesn't get out of hand at
all.  At most you'll have a height 3 to deal with.
cool game.  I look forward to trying it again.
