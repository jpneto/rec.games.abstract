Rich Hutnik wrote:

So, abstract strategy games don't involve pieces and boards?  Come on
Mark, you can't define an abstract strategy game without defining it
as a boardgame.  Why do you want to remove this?  It is fine to expand
it, but to remove it?  Let's get real here.

The point is not that they all have them, but that all other board games have them too.  We don't define humans as "mammals with hearts", even though humans are mammals with hearts.  Since all mammals have hearts the information is tautological, so it doesn't /help/ us.

It needs work to show how an ASG board and pices are different from a non-ASG board and pieces, and starting off the document with it as "key elements" is like starting a text on cheese with how to milk cows: while ultimately a crucial part of the process you don't actually hve to know how to do it to make cheese, and if you've already got a bucket of milk it just gets in the way.
