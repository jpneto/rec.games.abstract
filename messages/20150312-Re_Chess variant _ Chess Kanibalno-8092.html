How can you say that it was already done?
Even in the classical chess game you will find a rule a piece can not capture another similar (example of the king).
Does this mean that a particular rule aplly to all the pieces?
Let us take the example of checkers.
You need to jump over ONE checker to capture it.
Assume that I as game designer imagine a game where you need to jump over 2 checkers (not ONE) to capture them. Will my game be a simple variant of checkers or something else?
Is it a minor modification or a major one?
What you do not know is WHEN a new rule create a real new game?
WHEN a new rule add only complexity (or simplification) to the game?
WHEN a new rule broke the game making it boring to play?
Rules can improve the game or make it worse.
The best rules are the one`s reaching the optimal gameplay.
Not easy to produce them.


I'm going to surprise you Moh, and agree with you. It is extremely difficult to tell, beforehand, how a new rule or game is going to work out. As for optimal gameplay, that's a tough question also. What I aim for is good gameplay in an intellectually challenging game. Then I listen to the people who play it, and see if they think there is room for improvement.

I think we will always disagree on what is original and what isn't, because we look at games in different ways. I've said before you are a splitter and I am a lumper. That is a major difference that always leads to disagreements. We can just agree to disagree. I have no problems with that, and I accept that each of us will think he is right and the other is wrong. If everybody thought alike, the world would be dull and uninteresting.
