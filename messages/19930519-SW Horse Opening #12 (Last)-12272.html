I have discussed the Same Direction Cannon opening (SD Cannon; 7 posts) and 
the Sandwiched Horse opening (SW Horse; 12 posts) in some details, though
these are by no means exhaustive. There are still many sub-variations and
sub-sub-variations to the ones I mentioned. Nevertheless, I have tried to
include the major variations in these openings, as far as possible. The
openings that I have introduced are also quite recent and up-to-date. If any
of you by chance have copies of recent years' (90s-) China National Open,
you would not have much difficulties in understanding these openings as some
of these are the same as what I have discussed.

For Xiangqi beginners, my advice is: Try to play the central cannon opening
when playing Red and the SD cannon/SW Horse when playing Black. It is only
after we have familiarised ourselves with these openings shall we attempt to
play more subtle openings such as E3+5, S7+1, C26 or C24. The chained Horse
central cannon opening is the most direct, aggressive and the most
fundamental opening one must be familiar with before we shall attempt
anything else.

With that, I conclude my discussions on opening theory. Perhaps in future
postings, I shall concentrate more on the tactical aspects of Xiangqi (?)
since without sufficient knowledge of tactics and strategies, a player would
be lost once the moves are played outside the books :)
