There are three interesting questions:

  1: In chess, you may only capture en passant on the first move after the
     opponent's pawn moved.  Is this rule relaxed to allow you to wait until
     you next roll an ace?

  2: as Thomas Hayes asks: can you capture en passant a pawn that made a double
     move [rather than a two-square single move]

  3: generalizing 2:

      White's pawn is on E5.  Black rolls 11 and plays D7-D5-D4.  White rolls
      1X; perhaps we can let him move E5-D6*?

  4: If white moves a pawn two cells and puts a real piece behind the pawn can
     Black capture both?

      White has a pawn on E5.  Black rolls 12 and plays pawn D7-D5 and N-D6.
      Can white play E5-D6** and gobble up both?  Can he play E5-D6* and take
      only the knight
