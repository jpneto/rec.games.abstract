No, but they can look very deeply into the endgame.  Back in the
PDP-11 Version 6 Unix days, I wrote a Reversi program that looked
ahead through the entire (shrinking) endgame tree when there were (I
think) eight vacant squares remaining.  Modern CPUs, and more
sophisticated programming than I was doing, could probably improve
this considerably.  I would hate to play against a computer that knew
the entire game tree with twenty moves left...



In the 4th Computer Olympiad in London Jacco Gnodde and I entered
an Othello-playing program (Aida) which ended up 2nd (the winner was
a French program Jacp'Oth (if I remember correctly)). Aida runned on a
SUN SPARC-2, and can find the outcome of the game with 18-20 vacant squares
left. Sometimes it even find the outcome of the game with 29 vacant squares
left, but this rarely happens.
