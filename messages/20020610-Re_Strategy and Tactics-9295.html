I am not a good Othello player, but for the first 30 moves, probably, trying
to _minimize_ the number of your own disks is likely to be a better strategy
(yes, I do know the goal is to get the maximum number of disks in the end).
So it seems you still have quite a bit to learn about Othello _strategy_ :).

Another strategical thing that happends in Othello is when it comes to the
domination of important spots (such as corners). By having few disks, you
create "waiting moves" for yourself in other places, hopefully in the end
forcing your opponent to play near the corner, so that you can take it for
yourself.
