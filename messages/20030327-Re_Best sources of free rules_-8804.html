I disagree.  While it is true sometimes it just involves a vague description, I
find that most of the time the rules are included on the game's web page.  Most

Virtually all of those games were just made up by the author of that
script, and are not for sale anywhere as a physical set. So, there's a
lot of chaff to sift through, to find games that are really worth
pursuing. It helps to have the Zillions engine, to get a feel for the
basic tactics.


This presumes that the only worthwhile games are the ones that are being
published.  In fact, a lot of the best abstract games have never been published
or had only a limited publishing run.  Abstract strategy games that have a
continous publishing run are very much the exception and not the rule.  That
this website carries games that can not be found anywhere is precisely what
makes it so appealing.  Granted there is a mix of both good and bad games, but
that's what you'll find with any extensive compilation.
