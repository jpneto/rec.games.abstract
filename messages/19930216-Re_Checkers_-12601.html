In article <1993Feb15.171154.19765@zip.eecs.umich.edu>, positron@quip.eecs.umich.edu (Jonathan Haas) writes:


How good are computer checkers players? Has the game of checkers been
"solved"?


Checkers has not been solved.  Checkers is discussed regularly in
rec.games.chess, with the most recent discussion ocurring earlier
this month.  The best checkers playing program comes from the
University of Alberta, contact Jonathan Schaeffer at
jonathan@cs.UAlberta.CA for information on the program.  The
authors have (I believe) a database of all 7 piece end-games and
40% of the 8 piece end games (4 pieces per side).  This database,
combined with a large look-ahead, produces a program that plays
quite well.

The program played in a tournament that would have selected a
challenger for a World checkers championship match, and the
computer came in second behind the current world champion.  Avoiding
the problem of a computer world champion, the champion and the program
played an unoffical (but serious) match.  The human won but the games
were mostly draws.

The UofA team does want to solve checkers.
