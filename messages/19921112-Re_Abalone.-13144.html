Yep.  I've played Abalone.  I even tried an e-mail game or two, and here
is the notation we used.

     x x x x x
    x x x x x x
   . . x x x . .
  . . . . . . . .
 . . . . . . . . .
  . . . . . . . .
   . . o o o . .
    o o o o o o
     o o o o o

Directions are west, northwest, northeast, east, southeast, southwest
(abbreviated w nw ne e se sw)

The rows are numbered from 1 at the bottom to 9 at the top.
The space in row 5 are numbered 1-9 left to right.
A space in another odd numbered row gets the same number as the space in
row 5 it shares a vertical line with.
A space in an even row gets the same number as a space northwest would.
Space are identified by concatenating the row number and space number.

For example, 23, 55, and 97 would be:

      . . . . .
     . * . . . .
    . . . . . . .
   . . . . . . . .
  . . . . * . . . .
   . . . . . . . .
    . . . . . . .
     . . . . . .
      . . . . *

The two types of moves (push and roll) are noted by:

p <space of rearmost marble> <direction>

r <space of endpoint1> <space of endpoint2> <direction>

(where "direction" is the direction a marble moves)

Example: Black starts with "p97nw" and white moves "r3436se"

      o o o o o
     o o o o o o
    . . . . . . .
   . . . o o o . . 
  . . . . . . . . .
   . . . . x . . .
    . . x x x . .
     x x x x x x
      x x x x .
