In a more serious vein -- some people (including me) were sort of
exploring how one might run a "Speakerless Nomic".  It'd be worth
trying to hash out an initial Ruleset for such a beasty.

Two issues remain:

 * How are rules ordered?  The standard ruleset uses rule numbers to
   determine precedence, but without a speaker, it's not obvious how
   to number the rules.


Why not just number proposals in the order that they appear on the
mailing list?  The players could either refer to the proposals by
title until they became Rules, or there could be an officer who kept a
list of numbers up for vote.

A bigger issue would be where to send the votes.  They could be sent
to the proposer, but it is convenient to send all the votes to one
place.


 * Why play?  In what I'm familiar with, the "prize" for winning the
   game is becoming Speaker.  What's the prize in a speakerless
   Nomic?  Or are we back to only a single game?


Really, what kind of prize is that?  It reminds me of Socrates saying
that the best leaders are those who don't want to lead, and must be
forced to.
