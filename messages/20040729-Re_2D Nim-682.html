Clearly, the above game should not be called "donimo" but "2D-Kayles".
I searched for "Kayles" in Google, and apparently it is more than a
century old!

The question "Is there a shortcut for Grundy values of long rows?" can
be answered positively (see e.g.
http://home.att.net/~numericana/answer/games.htm#kayles): for large
row-lengths the Grundy values repeat with period 12.

The above mentioned web-page also describes Kayles-variants in which
more than 2 adjacent pieces can be taken. Therefore, it is appropriate
to refer to the various games described in this thread (elimination of
arbitrary strips or elimination of at most two adjacent pieces) as
"2D-Kayles with build-up equalisation".
