On Thu, 14 Jun 2007, Wlodzimierz Holsztynski wrote:

My new invention, the games "Justice" and "Injustice", are on their surface just a variation of Nim. But the strategies are different, they need to be studied and the winning strategy has yet to be discovered.

You start the game with any number of plates with (small :-) stones on them. A move consists of removing any positive number of stones from exactly one plate.

JUSTICE: if after your move there is the same number of stones on each of the plates then you ** WIN ** (and your opponent loses).

INJUSTICE: if after your move there is the same number of stones on each of the plates then your opponent wins, you ** LOSE **.

Justice is the game that's more similar to Nim. With an even number of plates, I think that it yields to the same analysis as Nim. This is because every losing position (equal stones on all plates) is a first-player loss in Nim.

For an odd number of plates, that's not the case. For an odd number of plates, n > 0 stones on each plate is a first-player win in Nim.
