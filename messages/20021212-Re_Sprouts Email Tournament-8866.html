As I mentioned to Nick, there are two types of UTs.  The "type 2" UT
includes a 1-liberty spot which communicates with a spot or spots that
are not part of the UT.  A move from the 1-liberty spot to one the
other two spots that make up the UT can actually be a good move, even
the only good move.  I've been calling that move "crashing" the trap.

Moving to a type 1 UT, on the other hand, is never necessary in a
winning position.  For that reason, we might call such a move "wrong"
from a practical standpoint.  But such a move does not necessarily
screw up a winning position either, so a lawyer might take exception
to your calling your statement "more accurate" even in the case of
type 1 UTs.
