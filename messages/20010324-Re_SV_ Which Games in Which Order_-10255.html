It is true that Go is a game which is difficult to get into without
the guidance of other Go players. It is also true to say that it takes
are great deal of study to become an expert at it. However, you don't
need to be an expert to enjoy it, and I don't think it's accurate to
describe Go as "really really difficult" to actually play.

I learnt to play Go from a work colleague who brought a set into the
office and we played during the lunch hour. It took ten minutes to
explain the rules with a few simple examples, and immediately I was
fascinated by the seemingly random scatterings of stones which somehow
worked together to form connected defendable territories.

By the end of the lunch hour I was starting to understand how to form
"living" groups, and the basic idea of Ko fights. Within a week of
lunches, I knew how to play, albeit at a very novice level. I then
started going along to a local Go club and I've never looked back.

Please don't be put off by people saying Go is difficult. I find it
easier than chess whilst at the same time being much deeper and
therefore more interesting. Go is a hugely rewarding game once you
have got over the initial hurdle of learning the basic ideas.
