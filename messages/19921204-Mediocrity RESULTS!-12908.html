Thankyou everyone for your active interest in my mediocrity game.
All entries have just closed. Please do not send any more.

The WINNERS ARE:

For the Mean: Joseph Beckenbach with his value of (10^(10^(10^100)))
For the Median: Aaron Sawyer with his value of 29
For the Mode: Dan Blum, John Wetmiller, Doug Chatham, Denis Howe 
                and <jens@chemical-eng.edinburgh.ac.uk> with the value 7.

Hooray!
Many thanx to the people who sent in the horrendously big numbers,
as, surprisingly, they made the "mean" winner very easy to determine.

Many thanx also to the people who sent in all the sevens. For a while there
I thought the mode was going to be spread over a LOT of values.

A hearty BOO! to Brian Kris Grant who sent in the value 1024 from 4 different
e-mail addresses. I only counted it once, Brian, and you wouldn't have won the
Mode entry anyhow.

My sincere regret to those who entered numbers such of orders of magnitude
10^9, 10^23 or 10^1134, in the hope to sway the mean. When faced by a 
googleplexplex and a googleplexplexplex, they don't sway very hard.

Congratulations to the winners. You have shown yourselves to be truly ordinary.
Your wins will be recorded for scores in the Mediocirity tournament. Of course,
in a tournament, you don't want to win the MOST games, you merely want to win
an average number, somewhere near the middle. :)

And thank goodness that's all over. 
Processing all those entries by hand was a nasty job, and not one I'm likely
to do again.

Seeya,
