I think Martin Gardner described one in one of his books. As far as I
remember it was either a hardware implementation, or based on ideas
from hardware.  I vaguely recall seeing a resistor network, but I may
confuse it with his description of the rather similar game Bridg-It.

Eliott Kofmann wrote a Ph.D. dissertation called 'Machine learning as
applied to a class of games', or something like that. From Case
Western University somewhere in the 1970's I think. I think Hex was in
the class of games he considered, but I can't recall what his results
applied to that particular game was.
