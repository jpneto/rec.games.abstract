The rules? Oh, here they are: it's played on a 7x7 grid
and if you're going to play it "in real life" then you probably
want to use othello (i.e. reversible) pieces rather than go pieces
because there's a lot of flipping. The start of the game
looks like

B.....W
.......
.......
....... .......
.......
W.....B

(let's call the players White and Black). Play alternates and when
the board is full play ends. A play consists of two parts: firstly
a player does a "move" and then some "flip"s. The move can be of
precisely one of two forms: a player can either do a "spawn",
which involves placing a new piece of his colour on a vacant
square which is adjacent (in the "kings move" sense i.e. diagonals are OK)
to a square already occupied by himself, or a "jump" which involves
picking up one of his pieces and moving it two squares away
(i.e. a place which can be reached by 2 kings moves but not by one).
That's the end of the "move". The player has then occupied a previously
unoccupied square and he then does the "flip", which involves
flipping over all the pieces of his opponent which are one move
from this previously-unoccupied square.
e.g. a full move looks like this: from

.......
.W..BB.
......

white could spawn a new piece e.g. giving

..W....
.W..BB.
......

or

.......
.W..BB.
.W....

or he could jump (and then flip), for example

.......
...WWB.
......

or
...W...
....WB.
......

The game ends if and when no-one can move and the winner is the
person with more pieces.
See http://www.gamerz.net/pbmserv/ataxx.html for more details.

The strategy that I've found to work well is a 1 level min-max
tree.  you make a move that flips the most pieces (after your opponent's
best response)  If you go 2 or more levels... you get better results,
