There's an ongoing argument in one of the comp.games NGs
comparing games on their tactical depth. What irks me
is how both sides of the argument can be considered correct,
depending on where you draw the line between what qualifies
as a tactic as opposed to a strategy. In most games, movement
etc. is discretized, does anyone have a reasonable definition
of strategy vs. tactics for games that is more concrete than
the military version, hopefully one that makes arguing what is
tactical and what is strategic a little less fuzzy?
