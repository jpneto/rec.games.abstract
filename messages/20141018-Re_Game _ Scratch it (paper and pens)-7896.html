It is a 2-players game.







A board 6*6 is filled randomly by 6 copies of 6 numbers from 1 to 6.







One number per square.







Player 1 choose one number k(1) in the board and scratch it.







Player 2 must scratch k(1) numbers under the condition that each next scratched square is adjacent (horizontaly or verticaly or diagonaly) to the scratched previous one.







Player 2 choose another non scratched number k(2) on the board and scratch it so player has to scratch under the same conditions above and so on.







The same process goes on until one player can not make a legal move.







Players can not scratch the same square twice.















The last one to make legal move wins the game.

Easy 
On your turn you have to perform 2 actions :
1. You have to scratch what your opponent has choosen as number of scratches to do. All the numbers scratched have to be adjacent (hor, vert, diag). You can choose ANY non scratched number on the board. No adjacency is required for the starting square.
2. After scratching the number of squares required. You can choose any non scratched square on the board to order your opponent to start scratching.

The only different move is at the begining of the game : the player 1 scratch one square and the play begin.
Players take their turn alternatively.
That`s it! 
The last one to make the legal move win (legal = preforming the 2 actions without being "blocked).
If you can not scratch what is required and you have no square non scratched LEFT after that YOU LOOSE THE GAME.
