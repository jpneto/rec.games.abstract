This might be an interesting game -- I can't quite tell if it is, although
2-player strategies spring readily to mind.   For large n and many 
players, I don't see any obvious ways to use skill to improve one's
score, and imagine that most players will get a score of 2 or 3 per
offense round, and the winner will be that one person in 10 or 20
who randomly happens to score 4, 5, or more.

Following are frequencies of scores from 1 to 10 when the initial list of 
1's and 0's is a random number with n bits, based on trials with 100 
million random numbers per trial.  (Repeated trials give results that vary, 
typically,  in the third or fourth significant digit.)  For example, when 
n=30, about one-half percent of the random binary numbers gave a score

Note, as the only time for strategy is when writing down the initial
string S of 1's and 0's, if effective strategies exist the game's outcome
depends on how S is written down.  There are dozens of ways that 
can be done,  depending on when new and/or previously appended bits 
are revealed and on number of bits appended at once.   Here's an 
interesting possibility:  Suppose there are p players.   Let the p-1 
non-offense players each contribute a bit; then reveal the result; 
and then the offense player gets to append p-1 bits at once.  
Repeat until S has n=2*k*(p-1) bits in it, for some small k.
