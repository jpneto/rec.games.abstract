I purchased this board game in Germany recently, and forgot to ask its
name. The girl in the store gave me a brief introduction to the rules,
but I'm hoping to find more info on the internet.

The game is carved into wood as shown below:

   B B G G Y Y R R
   BBB GGG YYY RRR
  .................
  .   .   .   .   .
  X...X...X...X...X
    .   .   .   .
    .............
      .       .
      ..X...X..
        .   .
        ..X..
          X
  ........X........
  .               .
  ........X........
         (!)

Up to 4 players can play (B=Blue, G=Green, etc.) A player begins with
5 colored balls in the divots at the top of the board. The dots (.)
are empty black divots. The (x) divots have white balls -- these are
the 'blockades'. A 6-sided die is included, with the '1' being
replaced by a large white spot.

Players take turns rolling the die and moving one of their balls the
appropriate number of spaces. They cannot move past the blockades. If
they roll a '1', they are allowed to move any blockade to (any?) other
space on the board. The first player to reach the goal (!) wins.
