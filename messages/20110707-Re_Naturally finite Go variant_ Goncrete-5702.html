The rules are those of Go, with the following exceptions:



a) When one player makes a capture, all the surrounded enemy groups
are flipped (i.e. converted to the player's color). Then, the player
must remove one stone from as many of the groups just flipped as
possible without splitting the newly formed group.



b) The game ends when both players pass in succession or when one
player loses all his stones as a result of a capture.



c) Suicide is illegal.



d) Area scoring is used.


I see only one exception here. Using 'othellonian' capture effectively
gets rid of ko situations indeed, but your new rule to ensure life
doesn't seem very natural, but rather a quick & dirty 'fix'.


Strictly speaking, there are two exceptions. The second one specifies
two possible circumstances which cause a game to end: two consecutive
passes and a capture which removes all the opponent's stones. Without
this rule, cycles would arise after a full board capture, as in the
last example. Rules 'c' and 'd' simply specify a forced choice among
the different Go rulesets available.
