Jacques Treatment wrote:


In my experience, it's not so much that the
edges are vulnerable, but that the central
portions of the edges that leave openings.
I will generally only target the following
positions on the edges: 1,3; 1,6; 3,1; 3,8;
6,1; 6,8; 8,3; 8,6.  These squares seem to
have the best offense for strikes at the
corners and allow for best defense along
the sides.


This sounds a lot like the strategy I had devoloped by
playing a weak computer program before showing up in my
first tournament back in 1990. In the first match I lost
60-4. After reading "Othello: Brief & Basic" and getting
about a year's practice in tournaments, I could beat the
same person (almost) consistently. I can now say that
there are situations where it is correct to go for those
squares, but there is a lot more to edge play than that.

Download WZebra from

http://www.nada.kth.se/~gunnar/othello.html

, let it play against itself with a proper search depth,
and you will see some high quality Othello!
