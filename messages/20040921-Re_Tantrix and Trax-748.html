I'm in the USA (Texas to be precise).

I've been playing Trax for about 10 years.   I'm strictly a mediocre player... too many other games occupy my time.   To be a good Trax player you have to actually *think*.

It is purely abstract, and a small player advantage (one player just a little better than the other) will often mean that the better player will completely dominate the other.  It's frustrating for a player just learning the game.   And sadly, anything that I've seen described as a handicapping mechanism makes it worse (the lesser player would not learn anything).

The best you can hope for is a patient coach who will play with you, and point out fumbles and allow you to take back enough moves to learn from them.

I run a play-by-email server (pbmserv@gamerz.net) that supports Trax (and over 100 other games).  In fact, Trax was the reason I created the server.
