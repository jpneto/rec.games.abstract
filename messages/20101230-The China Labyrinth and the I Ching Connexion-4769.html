I find it interesting to discuss the idea, but the Stacking thread
isn't a very good place for it, so I started this one.

Let me say on a personal note that when I discovered the one-to-one
correspondence of the hexagons of the Labyrinth and the hexagrams of
the I Ching, shortly after Martin Medema had conceived the China
Labyrinth, I felt obliged to follow through with an implementation of
the obvious implications: providing the graphical representation of
the way the hexagrams interact in any given divination. Ed van Zon
agreed and wrote the program. I manually copied the text of the Legge
translation into it, a translation I prefer above the 'Wilhelm'.
We worked very conscientiously at it and published it free for all to
use.
Neither Ed nor I use it ourselves, and I am not interested in
discussions regarding the I Ching itself, what it is, what it does and
what, if anything, it all means. But building a game around it is fine
with me.

In that respect, and now I'm back to Cameron: in Stax the first
hexagram is present, unmovable and unchanging, how fitting :) , but
there are two asymmetrical threes missing - that's twelve hexagrams.
Those would have to be included in a new game - and we're talking
about a new game here, right :)
