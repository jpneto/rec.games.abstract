Nor I do. I find the distinction between "forks" (conections between
two corners) and "bridges" (connections among three sides) rather
artificial, because it counter-intuitively excludes corner cells from
the sides. I first thought of a Havannah variant with only two winning
connections: loops and forks, forks being connections among three
sides, and corner cells belonging to sides. I like Teime more, though,
because it makes the game longer and keeps the game from being a race,
since a full board always has only one winner.
