It's a "hello world" program.  Or a "Yes, I figured out how to use
Gamemaker software" program.  Normally one wouldn't make an
announcement about something of such little significance.  Question is,
where do you go from here?  If you put a lot of time into turning Tic
Tac Toe into Tic Tac Toe Plus, you're going to end up with something
utterly non-spectacular.

You're wearing too many hats, Mitoshima.  Leave the game design to the
game designers, like me.  You just write the program.  Your graphical
interface and the AI portion you write will be your contribution.

Let me show you a program that was recently written for one of my
games, Cephalopod, using the Zillions of Games platform:

http://www.zillions-of-games.com/cgi-bin/zilligames/submissions.cgi/65955?do=show;id=1362

Something like that program, with its strong AI component, is what
you're ultimately shooting for.  But even now you need to set your
sites a little higher, even for a first time project.

You might want to take a look at Scibe, my latest game.  Nobody has
programmed it for offline play yet:

http://www.marksteeregames.com/Scribe_rules.pdf

Copolymer, another of my games, is ultra simple but the AI would be
extraordinarily difficult.   I wouldn't recommend using Copolymer for a
first time AI programming project.
