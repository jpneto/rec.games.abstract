So far as I know, Gale first wrote about Chomp in 1974.  It's isomorphic 
(as Westerink pointed out in 1975) to Schuh's Game of Divisors described
in 1952.  Gardner publicized it in 1973.  There's also a chapter on it in
Beasley's 1989 book and a paragraph in _Winning_Ways_.  I've never seen the
articles by Schuh and Westerink.


Frederik Schuh.  Spel van Delers [Game of divisors]. 
_Nieuw_Tijdschrift_voor_Wiskunde_ 39 (1952), 299-304.  

David Gale.  A curious nim-type game.  _American_Mathematical_Monthly_ 81
(1974), 876-879. 
    
G. J. Westerink.  Schuh's 'Spel van Delers' en Gale's 'Chomp'.
_Nieuw_Tijdschrift_voor_Wiskunde_ 63 (1975), 18-27. 

E. R. Berlekamp, J. H. Conway, and R. K. Guy. 
_Winning_Ways_for_your_Mathematical_Plays_.  Two volumes.  Academic Press,
London-New York, 1982. 

Martin Gardner.  Mathematical games: Sim, Chomp and Race Track.  
_Scientific_American_ 228 #1 (Jan. 1973), 108-115, #2 (Feb.), 109. 
Mathematical games: encores for Race Track, Sim, and Chomp.  Sci. Amer. 228 #5
(May 1973), 105-107.  Reprinted in Martin Gardner's 
_Knotted_Doughnuts_and_Other_Mathematical_Entertainments_. Freeman, New York,
1986. 

John D. Beasley.  _The_Mathematics_of_Games_.  Recreations in Mathematics 5.  
Clarendon Press, Oxford Univ. Press, New York, 1989.
