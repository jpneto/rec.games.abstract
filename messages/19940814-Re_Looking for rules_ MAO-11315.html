I thought Mao was the game also known as Eleusis. It goes roughly like this.


Mao is a related game, but it is NOT Eleusis.


	In my mind the difference is essentially that in Eleusis, you're trying
to outwit the dealer; in Mao, you're trying to outwit the game itself.
(More standardized.)


Rules can be something like "on the play of a five the direction of play
reverses" or "any player who plays the queen of spades must say 'Agora Agora'"
or "when a player has two cards left, e may give two cards to any player of eis
choice", etc.


	Um... are you sure you're thinking of Mao? :-)


There are many different "flavors" of Mao that I know of, each with a different
ruleset.  Some are relatively simple, some frustratingly complex, and some
silly (like one where every rule involves saying phrases from The Hitchhiker's
Guide to the Galaxy).


	However, to emphasize the difference from Eleusis -- "flavors" are
standardized, but never actually written down.  Therefore, essentially,
dealers just have an "idea" of what the real rules are... with some pretty
interesting connections to memetics.


It is taboo to talk about the rules.  This is a social game, where the fun
generally lies in seeing newbies stumble through the game and amass a huge pile
of cards.


	Hmmm... I remember a flavor called Eastern Mutant Semi-Expert, under
which I got called for "Failure to call an illegal penalty", and later for
"Failure to call a misleading penalty." :-)
