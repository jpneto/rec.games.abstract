In article <3itred$7bl@uwm.edu>,
Bruno Wolff III <bruno@cerberus.csd.uwm.edu> wrote:


This game appears to have a pretty easy first player win. You just keep
attacking the other player's piece. If the other player stops defending
to attack you you will surround his piece first. If he keeps trying to defend,
his piece can be trapped along an edge in short order.


No: the board's periodic: there is no edge.  This strategy actually
loses: you build two walls, the opponent builds one then moves.  If
the first player then stands still, they're dead: they're now a wall
behind.

Example: O moves first, builds wall above  X, X builds one below O, O
builds right of X, X moves left.  Board position is

. . . . .
       O
. . . ._.

. . . . .

._. . . .
  |    X
. . . . .

and O loses if he stands still.  I suspect that the first player does
have an advantage, however.
