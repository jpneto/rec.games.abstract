All kidding aside, I really do find Prodymple sickening.  I'm tired of recombined old game ideas which is all we're seeing now, and I'm fucking tired of Symple.  Luis' last two games were copy pasted.  First he ran off with my Tripen, adapting it from a Hex board to a square board.  The fact that he had to be pressured into grudgingly acknowledging the similarity (outside the rule sheet) doesn't bother me as much as just the fact that it's recycled.  

Then Luis became the third person (after Bill Taylor and Nestor the filthy plagiarist) to run off with Nick Bentley's Produto, without crediting Nick of course.  Now, as if three Produto rip-offs weren't enough, Christian Freeling has leapt into the fray with Prodymple.  At least Christian had the decency to credit Nick.

I am so tired of Symple.  There's nothing about Symple itself that's irksome.  Not even the fact that it's based on a complicated, inelegant, kludgy mechanism designed to balance an utterly opaque game.  It's the fact that Christian relentlessly touted Symple as "perfect", "quintessential", and "great".

Now Christian has moved into Phase II - blending the Symple mechanism with other existing games.  This isn't ordinary torture.  It's "please let me die" torture.

Finally we have Prodymple.  The over-copied Produto combined with the over-promoted Symple mechanism produces a nausea synergy where you can't stop dry heaving.
