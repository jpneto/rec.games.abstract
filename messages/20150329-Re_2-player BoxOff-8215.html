It's been suggested to me that 2-player BoxOff can be deepened by first allowing the players to place stones anywhere on the board, on alternate turns, until the board is full.  This adds an entire strategic phase to the game.

There are two ways to do this:

1) On each turn a player draws a random stone from the bag, and then places it anywhere.

2) All the stones are visible to begin with, and each players chooses which stone to play on their turn.

Note that the second method brings 2-player BoxOff into the realm of perfect-information 2-player games of pure skill.
