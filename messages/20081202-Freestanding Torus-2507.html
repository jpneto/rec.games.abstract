Freestanding Torus . . . and Hex and Y and Lariat, etc., etc., etc.

With all this discussion of when and under what circumstances a bridge
can (or must) be placed between diagonally adjacent cells on a
rectangular tessellation grid, how about whenever and wherever you
want?  Maybe I missed it somewhere in these recent discussions but I
don't recall any mention of freestanding bridges - i.e. there's an
unoccupied cell at each end of the bridge.

Why these awkward, inelegant restrictions on bridge placement?  Better
yet, why these awkward, inelegant bridges?  Torus, etc. can obviously
be played on a tessallation that consists of a square grid of
octagonal cells with square (diamond) cells filling the gaps - all
cells of both types being playable, one cell per turn, without any
restrictions whatsoever.
