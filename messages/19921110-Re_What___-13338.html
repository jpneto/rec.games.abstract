I believe 4-in-a-row on a 4x4x4 board has been shown to be a
first-player win.  It is very difficult, however.  

    A winning strategy for the first player in 4x4x4 4-in-a-row converts
immediately to a winning strategy for 4x4x4x4 4-in-a-row, of course;
choose one of the 3-dimensional subspaces of the hypercube, and play
3-dimensional tic-tac-toe there.  If your opponent plays in the same
space, use your winning strategy.  If he plays elsewhere, it's like
having free moves.
