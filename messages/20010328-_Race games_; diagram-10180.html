I doubt that there are "race game". Abstract games (which i know best)
have usually one final goal, sometimes two final goals which are equally
important (Example: Ninuki Renju --> capturing 5 x 2 = 10 stones OR
5-in-a-row).  Backgammon (including diceless backgammon, diceless
ckeckgammon) is "normally" considered a "race game". But what is a
race? In any finite abstract game players try to reach a goal as fast as
possible. Otherwise you would waste moves and this isn't good in any game!
You have mating races in Shogi, connection races in Twixt, killing races
(semeais) in Go, etc. BTW, "racing" is not the final goal in
these games nor it is in Backgammon. The goal in Backgammon is to bear your
pieces off the board (and speed just helps you to be the first player to
achieve this goal). It appears that Backgammon is logically
related to Phutball or Alapo. But Checkgammon or Nardy bez Kostey (checkless
Narde) should, nevertheless, form a special subcategory:
