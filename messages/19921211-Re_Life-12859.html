kassover@rumsey.crd.ge.com (David Kassover) writes:

People have theorized that it would be possible to make a
*computer* that operated on Life, with streams of gliders
instead of streams of data. It could be done. This is important,
because the rules of Life could actually be the rules of
growth of some kind of bacteria or something. And if so, an
actual *living* computer could be constructed.


Actually, I think it was Conway himself who put forth this idea.


Conway suggested that a computer (Turing machine) could be
constructed in the Life world, and someone (I don't remember
who) later proved that he was right.  But the number of cells
required could be very, very large.

But I've never heard anyone suggest that some kind of actual,
living bacteria might reproduce according to the rules of
Conway's Life.  Bacteria just don't behave that way.
