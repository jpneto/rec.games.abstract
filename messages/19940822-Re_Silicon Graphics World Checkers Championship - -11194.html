    Your analogy is unsound.
    In a human/human contest such as the chess world championships, it
is understood and accepted that the stamina inevitably plays a role,
and fatigue affects both contestants.  But remember this thread is about
a human/machine contest, in which one side, and only one side, is subject
to fatigue.  The machine side therefore has an advantage which could
confound the interpretation of the results, unless appropriate steps
are taken.
    Is anyone interested in the outcome of a stamina contest between
human/machine?  Jonathan Schaeffer said the point was to discover whether
it was possible to build an entity that plays better than all humans; not
one that could wear out all humans.
