2 comments:

1) Have you considered making the score the number of moves required
to achieve the goal pattern?  In this case it would still be 6, but

would encourage risk taking to defer a win until a later move.

1) Hmmm. Interesting variation. There is already risk-taking, though,
during some games with my variation, because a player may hold out for
a better position later. (My example round shows player 1 taking such
a risk, and losing.)

2) I am pretty sure every round leads to someone winning. Remember,
you change however many squares' states you need to to ensure a unique
pattern. And all patterns must be unique. So, eventually, since the
number of moves per round is finite, a pattern will be achieved where
one player wins. 2n patterns, for n number of squares per row, are end-
moves, out of 2^n possible patterns total.
