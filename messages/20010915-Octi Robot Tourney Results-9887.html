Congratulations to Charles Chiou, who programmed the winning Octi robot.  The
robot plays nicely, with a balance of aggressiveness and patience.  Searching
only 6 ply per 3-minute turns, the bot is vulnerable to long-term strategies and
is easily tricked into chasing after sacrificed pieces.  Nevertheless, this
robot plays with flair and is competitive with an advanced beginner player. 

Congrats also to Aaron Armading, who developed the winning Octi-for-Kids robot.
The field was thick with good OFK robots, but this one was a cut above the
competition due to its strong algorithm and efficient searching.  A java version
of this bot (slower than the C++ version but nonetheless quite respectable at 7
or 8 ply) is available for play over the net at
http://www.abstractboardgames.com/aarona/javakids/ .

I look forward to next year's robot tournament at Yale University next summer.
For more info about Octi or tournaments (human or robot) see www.octi.net or the
Yahoo! Octi club.
