It give's me a completely ticklish feeling to be announcing this year's
University of Waterloo Computer Science Club's annual Computer Othello
Competition.

The play ground is green and 8x8.
The goal... domination by a single colour.
This is not the innocent fun of a child's game.
This is a machine versus machine fight.


For those of you who haven't seen this before, picture thousands of
gates working in unison hammering out a finely tuned algorithm paired
with pain-stakingly thought out evaluation tables.  

When:	November 26, 1994 (9:00am EST - 9:00pm EST [at worst])
Where:	UW, Math and Computer building, 3rd floor
What:	several rounds, swiss, 2 losses out, plus some "verification" rounds.
        30 min / player / round
Cost:	$4 CSC members, $6 others ($2 for CSC membership)

We will be attempting to use the Internet Othello Server located at the
University of Paderborn in Denmark. Client code should be available.
Registration forms available via e-mail, or in the CSC.


For more information on the tournament or the new othello client
routines please contact Mark Tompsett:
