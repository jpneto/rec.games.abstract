It may have been discussed before, but I wanted to reinforce that the
games should stand on their own merit, regardless of whether or not a
computer implementation happens to exist.  For example, one risk is
that an AI with limited search depth may have the effect of making the
game appear more tactical than it actually is, since deep strategies
will be overlooked.  Therefore, I would like to suggest that all games
be subject to some amount of human vs. human play.  Also, determining
the "fun factor" of a game, may best involve human play.  I may not
need to mention this (perhaps I'm stating the obvious), but I think
it's important for the judges to keep this in mind in order to support
their unbiased evaluations.
