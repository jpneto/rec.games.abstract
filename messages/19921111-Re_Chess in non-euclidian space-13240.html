howesb@monashee.sfu.ca (Charles Howes) writes:
] A sphere is when the left and right sides touch, and you can go from any
] square on the top edge to any other square on the top edge; same for the
] bottom edge.

hmmm.  That would be a sphere, but you have now turned the "squares"
in the top row into triangle-like things that meet at a point (call
this point the north pole).  How do you decide what happens when a
piece (say a bishop) goes over the pole?

I think that this spherical board (or ones isomorphic to it) is the
only one in which these properties hold:
  1. each square shares one edge each with four other squares.
  2. moving one square "left" then one square "down" takes you the
     same place as moving one square "down" then one square "left"
     (and so on for other paths-that-should-go-the-same-place).
