"Geoffrey" == Geoffrey P Wyatt <geoffw@teleport.com> writes:

Geoffrey> It is interesting that a 67 year old man could be World
Geoffrey> Champion of checkers.  Not meaning to start any sort of
Geoffrey> checkers vs .... debate but it IS interesting that it would
Geoffrey> be next to inconceivable for a 67 year old to be world
Geoffrey> champion of chess, for instance (I don't know about go --
Geoffrey> doesn anyone else?).  Anyway, for those out there who know
Geoffrey> more about checkers than I, why is there this difference?

i don't see what's all that inconceivable about it.  the average 67
year old still has full use of his or her mental faculties, and has
the added advantage of having had more time to learn and practice
than, say, a 45 year old, by virtue of being older.  as long as the 67
year old is not senile or so infirm as to be unable to play, i see no
reason why such a person cannot reasonably aspire to the chess,
checkers, or go championships.
