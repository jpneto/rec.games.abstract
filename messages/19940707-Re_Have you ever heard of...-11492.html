In article snd@panix2.panix.com, kjm@panix.com (Kevin Maroney) writes:

There are at least two other games I know of which revolve around making 
up rules for subsequent moves: the board-game LEMMA and the word game 
WORD LEMMA. 

Lemma consisted of a board with a complex geometric design (consisting of 
imbedded squares and probably other shapes-- more complex than the 
Kennsington board) in various colors and three sets of wooden tokens, 
colored on one side and blank on the other. Each player in turn 
formulated a rule for the placement or movement of tokens on the board, 
and then placed or moved a token, obeying that rule and all previous 
rules. The last player to be able to make a move won the game.
