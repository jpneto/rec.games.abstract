As for notation, is there some sort of (International) convention
on this?  I certainly don't want to reinvent the wheel and I also
do not pretend to have a better solution, but I thought about this
for a while yesterday (before I saw Melvin Nicholson's article) and
came up with this scheme.  Rows are labeled A through I, say starting
on White's side (the bottom).  In each row the spaces are numbered
starting from 1, giving A1-A5, B1-B6, C1-C7, ..., E1-E9, ..., I1-I5
as valid labels.  Example:

       . . . . .
      . * . . . .
     . . . . . . .
    . . . . . . . .
   . . . . * . . . .
    . . . . . . . .
     . . . . . . .
      . . . . . .
       . . . . *

The marked points would be H2, E5, and A5.  What do you think of
this scheme?  (he said, slipping into his asbestos suit).
