Jim Beers <beers@nr-tech.cit.cornell.edu> writes:


My first idea was just random trys and pick the best, then I thought
perhaps some simulated annealing on top of that, then I figured, how
hard would it be to try every possible combination of moves?


I think, it is not an effective way of selecting the perfect path. In my case
(I know the game too and play it sometimes in a BBS) the size of the field
is 80x24 fields. Supposed, you do not have a Cray, it would be a too big piece
of work for the computer.

Friends are following another strategy: They divide the whole field in some
smaller squares, solving each of them one after another, only defining the
connection between the fields. This method is surely NOT the best, but it
runs a bit faster.
