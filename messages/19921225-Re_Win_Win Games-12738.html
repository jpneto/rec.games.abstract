mdm@bnr.ca (Diane MacMartin) writes:


I've played a couple of games of this type.
---------------------
Crossed/uncrossed - people are sitting in (more or less) a circle or
ring, some people with their legs crossed.  (this can be either 
Indian-style on the floor, or on chairs with their legs crossed)



A pair of scissors is passed from one person to the next, with the
passing person saying "crossed" or "uncrossed".  Those who are "in" on
it say "wrong" when the person is wrong.



The key is, you say "crossed" if the scissors are in the closed
position, "uncrossed" if you pass them in the open position, i.e.:



0 0                    0  0              
V  crossed             \/    uncrossed  
V                     /  \              
V                                       



Use the legs to distract people.  (this may also be used with
eyeglasses, or anything of that type)
---------------------


No, no, no, it's the other way around. It's too simple to hand the scissors
around and let them be the rule for when to say crossed/uncrossed. Instead,
hand the scissors around and let whether the person handing the scissors have
his/her legs crossed be the rule. It's frigtheningly easy to change position
when everybody have their attention on the scissors. Took me half an hour to
guess the first time.
