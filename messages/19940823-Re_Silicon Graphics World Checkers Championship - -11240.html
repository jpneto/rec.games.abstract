'Legitimately'? I remember an earlier note that the checkers
organization (or organizations?) refused to allow a real title match,
and that this was some kind of pseudo-title.  Was that wrong?

If it isn't, there can be no discussion about legitimacy. The title
won would only be the pseudo-title, invented for this match.

Even if I do remember wrong, and the real title was at stake, there is
such a thing as sportmanship in accepting awards as well. You may
decline to compete for a title, but if you do compete, and win, even
by default or a resignation, you don't refuse to accept the
consequences out of some misdirected sense of personal pride.

Sportsmanship cuts both ways.
