: In practice, how can i use the pagoda function to cut the search space?

If f is any pagoda function, and

f(current position) < f(final position)

then you cannot reach the final position from the current position and 
you don't have to search any further.

It is helpful to have several pagoda functions to test.  Different
pagoda functions are useful in different situations.  

A very simple pagoda function, for example, would be to simply count the
number of pegs that whose coordinates are (even, even).  Of course, you
can substitute any combination of odd or even, to get 4 different pagoda
functions.  At the very least these should be implemented.
