Actually, the game can be made more elegant (and more interseting?) by
changing the rules slightly: instead of accepting or rejecting the
start, the other player can accept the start or add squares to create
an updated starting position.

So the rules can be written:

PolyoNimo: Starting from an empty configuration, both players in turn
add sqares to build a polyomino, until one of the players - instead of
further building - decides to remove a straight polyomino (a Nx1
rectangle of squares). Both players subsequently have to take away a
straight polyomino from the configuration. The player who made the
last move wins.
