mathwft@math.canterbury.ac.nz (Bill Taylor) writes:

Pity it isn't possible to play by email!


Actually it is -- if the players agree to use appropriate
authentication software (e.g. GnuPG; see <http://www.gnupg.org/>),
then they can digitally sign their e-mail messages, which would allow
them to exchange bids atomically.  Transmitting the signature of your
bid does not reveal any useful information about the bid itself, and
once you have received your opponent's bid's signature, it is safe to
send your own bid, because you will be able to detect if their bid is
altered after being signed because the signature will no longer match.
(Players should also include the date, turn number, or other abitrary
information in the message being signed, to avoid reusing identical
bid/signature pairs.)

An alternative would be to exchange bids through a mutually trusted
third party, but I believe this scheme is easier to arrange.
