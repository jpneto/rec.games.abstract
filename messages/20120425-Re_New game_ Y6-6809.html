Certainly the center in Y6 is important,  but that is also true of
Hex, and even more so in regular Y.  I don't see a center-oriented
game as problematic in itself.  Most checkmate games, most connection
games and most elimination games are center-oriented, and there are
many in these classes which I enjoy.  However enjoyment is tied to
personal taste, and for some people a center-oriented game may well be
distasteful.  But to me it is only a problem if the gap in importance
between the center and edge is "excessive," there being no exact
definition for that.

Most territorial games, however, are more edge-and-corner oriented,
e.g. Go, Amazons (I think anyway, but I'd have to ask an expert player
to be sure of that), and my own game Anchor.  (This is not to say that
the center in these games are unimportant, only that they are less
important than the edges and especially the corners.)
