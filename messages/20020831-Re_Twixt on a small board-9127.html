Turns out I had a little misconception about what moves
were most suitable as defending vs. attacking moves, and
that after 1. D2 one should perhaps first look at the 4/0
move 2. D6 and the 4/1 move 2. E6. I think white still
wins after 2. D6 because of 3. D7, whereas 2. E6 is indeed
a win for black (if for instance 3. C5, then 4. B5). 

I think Carl Johan is right that 1. B4 is a win for white
(if 2. C6, then 3. E6), but I also think 1. B3 is still
a win for black (2. C5; if 3. E5, then 4. F4). If we've
got it right now, then the set of winning first moves has
a nice elliptic form, exactly as expected:
