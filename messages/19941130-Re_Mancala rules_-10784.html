Is anyone out there familiar with an African game called Mancala (I'm told it's 
also known by many other names, but this is the one I remember).  


"Kalaha" is a very common name - at least in Europe.


The board 
consists of 14 "pits" laid out as follows:



          O O O O O O
        O             O
          O O O O O O



The starting position puts pebbles in some (or maybe all; this is why I'm 
looking for the rules) of the pits.  


7 pebbles in each of the 6 pits on either player's side.


A move consists of picking up all the 
pebbles from one pit and redistributing them by putting one in each of the 
next 
several pits in the clockwise direction until all the pebbles are so 
distributed.


This is correct. You may choose to start from any of your own pits, as long as
it contains at least one pebble. Should you happen to put the last pebble in
your own "kalaha" (Which is the middle pit to your right), you get an extra
turn. 

An optional rule is that if you end up in one of your own, EMPTY pits, you may
put all pebbles from this pit and the one opposite of it in your own kalaha.

The aim is to get the most pebbles in your own kalaha. Once no pebbles remain,
except in the Kalahas, the game ends.

Hope this is clear enough.
