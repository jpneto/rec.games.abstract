"GAMES is a highly interactive, captivating and unpredictable
magazine. Full of crosswords and pencil puzzles...cryptograms
and logic tests ... contests and trivia tests ... GAMES is sure
to challenge and delight every reader.  You'll find four-color
glossy puzzles such as Eyeball Benders, pictorial quizzes, time
sequences, visual logic, and the latest craze: Stereograms.

Each issue features articles on the latest in video and
electronic games, reviews of new games and books, and reports of
national and international gaming events & tournaments.  There
are pages of games and puzzles in a variety of styles at every
skill level.

GAMES is the magazine for creative minds at play."

The feature article in the February 1994 issue of Games Magazine, "The 
Girl Who Would Be Queen" by Christopher Chabris takes an indepth look at 
Judit Polgar the "Young Queen of the Chess World." Other articles in this 
issue include Stephanie Spadaccini's "Generation Gap Quiz," the annual 
"Game & Puzzle Events Calendar" compiled by Mark Danna,  two 
contests: one with nursery rhymes, the other involving rebuses, and 
enough puzzles to keep you busy until next month!
