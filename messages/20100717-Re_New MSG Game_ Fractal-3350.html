The design goal in Fractal was a more level playing field, a more
equitable game.  Fractal presents a finely scaled array of values for
first moves.  Player 1 can select a move much closer to zero in value
than he can in Hex, severely weakening Player 2's pie rule advantage.


   I've worked up a variation on the original Fractal board, It can be
found here:

After playing a few times I noticed that there are 2 hexagons which
rarely get used.  In my last game I noticed that they seem to be worth
less than a normal move, giving the board different terrains.  I tried
evening it out with this board:

I think Fractal is an interesting experiment in connection games.  I
imagine it could even start a genre of Fractal hex games.  The board
above from Cubist also looks somehow fun to play.  Both of these games
have me thinking about this quite a bit anyway.

In fact it might be interesting to purposely make stronger and weaker
points and somehow exploit that in the rules or general mechanics.
