Considering games of the "Winning Ways" style, does anyone know of,
or can anyone please construct, an example of this type:


-----------------------
That there are three symmetric games, A, B, C, all of type "1st-player wins",
(i.e. games whose values are "confused with zero", i.e. "hot-position" games),
and with the property that -

1) in game  A + B, the only winning strategies require starting in subgame A,

2) in game  A + C, the only winning strategies require starting in subgame C,

3) in game  C + B, the only winning strategies require starting in subgame B.
-----------------------


Informally, A is more urgent to play in than B, but B more than C, & C than A.

It would be good even to have non-symmetric subgames A, B, C, though that
is a nastier case in that there are more than three subcases to consider, 
as it also requires attention as to who moves first.

But a symmetric solution, as mentioned above, would be super-cool.

I can't see any theoretical reason why it couldn't occur, but all my
attempts to construct a case come tantalisingly close, but just fail.

Help please!
