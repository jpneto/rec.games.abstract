Quoting marksteere@gmail.com's message of Yesterday:

Checkers, Chess, and Go are all magnificent achievements for their
simplicity and fundamental uniqueness.  Checkers especially has
probably been more imitated than any other game.  How many games
include the Checkers jump?  The jump is a wonderful mechanism.  It's
simple, unique, and easy to perform. But every time I see the jump
appear in a new game I want to barf.  Designers: Just leave the jump in
Checkers and come up with your own mechanisms.  It's challenging but
not impossible.

I think rather differently where mechanisms are concerned.

Some mechanisms in new games appear contrived to me.  I can't think of an example here and now, but at least one of Sid Sackson's games struck me this way.

In contrast I find some of the older mechanisms beautiful in their simplicity, and some games still find elegant ways to use them.  I like movement to adjacent squares, or by sliding in a given direction; I like capture by jump, replacement or surrounding.

I don't dislike strange new mechanisms altogether.  Cannon is a new game that appeals to me, though it uses non-traditional mechanisms like the cannon firing, and the movement of a whole column that I've seen in another modern game (Epaminondas?), in addition to more traditional forms of movement and capture.
