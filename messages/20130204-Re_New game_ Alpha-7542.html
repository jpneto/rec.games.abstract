What if refusing the swap gives white the win and accepting it gives black the win, in case of a draw?


The rule is that White wins ties, and a swap can't change that. If the second player has swapped, he'll have played as black and lost. If he hasn't swapped, he'll have played as white and won.

The situation you describe could only happen if the rule were wrongly worded like this: "in case of a tie, the second player wins". This would certainly give the second player an unfair advantage, since ties would always favor him no matter if he swaps or not.
