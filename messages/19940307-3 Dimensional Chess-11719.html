For those who have expressed an interest in 3-D chess, I have received
some more information from Mr. Bernard Kennedy, the inventor of a 3D
chess game being marketed in Ireland at the moment.  I enclose a copy

While I have no business relationship with Mr. Kennedy, or his

email enquiries to him.

The game was launched relatively recently (last October, I believe),
and has been selling well here in Dublin.  Mind Games are currently
work on overseas contracts also.  A 3D chess club has also started up
recently in Dublin, and meets every two weeks.  So, the game seems to
have become quite popular.


3-Dimensional Chess is a game that has been tried several times in the
past and from several different countries and backgrounds.  The
problem with developing a successful version of the game is to create
a game that isn't too difficult or too easy to play.

Remember, to be faithful to chess, it must be challenging, but when
you introduce vertical motion into pieces, you immediately open up the
number of possible moves from tens of thousands in chess to millions
in a three dimensional equivalent.

In Mind Games' version, we establish two classes of piece: the upper
class that can either move in three dimensions, or like ordinary chess
pieces, and the lower class that cannot move up or down.

The playing surface consists of three 8x8 boards, one above another.
Each board has eight black and white pawns and pieces, giving each
player 24 pieces and 24 pawns.

This means that the rules of the game can be explained thoroughly to a
chess player within three minutes (with practice), while it takes
three or four games to become competent at the game.  The challenge of
3-Dimensional chess, after your fourth game isn't remembering how
pieces move, but it is in determining what would be the best strategy
to win.
