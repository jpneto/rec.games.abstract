Sprouts:  Uses pen and paper, or equivalent.  Initially some number of dots
are drawn on the paper.  Play alternates, where each player draws a line
connecting any two dots, and draws another dot somewhere on the line.
Subject to the restrictions that:  (1) No more than 3 lines meet at any
"point", and (2) No lines may cross.  First player unable to play loses.

A 2-point game would proceed

1.  Initial:
		*           *
2.
		*------*----*
3.
		*------*----*
		 \    /
		  ---*
4.
		      (d)
	    (x) *------*----* (a)
		 \    /      \
		  ---*--------* (b)
		    (c)

Move 5 either ends the game if (a)-(b) are joined by a line "inside"
the trapezoid (a)-(b)-(c)-(d).  If (a)-(b) are joined by a line "outside" 
the trapezoid then another move can be made joining (x) and the new point.  
Ain't ASCII great?
	
I think this was another SciAm game from days gone by.  It gets pretty hairy
after about 7 points.
