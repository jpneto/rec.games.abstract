Well, it has as much "purpose" as any other theoretical research does, which
a lot of people might argue is none at all. After all, it is theory. Some
people are interested (believe it or not) in knowing which games are unsolvable
by computers, and which aren't. Or upper bounds on the minimum # of moves it
would take to kill and opponent in chess given your pieces remaining, etc.
I'm particularly interested in how games can be classified and represented by
computers. It's interesting to know that some games, (maybe only under given
conditions) is equivalent to another game, etc.

I suppose it's hard to answer that question... theory doesn't really apply
itself so it's hard to tie a purpose to it. It's just fun to talk about, I
guess :)

Let's put it this way: I'd like a group called alt.games.theory or

alt.game.research.

You have two choices: have these discussions in existing forums (like one
for designers of games - and yes, perhaps players of games as well), or
start your own forum.  The process of making a newsgroup is not that easy,
and nobody else is going to do that work for you.  Why don't you go ahead
and start your discussions now (I still recommend rec.games.design). Then
once you've gotten a passionate enough group, then go ahead and start up the
new forum.

That's exactly what I'm going to try to do. I don't expect anyone to do this
for me, but I'm looking for my audience first.
