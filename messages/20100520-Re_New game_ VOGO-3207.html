On May 20, 8:37 am, luigi <luis.9.8...@gmail.com> wrote:


One possible problem with Vogo is that small boards might not allow
the creation of surrounded territories with sound play.


Or even large boards with overly defensive play.  An abstract game
should never depend on "sound play" to function.  The two most
defensive players in the world should be forced to fight it out,
however reluctantly.


Anyway, my main worry about this game (and its variant) is the fact
that it could end up being trivial. I guess it usually happens with
most simple game ideas. Any thoughts?


It's an interesting concept and yes, most interesting concepts end up
not working out as a game.

My advice?  Don't try to patch up Vogo.  A good idea that doesn't work
isn't really a good idea.  It's impossible to salvage it anyway
without totally complicating it, missing the point in a different
direction.  Rather than wasting time on an idea that you already know
doesn't work, just start over with a clean slate.  Infinity minus Vogo
is still infinity.
