I own several Life programs for the IBM PC, including one called CA Lab
(put out by Autodesk), and a freeware version I received with a book
called "Fractal Vision" by Dick Oliver.  CA Lab is very flexible, letting
you tweak almost every parameter of both one- and two-dimensional Life-like
simulations.  The freeware version is one of the fastest I've ever seen.
It takes advantage of VGA, and it can handle a HUGE grid!  It came with


The fastest one I've seen runs on the Amiga, doing 25 generations a second
on a 640x512 screen! It's really impressive to watch...
