In article <PLYON.93Jun10204254@emx.cc.utexas.edu> plyon@emx.cc.utexas.edu
(Paul Lyon) writes:


For zero sum games with 3 or more players, one may also need a further
restriction, namely one prohibiting coalition formation.


That's a very difficult thing to do. Explicit coalition formation can be
banned, as in player A saying to player B "Let's gang up on C".

But that still doesn't stop tacit coalitions occurring - e.g. player C is
getting near to winning the overall game, so both the other players decide
they'd better concentrate their attention on player C and put off any
disputes between themselves until the immediate danger has passed.

And tacit coalitions can complicate analysis just as much as explicit
ones...
