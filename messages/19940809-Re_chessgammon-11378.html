 >   I have some questions too. :)  Does the game end when the opponent's
 >king has actually been captured and removed from the board, or is some
 >version of "checkmate" recognized as a win?  I was thinking that it would
 >be the first of these options, but then reading more in the rules made me
 >unsure.  The first case adds an element of randomness to the game's end
 >(which is fine) and also has the effect of insuring that there can be no
 >drawn games.

The game ends when a King has been captured.  The chess concepts of
check, checkmate, and stalemate do not apply.

Drawn games are possible, but are usually by agreement between the
two players.  Certain sparse positions with no passed pawns and no
immediate threats could take a long time to play out, so the players
may agree to abandon the game instead.  Players are always free to
negotiate a settlement, just as in backgammon.
