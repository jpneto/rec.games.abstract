one of the quirks of the internet is the vast amount of duplicate
information around the globe, including archives of all the usenet
groups.  the usenet postings are timestamped.  a post here in this
group is thus archived on many computers around the world with a
timestamp local to the machine - all within minutes of each other.  to
be able to forge the timestamp, you would have to find all those
computers and make a clandestine hack into them.  i'm not sure this
can be done.  in a certain way, publishing on the internet is a very
iron-clad method of documenting the creation of intellectual
property.  however, the legal beagles havent (to my knowledge anyway)
given this line of thought any attention.
