The Encyclopedia of Chess Variants says: "Blockers can neither capture nor be
captured; they move up to three squares in any direction except sideways and
always over unoccupied squares, and can change direction(s) during a move."

The problem I have with the game is that the goal seems difficult to achieve --
elimination of all opposing attackers.  Combined with the fact that there is no
reason for a player not to leave his attackers on the edge of the board, I
imagine that wins will be hard to force.  But let us know what you find.
