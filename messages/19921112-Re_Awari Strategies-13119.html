Very different indeed.  Obviously there must be a lot of different
versions of this game.  As someone else mentioned; they are all
mancala (or mangala) type of games.  "My rules" are these:

(I'm using the word kalaha for all cups, is that correct?)

The kalaha on the far sides of the board are used only to collect
"prisoners"; your's is to your right.  You "deal" counter clockwise.
When your last bean lands into one of your opponent's kalaha and as
a result the number of beans becomes two or three, you capture all
the beans.  If the kalaha immediately to the right (if there is one,
of course) also contains two or three beans, you get to take them
as well, etc.  There are no bonus moves and there's no obligation
to capture (if possible).  Example:

   4 2 1 3 1 0                     4 0 0 4 2 1
 4             2        ->       4             7
   2 3 2 3 4 5                     2 3 2 3 4 0 ^
             ^                               ^
