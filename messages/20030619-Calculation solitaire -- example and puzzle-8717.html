What is the chance you wil win from this position?
---------------------------------------------------

Less than 100% surely, as you have no chance if the next
four cards are K,K,T,T in that order.
Another sequence that hurts is 5,5,7 but this is not necessarily
deadly -- you win if the following cards are 4,9 (in either order).

In can be shown (I think) that you have a definite win over 5/9
of the time: as long as a K appears before a T (that is any
of six permutations except TTKK, although K at *very*
end is no trouble) and a 7 appears before a 5
(that is any of three permutations except 557).
Even then you win if (94 or 49) appear early enough,
or combinations like (55J94).

 ===========  Rules of Calculation Solitaire ============

In the recent millenium in <CFuKA4.Bu1@cantua.canterbury.ac.nz>
Bill Taylor (wft@math.canterbury.ac.nz) described a solitaire game:
# CALCULATION
# ~~~~~~~~~~
# A shuffled pack is face-down before you,
# and cards turned over one by one.
#
# Each card, on being turned over, must be played onto either:
! one of H "holding piles", or:  one of four "goal piles".
!    We'll call this game G(H).  Usually G(4) is played.
!    The top card of a holding pile can also be moved to an
!    appropriate goalpile at any time. [jda]
# The four goalpiles are built up regardless of suit, but in different ways.
# One must begin with an Ace, one with a 2, one with a 3, and one with a 4.
# Subsequent cards may be put on, if the number is exactly "n" above the last
# card placed, for pile with base "n";  all done modulo 13;J=11,Q=12, K=13.
#
That is to say the four goalpiles are built up, without regard to
suit, as:
 A,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9,T,J,Q,K.
 2,4,6,8,T,Q,A,3,5,7,9,J,K.
 3,6,9,Q,2,5,8,J,A,4,7,T,K.
 4,8,Q,3,7,J,2,6,T,A,5,9,K.
The game is won if you play all 52 cards to the goalpiles.
