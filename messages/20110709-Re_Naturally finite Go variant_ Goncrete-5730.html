Yes, and nothing prevents a Go player from filling in his own eyes,

Go rules always have required mutual understanding and agreement, and

This is a case of hard finitude versus soft finitude, to borrow two or
your terms. Of course, Go is already soft-finite, so opening the door
to cycles, no matter how unlikely they may be, would have defeated the
very purpose of my game.

But it's more than that. In Go, sensible play never leads to filling
your own eyes, but, in Goncrete, sensible play (I mean, without
cooperation between the players) can well lead to a full board
capture, especially in 9x9 games between differently skilled players,
where the weaker player may be unable to create a single alive group.
