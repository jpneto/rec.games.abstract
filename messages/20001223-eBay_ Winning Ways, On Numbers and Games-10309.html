I have copies of two of the classic works on mathematics as applied
to games (not game theory, which is a different subject): Winning
Ways For Your Mathematical Plays (by John H. Conway, Elwyn R. Berklekamp,
and Richard K. Guy) and On Numbers and Games (by Conway).

These books (particularly ONaG) can be fairly heavy going for the 
non-mathematically-inclined, although they don't presuppose any 
particularly specialized mathematical knowledge.  Just so you know.
