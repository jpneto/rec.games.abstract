In article <tgee.721365643@cunews> tgee@alfred.carleton.ca (Travis Gee) writes:


4-D tic-tac-toe has been invented, but
never played. It is a *truly* abstract game (-:


Ahem... I beg to differ. I've printed up some sheets of paper with a
4x4x4x4 grid on them and played some quite enjoyable games of 4-D tic-tac-toe.
(As someone has already pointed out, 3x3x3 and 3x3x3x3 tic-tac-toe are
sure wins for the first player... 4x4x4 and 4x4x4x4 are not, though)
(Actually, there's a simple proof that *any* variation of tic-tac-toe is
either a sure draw or a sure win for the first player)
