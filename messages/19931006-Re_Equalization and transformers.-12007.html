In article <28uji6$so7@csugrad.cs.vt.edu> dbush@csugrad.cs.vt.edu (David Bush) writes:



The trouble with the 1-2-2-2-2-...  multimove transformer, is that



(i) it leads to a quite different sort of game, for the most part;

(though fun!);

Good god, yes! In chess for example, a whole new set of rules would need
to be tacked on. Would capturing the king be legal? Must you escape check
in the first move? Must you deliver check only in the last move? etc...



I would say that these are reasonable restrictions, analogous to
Backgammon and Acey-Deucy, which are multiple move per turn
games.

In both, if one has a man on the bar, one must enter that man
before making any other move.  If one has a choice between making
either of two rolled moves, one must make the larger one.

In Acey-Deucy, in addition, if one rolls Ace-Deuce and cannot use
both of them, one doesn't get to take one's double-anything.
