I just wanted to clarify a misinterpretation of a previous posting
of mine regarding the computation of checker endgame databases.

The total number of positions (both legal and illegal) in checkers
is 5 x 10**20.  This can be broken down as follows:

We have currently computed over 250 billion of these positions.
Specifically, all positions with 7 or fewer pieces on the board
are done as are all even 8 piece positions.  We are currenty
working on the 5 vs 3 piece positions in the hope that we can
solve the 5 vs 4 (and eventually 5 vs 5) piece positions.

I really wish 250 billion was half of 500 billion billion, but
that extra billion really makes my life miserable :-).  It would
be interesting to know, however, how many of these positions are
impossible to reach in a game of checkers.
