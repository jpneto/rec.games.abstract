As for checkers being solved in the next few years, I doubt it.  The
game is likely to be taken beyond the human scope (Chinook II may be
able to beat even Tinsley, who is far and away the best human
checkerer), but I doubt that will result is a first-player-win algorithm
any time soon (maybe within 10 years as data storage gets better, but
not before then).


The game is big but Chinook is making big strides in sizing it up.  Chinook
uses a huge endgame database and on fast enough hardware searches an everage
depth or 21 or so.  This enables the program to announce the outcome of some
games after only about 15 moves of play or so.  Right now, I believe Schaeffer
and crew are working on increasing the size of the endgame database which will
aloow for earlier "outcome determinations".  The claim simply is that event-
ually they'll push it all the way back to move 1.  True that's quite a leap,
but I think its possible.
