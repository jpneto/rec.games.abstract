I was one of the people who analyzed this game last spring.

This summer I heard a super generalization of this type of game.
In a Richman game (named after the late mathematician), players
make secret bids for the right to move. They must bid at least zero
and up to their entire holdings. In case of a tie, flip a coin.
The winner pays the loser the amount of his bid. (In the poorman game
variant, the winner pays the bank.) 

Here is a game we analyzed at the summer program I taught at Hampshire
College. Josh is in a south american country. Each turn the highest bidder
can move Josh to an adjacent country. I win if Josh reaches Argentina. You win
if he reaches Panama. Find the optimal play from all south american
countries.

By the way, in the original tug of war game I assumed that a bid of zero WAS
allowed. In some situations, there was a certain probability that such a bid
should be made.
