 > How do we know that any problem is hard, except in that way?

Not all hard problems are equal.  Some hard problems can be solved.
Experience can give us insight into which problems might be solvable,
and which one's are beyond our reach.  For example, experience teaches
us that monotonic problems are generally much easier than non-monotonic
problems.

There are many ways of measuring the difficultly or intractability of
a given problem, and human effort is *not* one of the better metrics.
If you're having trouble with this concept, see a mathematician, or a
theoretical computer scientist, or Vanna White; perhaps you can buy a clue.
