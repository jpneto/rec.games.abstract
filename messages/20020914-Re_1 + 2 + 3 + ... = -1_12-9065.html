The usual way this supposed identity arises:
Define the Riemann zeta function zeta(s) = 1/1^s + 1/2^s + 1/3^s + ...
= sum_{i=1}^{infinity} 1/i^s.

This sum converges only for Re(s) > 1 but zeta can be extended in a 
unique way to a function that is analytic over the entire complex plane, 
with a pole at s=1, zeros at negative even integers, and some more zeros 
elsewhere -- you could win a million dollars if you prove that 
"elsewhere" means only on the line Re(s) = 1/2.

Anyway, zeta(-1) = -1/12.
