I have not played it yet, though it's interesting enough for me to
want to try it.  Connection games are among my favorites.  Hard for me
to know if the rule about when you can place three is sufficient to
avoid the tendency for the first large group to win.  And I don't
especially like the rule about comparing second largest, third
largest, etc. to avoid ties, because it seems arbitrary to me.  I
don't think anyone would intentionally play in such a way as to build
a large second-best group, and in fact it might even be bad strategy,
so winning that way seems to me completely serendipitous.  Better to
call it a draw if the largest groups tie, or give the win to the last
player to have the largest group.
