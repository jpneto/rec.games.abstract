I won't repeat the rules, for several other people on this group have  
already answered this question, but let me just say that life is a really  
FACINATING little operation that deserves to be played with for hours and  
marks an interesting field for computers.  Eventually cellular automata  
like 'life' led to the study of artificial life on computers.  
I wrote a Life program with Turbo Pascal for the IBM and toyed with it for  
hours.  It's quite fascinating.
Oh, and thanks, John Haas, for introducing me to the 'spaceship'.  I'd  
known about the gliders but the spaceship was a new one to me.  I'll have  
to go home and tinker with that one for a while.....
