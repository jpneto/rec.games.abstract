Bad news. Ayu is not theoretically finite. The igGameCenter player known as PCM has constructed a cycle:

o . . . . . . x
.. x o . . . . .
.. x . . . . . .
.. x . . . . . .
.. . . . . . o .
.. . . . . . o .
.. . . . . x o .
o . . . . . . x

1. b5b8, g4g1;
2. f2f3, c7c6;
3. f3f4, c6c5;
4. b8b5, g1g4;
5. f4f3, c5c6;
6. f3f2, c6c7.

This requires cooperation between the players, but there's no guarantee that all possible cycles are that way. On the other hand, PCM thinks "it would be very hard to do a working [cyclic] setup with as many pieces on the board as you guys used", i.e. with the official starting set-up.

I'm not going to ban repetitions of positions. If one ever arises in actual play, it will be declared a draw.
