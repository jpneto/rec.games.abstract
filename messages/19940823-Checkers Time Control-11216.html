For the original Tinsley match we agreed to 24 moves in 60 minutes.
Don Lafferty asked for 30 moves in 1 hour and that is the rate at
which we are playing.  This is the normal time control used for human
tournaments.  It may seem like a rapid rate of play, but remember in
checkers all captures are forced.

Normal checker tournaments have either 4, 6, or sometimes 8(!) games
played in one day.
