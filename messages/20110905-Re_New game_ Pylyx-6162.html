Luigi: "Constructing a position where both players have equal winning
chances regardless of which side moves first must be almost impossible"


Just for the record, and because I have the impression that I am not
being understood:

Constructing a position where both players have (practically) equal
winning chances _with a certain side moving first_ is possible.
Examples are the pie rule, the 'extended' pie rule I mentioned above,
and the contract phase in Mind Ninja. For the Pylyx's mechanism to be
an improvement on these, it should be possible to construct a balanced
position _regardless_ of who moves first. But that's impossible. For
example, if a certain position is balanced with Black to move, the
same position with White to move _must_ be advantageous to White,
strategically at least. Therefore, the added step in the Pylyx's
mechanism is superfluous. I know I'm omitting some of the underlying
reasoning, but I hope that's enough.
