So, without being exhaustive, it looks like 1.C3 loses, and so does
1.B3, although not the way you thought. 1.D3, 1.B4, and 1.C4 all look
like winners.

For larger boards, I believe the "elliptical shape" for winning first
moves is a myth. Consider it from a positional standpoint.
A first move in the corner can be helpful both defensively (blocking
the opponent) and offensively (forcing a way through to your border row).
Suppose some initial move in the corner is a win for the first player.
As you shift this initial move horizontally, closer to the middle of the
first player's border row, you tend to lose effectiveness, because any peg
that could link to this first move probably would have been able to reach
your border row anyway. I suspect the locus of winning first moves for
24x24 grids or larger is more like four hyperbolas rather than an ellipse.
