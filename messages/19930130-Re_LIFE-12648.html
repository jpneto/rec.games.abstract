I wrote a coloured LIFE-program. It works the following:
Two (or more) groups of cells are put randomly in a 100x100 board,
where left and right side and upper and lower side are connected.
Each group has (in the beginning) its own colour.

Then LIFE is running normally, regardless of the colours, but
every "newborn" cell gets a colour depending on the three
neighboring cells (its "parents"):

 - if all parent cells have the same colour, the child gets it, too

 - if e.g. one parent cell is blue and another is red, the child is white

 - if one or two cells are e.g. red and the other(s) is/are white,
   the child will be red
