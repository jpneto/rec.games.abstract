	I also have a question about the new game Quadrature.  I talked 
to the creator and he was selling it as being a "pure strategy" game.
No initial move advantage and no draw games.


Assuming (considering the newsgroup) that Quadrature is an abstract
game---no chance, no hidden information---then this claim seems bogus.

If the game has no draw, then with perfect play it must either last
forever (a "draw" as far as most of us are concerned) or be a forced
win for the first or second player in finite time.

Perhaps he meant that the first player's position is lost?  That
would be "no initial move advantage" :-)

Posting the rules might clear up the confusion.
