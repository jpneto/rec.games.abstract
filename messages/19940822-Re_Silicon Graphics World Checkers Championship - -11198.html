In article <335noe$52c@mozo.cc.purdue.edu> indole@mace.cc.purdue.edu (Boy Cosmonaut) writes:

Since Tinsley resigned the match due to ill health, and since the Chinook
team have stated that they did not want this unearned title, why do they
not resign it, and _then_ challenge Lafferty, the new World Champion, for 
the World Championship title?  This would keep Si Graphics and the Computer
Museum happy as well as being a gesture of respect toward Tinsley.  (Not that
I am suggesting that the Chinook team has been in any way disrespectful.)


There is one problem with this logic.  There are two World Championships,
the human Championship (currently vacant, the person who was awarded it
after Tinsley giving up the title two years back died recently), and the
Man-Machine World Champion, which is specifically created for this event
by the Checkers Association.  Chinook has a better claim to the latter
title, as it was the challenger in the aborted match, not Lafferty.

Lafferty holds no current world title.
