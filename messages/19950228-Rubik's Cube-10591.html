(M = Twist the slice between R and L in a way that the centre square that
     was in the U layer ends up in the F layer.
 E = Twist the slice between U and D in a way that the centre square that
     was in the F layer ends up in the R layer.
 S = Twist the slice between F and B in a way that the centre square that
     was in the U layer ents up in the R layer.
 A formula between bracets and with a number behind it means : Apply the
 formula n times where n is the number behind the formula)

A nice pattern:
1. E- M E M-
2. (B- U2 B R- D2 R)2
3. (R E)4 U- (R E)4 U
4. (L E-)4 D (L E-)4 D-
5. L2 (R S-)4 F2 (R S-)4 F2 L2
And now you must have a pattern that the author (Anneke Treep) calls
'Knot of Wool'
