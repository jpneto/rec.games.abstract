The game GAIA (Connection Games, page 110; invented by Mark Thompson 2002)
is very nearly Free-Y on an oddly-even board. His board has 18 cells in the
perimeter.  He tries to get around the problem of an even perimeter by
having a slightly different object:

A player wins by either

1) Completing a chain of his pieces across the board between two opposed
cells, or
2) forming a Y that renders any Type 1 connection by the opponent
impossible.

A randomly filled board will NOT always have exactly one discernable winner
and the game may end with a race.
