Joe, not to take sides, but why do think of Kanibalno as a Checkers variant?

"Token are moved like chess rook
No jump is allowed"

To me, Checkers variants are identified primarily by the jump mechanism.  If anything, Kanibalno would be more of a Chess variant, using the classic slide and capture-by-replacement mechanism.

For example, I consider Mad Bishops to be a Chess variant.
http://www.marksteeregames.com/Mad_Bishops_rules.pdf

Also, I don't see Rock-Paper-Scissors here.  

"1 can capture 2 or 3 but never 1
2 can capture 1 or 3 but never 2
3 can capture 2 or 1 but never 3"

If it were "2 captures 1, 3 captures 2, 1 captures 3", that would be RPS.  As it is, there's no circular hierarchy.  Here a numeral can only capture an unequal numeral.
