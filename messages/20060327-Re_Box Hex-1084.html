Thanks for providing a free game, but...

Are the users supposed to peer and reach into the box, in order to
play?

This is essentially a two-dimensional game. It is equivalent to the
image at http://img156.imageshack.us/img156/6972/smallgrid4tw.png
Let the blue lines outline the cells which may be played in here.

As an experienced Hex player, I don't see that you have eliminated the
need for the pie rule at all. The advantage of the first move is a
TEMPO asymmetry, not a board asymmetry. So, your new version
makes play around the four corner cells more important. Suppose
the first player has the solid circle border rows on your grid. As the
first player, I am sure that playing in the cell marked with a purple
dot (or probably almost any central cell) would lead to an easy win.

There have been plenty of alternate maps suggested for Hex.
http://www.geocities.com/twixtplayer/pex1.html
http://img218.imageshack.us/img218/5791/square6df.png

The pie rule is recommended for all these variants.

|Box Hex is a Hex variant which plays exactly like Hex, except on a
|three
|dimensional surface.  There are four shortest paths of connection: two
|for each player.  This solves the strong first move advantage problem
|of
|regular Hex, where you have only one shortest path, said path being
|shared by both players.  (No need for the pie rule. )
|
|Box Hex shares the topographic properties of regular Hex: one
|connection
|must exist in a filled board, and opposing connections cannot exist
|simultaneously.
|
