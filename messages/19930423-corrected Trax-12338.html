    We call this type of threat a short L. It is related to the L I think I
    showed in the rules.
 6. Capping off kills the short L and give me some useful corners.
 7. Take one corner back and neutralise the one other while preventing
    some of the revenge possibilities(P.S. The "L" Threat as you call it
    is usually a component of most good LoopTrax tricks)
 8. The attack I just defended we call a 4-stage edge due to it being a 
    single attack (at the bottem) plus a standard 3-stage edge on the 
    right. You'll have to be more subtle.
 9. The attack was a side effect of my defense, I didn't expect it to
    catch you any more than this one, which is just my way of setting
    up for a later attack while I kill your line.  (Yes, I noticed it)
 10. Defend
 11. Kill the line
 12. Set up back-to-back L's (Knight's Threats)
 13. Kill the two L's (Knight and long knight)
