I think this might be conflating clarity with depth.  I think clarity
refers to the ease with which a player can determine which moves are
legal now and for several plies forward.  Depth is the degree to which
different levels of skill determine the winner.  If the moves are easy
to see for 12 plies, but the position takes great experience or skill
to evaluate, then the game has great clarity and depth.  I think
clarity is always desirable and that opacity is often used to create
the illusion of depth.  T-T-T has clarity but no depth.  Tai Shogi has
depth but terrible clarity.  Those would be at or near the extremes.
Games with excellent clarity and depth include Hex, Go, Breakthrough,
Epaminondas, Amazons, Oust, and doubtless others.
