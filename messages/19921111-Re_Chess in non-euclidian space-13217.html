royd@ERE.UMontreal.CA (Roy Daniel) writes:
] Take, for example, a game of chess where the right and left sides, and the up
]  and sides touch each other. It would be like playing chess on a sphere.

nope.  That would be like playing chess on a torus (a doughnut if you're
not up on your topology).  Same thing with screen-wrapping video games
such as Asteroids.

If you really want to play on a sphere, you could use a board like this: 

        a d c b b c d a
        g . . . . . . j
        f . . . . . . i
        e . . . . . . h
        e . . . . . . h
        f . . . . . . i
        g . . . . . . j
        a m l k k l m a

Where squares with the same letter touch (think of folding the corners up
so that they meet at the center).  This is actually much better than your
torus as far a chess is concerned, as the opposing kings don't start out
adjacent to each other, but the opening rook game is still a bit strange.
