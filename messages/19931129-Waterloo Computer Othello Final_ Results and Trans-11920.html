Subject: Waterloo Computer Othello Final: Results and Transcripts
From: brock@cs.ualberta.ca (Mark Brockington)
Date: 29/11/1993, 18:53
Newsgroups: rec.games.abstract,rec.games.misc

The final of the 1993 Waterloo CSC Computer Othello Tournament took place over 
the last week (November 23-27).  The top five programs from the Swiss system 
tournament held on November 13th, along with Vers2 (due to a glitch in the 
Swiss system pairings) were the invited participants.  All games took place on
the Internet Othello Server (telnet faust.uni-paderborn.de 5000), which has
proved to be a very suitable environment for running Othello tournaments.

The tournament results were extremely similar to the Paderborn tournament 
(which was held in October).  However, the margin of victory was a lot smaller
at this tournament: nearly every match was split between the two programs.

The winner was the same program that dominated the field at Paderborn:  Michael
Buro's "Logistello".  Mark Brockington's "Keyano" finished second, while Igor 
Durdanovic's program "Igor" finished third.  The top three programs were all
running on SPARC 10 workstations.  The other three programs are IBM PC based
programs:  "Brutus" and "Vers2" were running on 486DX2/66 processors, while
"Stell" was running on a 486DX/33 (it was the best PC that I could find on
short notice for the program).

For a more complete report, and notes from the various authors, an article
will be published in Othello Quarterly (the USOA's quarterly magazine).
