Pente rules:
1) Stones are played on empty intersections
2) First stone is always played on center intersection
3) Player wins when they have 5 (or more) stones in a row (straight or
  diagonal line) OR when they have captured 5 pairs of their opponents
  stones.
4) A pair of stones is captured when a player surrounds two-and-only-two
  of his opponents' stones. 


It is also legal to capture several pairs in a single move.

It is legal to move into a captured position, even if doing so creates
a pair surrounded by enemy stones.

Since the introduction of the game in 1973, and the purcase of the
rights of the game by Parker Brothers Games (tm) in 1984, a new rule
has been added. It's called the tournament rule. It aims to reduce
the advantage of moving first:

	"The first players's second move must be at least three spaces
from the center point. A square can be drawn between the four inner
"stars" on the board.  The first player's second move must be on or
outside (not inside) the perimeter of this square.  No other
restrictions are imposed on either player."
			from "Pente Strategy" by Tom Braunlich
			Warner Books, New York, 1984
