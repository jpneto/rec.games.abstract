I think that most people in this group agrees that an abstract game should
be without an element of chance. I personally think that the discussion fits
better in rec.games.board.

The game board may look nice, but to me, as an abstract games enthusiast,
this game is nothing special. I'd rather play a game of Backgammon than
teach someone these complicated rules, if someone insisted to play a game
with chance in.

Also, I am unimpressed by your hyped up topic, which makes the post looks
more like the regular spams that go to the groups than a regular post.

The following old RFD for the group rec.games.abstract summarizes this
nicely. It can be found at
http://groups.google.com/groups?q=rec.games.abstract+news.groups+hex&hl=sv&l
r=&ie=UTF-8&scoring=d&selm=BtMLqM.4qy%40uunet.uu.net&rnum=4 but I'll include
a quote:

"The newsgroup would be for discussion of abstract games where strategy,
tactics and player skill are paramount.  This includes the major category of
perfect information, pure-strategy (deterministic) games, i.e. those with no
element of chance (e.g. no dice), and where all information is available
(unlike card games and Scrabble, for example, where players' hands are
hidden).  The games under consideration are characterised by a simple board
and pieces, with a small number of rules.  The cliche `a minute to learn, a
lifetime to master' is often applied.  A list of example games is given at
the end of this RFD."

I think that althought the above uses the word 'includes', it is quite clear
that the group was intended for games of no chance during gameplay.

Hello Everyone,

Chebache is a new abstract strategy board game which is now
available as an Online version with a FREE download at:
                     www.chebache.com

Chebache unites elements of Checkers, Backgammon and Chess and many
unique modes of play. It was designed to fill the gap between
Backgammon and Chess in regards to the balance of strategy and chance.
There are more options of movement and strategy than Backgammon, while
still including the chance elements of dice.
(Please read the excerpts from some of the reviews and comments about
Chebache at the end of this message.)

The Online version of Chebache, which is up and running as a trial
version is a FREE download, which will have occasional updates.
The latest version is 2.3.

*Note: Chebache Online will only run on PC (Windows) computers, for
now. Sorry to all Mac users, we hope to have a Mac version ready
sometime soon.

There are several tutorial aspects
included, which are explained in the "Help" section of the MENU, along
with the rules, notation and other features. Once online, please check
the message board for updates.

For the FREE download and general information about Chebache,
including reviews from On and Offline magazines and comments from
Chebache players, please take a moment to visit:

                      www.chebache.com

* The links for the download and the Chebache Egroups forum are in the
left margin of the home page, although we may move the link to a more
visible location soon.


We are hosting Chebache Online Gatherings Monday and Tuesday nights
from 7-10pm EST, however, there is clearly a need to find another time
that would be more convenient for those of you across the ocean. If
you have a feeling about a given time that would work well for you,
please let us know and we will do our best to accommodate. You may
play online at anytime, as long as you can find someone there to play.
So, for the time being, you will find it easier if you arrange to meet
someone in the "game room" at particular time. One way to do this
would be to join Chebache egroups on yahoo.com where you can leave
messages about when you are available to play.

We would greatly appreciate your help in fine tuning Chebache Online.
We believe that we have successfully fixed all the bugs, but, have
not tested the server with a large number of people playing at once.
If you should have any problems or would simply like to comment or
ask a question about Chebache,  please don't hesitate to write us.
