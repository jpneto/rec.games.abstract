A friend of mine told me this paradox a couple of years ago. Forgive me if it
is well-known or trivial.

"HyperGame" is a simple game: you pick a finite game (finite in the sense that
the game always in a finite amount of time) and play it. Problem: is HyperGame
finite? Assume it is. Then we can play the game, decide to play HyperGame, pick
HyperGame, and keep playing until the end of eternity--so it can't be finite.
On the other hand, if we say it is infinite, and thus not a valid choice when
playing HyperGame, we would play HyperGame, pick some finite game (like, say,
football) and always finish in a finite amount of time. So it's not infinite
either!

I would deeply appreciate someone enlightening me on this subject.


See  William S. Zwicker's article:  Playing games with games: the hypergame
paradox.   Amer. Math. Monthly 94 (1987), 507-514.  

This article is reviewed by van Rootselaar in Math. Rev. 89e:90221.
