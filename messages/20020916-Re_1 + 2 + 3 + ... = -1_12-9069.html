Carrying on this silliness long past its use-by date, I respond to...

Douglas Zare <zare@math.columbia.edu> who writes:

|> 1) 1+2+3+5+8+13+...=?
|> It looks like one should be able to make sense of a sum of terms based on 
|> a linear recurrence relation, 

...by noting it is not too difficult:

 F = 1 + 2 + 3 + 5 + 8 + 13 + 21 + 34 + 55 +...

   =   3   +   8   +  21        55    +     ...        grouping pairs one way,

   = 1 +   5   +  13   +   34    +   89    +...     by grouping them the other.

2F = 1 + 3 + 5 + 8 + 13 +...                 by adding the two grouped series

   = F - 2    


So  F = - 2  .

This certainly agrees with each grouped series separately!   

Just add 2 to the front end of either of them, and see the microscoping 
cancellation whoosh everything away to zero!

So maybe there's something in it?

OTOH, summing the series Abelwise and setting x = 1 can certainly be easily done,
as f_n = [t^n - (1/t)^n]/sqrt(5),  where t = golden ratio.

And unfortunately this gives a different answer from -2.   Bugger.   So maybe
there's nothing in it after all...  unless someone slicker than me can rescue it!
(I can't help feeling that sqrt(5) in some suitable set of modular systems
should do the trick...)
