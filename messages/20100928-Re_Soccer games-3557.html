On Sep 28, 2:00 pm, Jackspritz <schackow...@live.com> wrote:


In general I think abstracts should not be designed around narratives,
whether they be sporting events or galactic wars. Abstract games
should ultimately be designed around mathematical and spatial
relationships.


Right, and if you were to misguidedly design an abstract game around a
sporting event, soccer would probably not be the best choice.  As we
see in real world soccer, the ball can be kicked back and forth
indefinitely without any goals occurring. There's nothing to drive
play forward.  The God-give-me-cancer class of games squarely includes
soccer games and all Checkers variants (except Cage).
