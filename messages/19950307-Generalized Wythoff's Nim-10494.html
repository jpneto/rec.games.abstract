I would like references to articles (or FTP sites for programs) on the
following Wythoff-type games:
(1) N piles; a move is to remove k stones from 1 to M piles (M=<N is fixed).
   In other words an equal but arbitrary number of stones is removed from 1
   to M piles. More generally we could take any subset A of {1,2,...,N} to
   be the set of numbers of piles we can remove stones from simultaneously.
   If Wythoff's game models a Queen on a two-dimensional board, this would
   model all generalizations to N dimensions.
(2) How about X queens on the same board? This is not a simple sum game, as 
   the Queens will interfere with each other. I am thinking here of a move
   shifting one Q only, but of course there are further versions.
I suspect Fibonacci quarterly has some results on this. But which of the many 
Wythoff Nim papers published there deal specifically with (1) or (2)?
