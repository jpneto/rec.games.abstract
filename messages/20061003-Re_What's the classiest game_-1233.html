Quoting Bruno Wolff III's message of Yesterday:

Expert players of rock-paper- scissors (yes, there are such) will detect your patterns and beat you fairly consistently.

If BOTH players want to try to engage in a meta game of who can outguess the other, then they can aggree to do so. But either one can opt out by choosing randomly.

If Chris's statement above is true, then it requires both players to play randomly before it becomes a game of true luck, and in that case it differs little from chess or draughts, if treated with similar contempt by the players.

If just one player chooses to play randomly, then it becomes a sort of asymmetrical game - one player is rewarded by skill in pattern matching, while the other is rewarded by skill in generating true randomness which, as Chris pointed out, isn't as easy as it sounds.

Not that any of this makes me think of it as a classy game.
