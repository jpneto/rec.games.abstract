Also, I think my own Paper and Pencil Go (http://senseis.xmp.net/?
PaperAndPencilGo) would fit in your list, even if its balance between
life and death is not completely satisfactory. A simultaneous capture
rule, as I mention in the Sensei's Library article, would help correct
it, as groups with a single two-points eye aren't still
unconditionally alive. With this rule in effect, marked stones
shouldn't secure life when touched by both players' stones, as it
would be the case in ko-like positions. (I must definitely edit the
Sensei's Library article to reflect this. Or maybe call it Paper and
Pencil Go 2.)
