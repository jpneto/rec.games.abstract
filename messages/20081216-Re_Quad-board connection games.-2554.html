(not that anyone worries about it but a certain individual who
laughably heads up all his game presentations with "please don't

Well, there's no denying the Atol idea is a good one, and the
graphics on that Dutch site are great.  But like everything else,
it can be extended.  Joao Neto and myself are already playing
variants "Hexatol" and "Octatol" (sounds like a medicinal petrol!),
which have respectively 12 and 16 border colour-zones, compared
to Atol's 8.  Same basic idea, and all with local-hex.

The win-criteria in these two are simple but intriguing!
In Hexatol the winner is whoever connects 2 opposite zones
OR three alternating-Y zones, pretty common idea by now;
and in Octatol it is whoever joins two opposite groups
OR makes a Y that *prevents* the opponent from doing this.
This pro-&-con criterion, (or at least wording), surprisingly
keeps the game win-loss complementary, and I am quite pleased
with this minor discovery!  :)

Locally-quad versions (whatever protocol) are also possible,
obviously, and Joao and I already play QuadrAtol as well.
(This time the prefix refrs to the local rather than global aspect!)
