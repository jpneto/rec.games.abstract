 With all the talk about Hex lately, I thought I'd post a
 hex puzzle that occured in a recent email game of mine:

                    A B C D E F G H I J K

                   - - - - - - H H H - -   1
                  - - - - - - - v v H v   2
                 - - v H H H H v H H v   3
                - - - v v v v - v H v   4
               - - H - v H - - - - v   5
              - - - - - v - - H - -   6
             - - - - - - - - - - -   7
            - - - - - - - - - - -   8
           - - - - - - - - - - -   9
          - - - - - - - - - - -   10
         - - - - - - - - - - -   11

 H to play and win (H=horizontal, v=vertical)
 Enjoy! 






   The winning move for H is B1.

   In a nutshell, a C2 response by v will end up laddering up to
   g1, for example: B1-C2-C1-D2-D1-E2-E1-F2-F1. Now, if v moves G2,
   we reply J5, and if v moves J5, we reply H4.

   A similar, but shorter, ladder occurs on the west side, laddering
   over to the H piece at C5. Ex:B1-A1-B2-A2-B3, and H is connected
   on the west.

   Depending on v's responses, these two move sequences could be
   intermingled (ex: B1-A1-B2-C2-C1, etc), but the outcome is still
   the same.
