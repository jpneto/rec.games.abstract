Looking at Cage again, I wonder if there isn't a better starting
position, one that is less chaotic and allows for some positional

Thanks for the feedback, fizzy.  Of course, different starting
positions will improve the game in different ways, but there's
probably no ideal starting position.  The main thing I like about the
current setup is that it's the Clobber setup.  That's kind of an end
in itself for me.  A little chaos up front isn't such a bad thing.  It
randomizes the initial play, helping keep first/second move advantage
at bay.  Once a little space opens up, the chaos gives way to
positional play.

The suicide capture isn't something to avoid.  There's no premium on
keeping your army maximized throughout most of the game.  In fact
being oversized may even be a liability at some stages.  It's not
unusual for a small army to decimate a larger one with multiple jumps.


Thanks for giving us some rationale.  The idea of chaos that gradually
gives way to order is interesting.
