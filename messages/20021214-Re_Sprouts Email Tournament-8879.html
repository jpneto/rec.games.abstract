Matthew,

The parity of the survivors would control the parity of the moves. 
Let's use "liberty" (L) such that we can say that every move uses up
two liberties but puts one back.  The total number of moves in a
position will be L - S, where "S" is the number of survivors.

The 5 spot game is a good example.  The player moving first can win by
forcing the production of 4 survivors.  Since every original spot has
3 liberties, the total number of moves will be 3*5 - 4.

Misere sprouts is sprouts in which the player moving last loses.  The
player moving first can also win the 5 spot misere game, this time by
forcing the production of 3 spots.

If you experiment a bit with both types of the 5 spot game, I think
you will see how powerful the survivor concept actually is.  You will
be able to beat all of your friends, almost without thinking, and
certainly without exhaustive tree analysis or any Sprague-Grundy
analysis.  And your friends will keep playing long after you know they
should resign.
