<dvdavins@earthlink.net> writes:


Yesterday I thought up a new hex variant.

The idea is to have simultaneous movement, rather than the swap rule. Each
player secretly indicated their intended hex, and then the intentions are
revealed. If each player chooses the same hex, then the process is repeated
except that the originally intended hex of off-limits until the next time
pieces are successfully placed (sort of like in Diplomacy).


You could, theoretically, end up in a situation where all remaining
hexes are unavailable, i.e., if both players always choose the same
hex.  If it is always clear which is the better hex to play, this
could very well be the case.  You could declare the game a draw if
this happens, but one of the nice features of standard hex is the lack
of draws.
