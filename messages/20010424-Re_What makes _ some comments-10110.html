Actually, what impressed me most about the article was the mention of "Nine
Men's Morris enthusiasts" and "the current world champion."  This tells me
that people still do play the game seriously--which means that even after
developing a good deal of skill at the game, they still consider it
interesting.  The world champion, I'm guessing, has not personally "solved"
the game.  He doesn't go into a match expecting that he and his opponent
will make the best moves every time (and that therefore every game will be a
draw).  Rather, he presumes that he and his opponent will likely both make
mistakes--and the player who makes the fewest mistakes will win, unless his
opponent can force a draw.
