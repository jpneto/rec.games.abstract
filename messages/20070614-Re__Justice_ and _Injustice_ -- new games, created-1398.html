For Injustice with 2 plates, here is the correct resolution (I think):

The losing initial positions are:
       
    (a,a) where a>=2 where a is even
    (a,a+1), (a+1,a) where a is even.

All other initial positions are winning.

If you change the rules so that, for Injustice, balanced initial
positions are immediately winning for player 1, then the losing
initial positions are:

   (a,a+1), (a+1,a) where a is even.

All other initial positions are winning.

The reason for the proposed rule change is simplicity and consistency.

For example, assuming the rule change, for Injustice, you get the
following:

No need to bar (0,0) as an initial position. The game is over, player
1 wins.

The initial position (2,2) is a _win_ for player 1, not a loss.

Thus, (2,2) is a win for whatever player receives it, regardless of
whether it's an initial position or a position received during play.

In general, the proposed rule change would imply that the status of a
position depends only on the position, not on whether it is initial or
not.

Hence, I strongly recommend changing the rules so as to declare
balanced positions terminal. The status of such balanced initial
positions would be the same as if player 2 had just finished moving.
Thus, for Justice, a balanced initial position would be a win for
player 2, and for Injustice it would be a loss for player 2.
