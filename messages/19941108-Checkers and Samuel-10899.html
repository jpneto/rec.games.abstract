Arthur Samuel's checker-playing program defeated a self-proclaimed
master in 1962.  This game went around the world as one of the first
major successes in artificial intelligence.  From this single event,
the impression that checkers was "solved" evolved.  Samuel himself
had no illusions about the program's strength.

I am looking for any references (books, journals, magazines, etc)
that make extravagent claims about Samuel's program.  Any pointers
would be appreciated.
