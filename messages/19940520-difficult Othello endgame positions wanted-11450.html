Hi everybody.

I'm interested to compile a collection of positions, with about 15-17 empty
squares, which computers find hard to solve.  These need not be actual
positions from a real game -- invented ones is fine.  For example, the following
position seems to take much longer than the average position with 16 empty for
computer programs to solve:

		   a b c d e f g h
		 1 o o o o o o o o
		 2 o o o o o o o o
		 3 x x x x x x x x
		 4 x x x x x x x x
		 5 o o o o o o o o
		 6 o o o o o o o o
		 7 . . . . . . . .
		 8 . . . . . . . .

Black (x) to move.

Here the best move is b7 (or g7), losing by 4.

Does anybody else have other challenging positions that are relatively
difficult for computers?  I'd like to accumulate a small library of such
positions, which can be used as a benchmark when comparing the endgame
speed of various computer programs.
