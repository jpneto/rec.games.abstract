There are indeed very strong moves for the second (the first
non-Cathedral) player.  The r-pentomino and u-pentomino pieces should
be placed astride opposite corners in such a way that they can be
connected by the w-pentomino piece (or individually complete their
corner with that piece's help).  Thus, on his third move, the second
player will be able to close off one of the two corners, or else
connect the two corners for a huge territory.  If the opponent is
aware of this, she can mitigate the damage somewhat.

Fortunately, the game is short enough that it can be played in several
pairs of games, switching sides and keeping a running total score.
