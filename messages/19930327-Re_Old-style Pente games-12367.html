Jumping back yet another step, the really cool pente sets in the tube

As for Go in China/Japan being as popular as Pro Golf is in the USA,
that is a drastic overstatement.  There were people speading the same
sort of ridiculous exagerations of the popularity of chess is the USSR
(well, back when there was a USSR).  I don't want to knock the popularity
of Go, as I freely admit that it's a very popular board game, but golf
in the USA has a lot stronger backing than Go (or Chess) do anywhere.

Aside from the established PGA, LPGA, and Seniors tours, which are
covered weekly by television and viewed by hundreds of thousands on
any given weekend during the season and by tens of millions during some 
of the celebrity events like the Pebble Beach Classic, there are also
numerous local charity and professional golf events which include a lot
of people throughout the country.
