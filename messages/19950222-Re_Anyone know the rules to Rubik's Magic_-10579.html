This is another attempt from memory, but I think it fills in more of the
details than in the last post:

The first move is made by placing one of your own pieces anywhere on the
board, either way up.

Subsequent moves; 
1. Flip an opponent's piece over into an empty, adjacent (not diagonal)
square so that it shows the other colour.  If all your opponent's pieces
are blocked in and cannot be flipped, skip this first stage.   

2. Place one of your own pieces on any free square, either way up.

Those two stages must be taken in that order.

You have only won if a line of three of your own pieces (even diagonally)
of the same colour cannot be broken by your opponent on their turn.  Just
producing the line is not a victory, nor is the creation of a line of your
pieces by your opponent's flip (although if they cannot break it next
turn, they will lose).

I seem to remember that it was impossible to draw, even with the strict
winning requirement above.  If you do find your games tending to
stalemate, I must have mis-remembered something.
