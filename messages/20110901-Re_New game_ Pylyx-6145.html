Actually, it hasn't. I quote from the Mind Ninja ruleset:


1. Player 1 invents a pattern, describes it to player 2, and then takes
as many consecutive turns as he wishes (or none at all).
2. Then, player 2 decides which player is the builder, and which is the
blocker.
3. Starting with the builder, the players alternate turns.


So, if player 2 chooses to be the builder, he will also play the first
move, but if he chooses to be the blocker, he will play second.
However, in Pylyx:

a) No matter which side is chosen by player 2, he will never play
first.
b) Player 2 may decide to play first instead of choose a side. Mind
Ninja doesn't provide for this.

Mind Ninja's is a true balancing mechanism, whereas Pylyx's isn't. Of
course, it isn't a pie analogue, either.
