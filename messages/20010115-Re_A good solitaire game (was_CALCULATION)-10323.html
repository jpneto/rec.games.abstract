In article <93r8t0$rfu$1@cantuc.canterbury.ac.nz>, mathwft@math.canterbury.ac.nz (Bill Taylor) wrote:


I stagger the cards in the holding piles so that all are visible,
and position them a few millimeters to the left or the right
according to [where] I plan to put them
Is this cheating?


Not a bit!  I do that too.  There's no point in solitaire in taxing one's
memory with "writable-down" information - there's no human opponent to feel
"cheated" by it, (even though it wouldn't be *strictly* cheating even in
a multiplayer card game); so please go ahead and indent.

I go further in some solitaires - there are a few that involve picking up
a laid-out exposed row of cards and using it for re-deal.  I always consider
myself able to remember it all, and check back to view it later if I want,
(but not viewing unseen or reshuffled cards, of course).


I think it depends.  In Klondyke (a.k.a. Windows!) solitaire, there is a 
business of counting to threes, and remembering what colour kings (in 
particular) are in the discard pile.  I'd consider it cheating to write 
that down.
