Whenever you place a pentomino on the board so that it lies edge to edge with a previously placed piece, a certain number of its constituent squares will abut those of another piece.  For example, if you place the I piece lengthwise against the top bar of the T it will abut three such squares. So you score three points for doing so. Following the same principle, each in turn places a piece on the board and scores 1 point for each of its squares which abuts a square of any and all adjacent pieces. The winner is the player with the greatest total when no more pieces can be played, a typical score being about 32 (perhaps not surprisingly). The first player scores zero on the first turn, of course.

I suppose this version might be called Pointominoes...
