Elements of this game occurs in Aquire, an abstract game that is
themed around hotel chains.

In Aquire, you put square tiles on a grid.  Each group of tiles (of at
least two) represents a hotel chain, and when two groups of tiles
connect, the chains are merged.  Scoring is through shares which are
affected by the size of a chain and which get bonus points when a
chain is merged.  You can't place tiles in arbitrary spaces -- each
tile has one place it can be put, and you draw a hand of random tiles.

So, while there is some similarity in the sense of merging groups of
nodes, there are also very large differences.

Aquire is, BTW, a very good game.
