I think a certain amount of that is inevitable in an
undergraduate course. It won't really sink in until you
are using it in a non-course context (which might include
simply a different course from the one where you learned it).
In full-time undergraduate study, there simply isn't much time
to be looking at any applications except your next course.

Perhaps you might enjoy reading on your own over the summer,
either a more advanced abstract text (such as Real Analysis,
perhaps the text by Rudin) or even some of the research
literature.
