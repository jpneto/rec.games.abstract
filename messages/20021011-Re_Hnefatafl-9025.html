Here's what I gather from my reading so far (excerpted from an article I
wrote on Fidchell and the Ballenderry game board generally thought to have
been a "Tafl" game):

There are some opinions which argue that Irish game Fidchell was not a
Tafl-style game. One argument suggests that it was not any game that is
currently known. The other theory is that it was a British variant of
Latrunculorum. Although it is recorded as early as the first century B.C.,
Latrunculorum itself is a subject of debate among classical scholars. A
number of Roman-era game boards in both square and rectangular shapes and
in varying sizes are believed to be examples of it...

Saxon invaders and therefore gradually replaced Gwyddbwyll.
