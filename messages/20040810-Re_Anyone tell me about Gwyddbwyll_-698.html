Quoting Mabon Dane's message of Sunday:


Does anyone think that Gwyddbwyll and the irish game Fidchell are the
same game?


Eoin MacWhite wrote an article in 1946 for Eigse: A Journal of Irish
Studies, in which he describes fidchell as a game of equal forces.  He
points out the fact that the names of the games are  etymologically
similar, but doesn't go so far as to say they are the same game.  "Wood
sense" is a very general term, after all.

H J R Murray [A History of Board-games Other Than Chess, p.34] seems to
think that they are one and the same game, both of the Roman ludus
latrunculorum type.  I think it probable that geographical separation
might allow some differences to arise, but that they are still of the
same game family, if not the same game.
