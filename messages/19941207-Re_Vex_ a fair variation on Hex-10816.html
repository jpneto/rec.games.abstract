I am somewhat dubious of n-move equalization because player A can select
an opening which is nontraditional but well-analyzed beforehand.  Player B
lacks knowledge of the analysis and is therefore even further disadvantaged!
Eventually everyone learns the "secret", but there are a lot of opening
moves!  Possibly a few fixed openings (as in checkers) can result in a
more equal game.
