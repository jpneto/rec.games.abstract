Many good games have possibilities of draws: Chess and Go are just a few
examples.  Chess and Go have artificial rules to avoid infinite play,
and it would be easy to add a similar rule to Stackers if it is found to
be needed.  I would not do so unless infinite play turns out to be a
problem, though.

In fact, you can argue that the possibility of draws is an advantage for
deterministic games, as absense of draws implies that one player has an
advantage.  Granted, presence of draws do not guarantee fairness, but it
is a requirement.
