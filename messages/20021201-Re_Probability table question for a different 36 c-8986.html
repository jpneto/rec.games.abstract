This is not quite the same as a 2D6 dice roll, Richard.  Let's say the
first card drawn is a 1.  The second card has a slightly lower chance
of being a 1 (since there are now five 1s and six of every other
number in the deck).  This imbalance in distribution will continue
(possibly in more dramatic ways) until the deck is reshuffled.

Now, if you're trying to emulate the roll of two ordinary dice, your
method is better than the deck of 36 cards with every combination on
them.  For example, if the first card drawn from the latter type of
deck is 1-1, you can't possibly get another result of "2" until the
deck is reshuffled.  If the first two draws from the former type of
deck ar both 1, the odds of getting a result of "2" is much smaller
than 1/36, but it isn't zero.

However, as you note, the purpose of the card deck isn't to emulate
normal dice rolls, but to ensure an even distribution.  In this
regard, your deck doesn't do as good a job.  It's entirely possible to
go through an entire deck and never get a result of a "7".  So while
your deck might be an interesting middle ground between the two
extremes, I'm not sure it really accomplishes what players are looking
for in a card deck.
