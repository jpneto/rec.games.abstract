Thanks! Yes, it surely resembles Dao. My program
creates a table over the number of remaining moves
by perfect play for all positions, so the 2 x 4
instead of 2 x 3 pieces may be just a little too
much to handle. I have played Neutreeko against
myself and it seems alright. Two years ago I
considered a similar game where the pieces only
moved one square at the time. There were perfect
matches with up to 17 moves and very few draws,
but the problem was that the players could set
up threats all the time and there seemed to be
too little room for creativity. I think Neutreeko
is better in that respect. Yesterday, I found
that there were only 2 ways to complete a match
until there were only 6 moves left starting from
the "most advanced" position and playing perfectly.
But there may be many possible deviations for
white where it is still hard for black to find
the win.

The rules of Dao appear to be:

1. Start in this position:

   O . . X
   . O X .
   . X O .
   X . . O

2. X moves first, and then players alternate.

3. A move consists of moving one piece horizontally, vertically, or
   diagonally.  The piece must move at least one space, and must keep
   moving in that direction until it reaches the edge of the board, or
   the next space is already occupied.

4. A player wins by achieving any of the following.  All four of the
   player's pieces in a horizontal or vertical row.  All four of the
   player's pieces in a 2x2 square.  All four of the player's pieces in
   the four corners.  One of the player's pieces in a corner and the
   three adjacent spaces all occupied by the other player.
