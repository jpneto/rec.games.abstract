Here's the first big problem: a single stone can have up to six
liberties, compared with four. Two connected stones have ten liberties,
instead of six. So every time you add a stone to a line and your
opponent forms his own wall alongside, you get three extra liberties,
instead of the one you get in two-dimensional go. Imagine how easy it
would be to dodge in go if you could run away straight up or straight
down. This makes encircling groups, and hence territory, very much
harder. Hard enough it probably isn't interesting. In a way it's a
	[...]


How about a go game that starts with a randomly generated graph,
rather than the rectangular grid pattern?

Each node would be connected to 4 other nodes, but they would
be randomly chosen. New configuration would be generated
for each game.
