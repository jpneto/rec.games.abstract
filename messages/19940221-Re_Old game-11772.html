This sounds like "Polar bears around the ice hole". Teacher rolls some
dice ( it doesn't have to be just two) and then announces
"There are <x> petals around the rose/bears around the ice hole".

The pupils have to figure out the rule that generated the number x from
the numbers showing on the dice.  The answer for this game is that a 
dot in the centre of the dice face is a rose (or ice hole), and x counts
the number of dots in the corners around it (petals or bears, depending).
