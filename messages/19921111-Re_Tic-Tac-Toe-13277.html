In article <1992Nov10.162826.17312@husc15.harvard.edu> blom@husc15.harvard.edu writes:

  [2,2,infinity,5+] is a first-player win.


Wrong.  n-in-a-row with two players on an infinite two-dimesional board
is a draw for n>6.  I think that for n=6 the answer is not known.

Reference: _Winning Ways: For Your Mathematical Plays_, vol. 2,
Berlecamp, Conway, Guy. Academic Press.

The form of the proof is: Pair up the squares into pairs so that every
possible winning line contains at least one pair.  (It is not obvious
that you can do this, of course.)  Then the draw strategy for the second
player is: Whenever the first player places an X in one element of a
pair, the second player places an O in the other.
