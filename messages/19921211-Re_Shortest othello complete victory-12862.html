  I have found a solution involving 14 moves; 7 by each player.


This is certainly the shortest game I've seen which is a 2nd-player win.
But surely shorter games which are first-payer wins are known ?

For instance....

     9     ( # plays first)
     8
     # o 7
     o # 6
 3 2 1 4 5

....is a win with 9 moves. An informal hand-search appears to show this is
the shortest possible. However I'm sure many keen types must have programmed
searches, and considering 9 moves need not be exceeded, achieved results
fairly quickly. 

Can anyone verify there is no shorter game ?

Has anyone found a 2nd-player win shorter than 14 moves, as above ?

Has anyone found shorter games of either sort in Reversi, (i.e. allowing
the start position  o o  ).
