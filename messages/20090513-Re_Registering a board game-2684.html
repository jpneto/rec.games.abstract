On May 13, 9:11 am, luis.9.8...@gmail.com wrote:


Imagine that I go to a game publisher company and propose them a good
idea for a game. I know this may be a rather silly question, but... if
I haven't patented my idea, couldn't they steal it to me, publish the
game, earn a lot of money and pay me nothing for my "creation
efforts"?


Yes, in theory. But I'm pretty sure none of the respectable game
companies will do this, any more than a book publisher will run away
with an author's idea. The risk of bad publicity outweighs the cost of
paying you royalties, almost certainly. Remember, you won't get rich,
or even get an income you can live by. There are only a handful of
professional game designers in the world.
