Both players start with 32 disks to place.  If you run out of
disks (due to your opponent being unable to play) you can
no longer play.

I've never seen a rule about playing a non-flipping piece.

Jan Kristian Haugland wrote:
On Tue, 25 Jun 2002, Ralf Gering wrote:


And which do you think makes for a better game?

I prefer Reversi.


And why is that? IIRC, there is a (possible?) rule in Reversi
that says that a player can put down a disc without flipping
anything if there are no legal ordinary moves. This is a quite
unpleasant rule for the player who is ahead, who has to keep
opening up moves instead of clinching a quick wipeout.

As for the first 4 moves in Reversi, either player can force
a parallel position, while the diagonal position in Othello
can only occur if both play for it. Is there something about
the parallel position that you like?

Other differences that I missed?
