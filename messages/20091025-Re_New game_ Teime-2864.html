Has everyone had a brain short-circuit, or what?

This allegedly new game is not greatly different from other attempts
to make a decent connection game on a hexagon, that uses
the alternate-edges-Y condition as the chief win criterion.

In particular, this claim...


as in Hex, any random filling
of the board always produces one, and only one, winner.


....is complete nonsense!

The board is almost certain to fill up with both players
getting to create a "Teime", namely one connecting edges A,B,D
and the other edges D,F,A.   (Marking edges cyclically, as obvious.)

That is, both create a winning position, and the one who does it first
wins the game.  It becomes a mere race.  Which we hate.  And in
particular, it essentially becomes a race to sprint across from
one side to the opposite one, in the above case A to D,
(then, in this game, join up to a third).

What on earth is everyone thinking of!!

Of course it is playable, but it's a mere race, and so of no interest
to us true connection-game afficionados, who regard the essence
of a connection game as being its win/loss complementarity,
described in the excerpted comment above.

See my parallel post, "The HEXA-Y problem", for a full account.
