LOGITAC

Several versions of LOGITAC use the same game principal: the manoeuvre of
pawns and pieces on several boards the surface of which becomes
smaller and smaller, thus giving, through simple movement rules, many
more possibilities than all known strategic games [note: this is
false, i'm just copying the lierature; GO is much more combinatorially
complex, but hey!].  In addition, some of the squares on these boards
are subject to special provisions, hence a multiplication of the
number of possible moves.


The LOGITAC basic model is a three-dimensional game consisting of four
boards located over one another as a pyramid with a square base.

In another version, boards are placed as a staircase.

The third version which is nearer to conventional games because of its
configuration presents all the boards on a single game level.
