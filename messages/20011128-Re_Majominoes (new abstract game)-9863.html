Bob Harris wrote:


4) It is X's turn to move.  O has already used her double move.  Who wins?

+--+--+--+--+--+
|  | o  o  o  x|
+  +--+--+  +--+
|  | o  o| x|  |
+  +--+  +--+  +
| o   | x  x|  |
+--+  +--+  +  +
| o|  |  | o| x|
+  +--+  +--+  +
| x      | x   |
+--+--+--+--+--+


As O already has won two regions, X needs all the remaining ones.
But since O is ahead in total in the two regions bordering the
West edge, X can't prevent O from winning one of those as well
and hence the game. Even if one of the two X's in the East-
South region was in one of the two critical ones instead, O 
could still win by playing anywhere in those two until they were 
full.
