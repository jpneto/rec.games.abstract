The closest thing to a 3-D version of Connect 4 is the game sold as "Score Four".  Unlike 3-D Tic-Tac-Toe, the markers (beads) played in Score Four drop down to the base/bead beneath them.  This makes the game similar to Connect 4, in which the checkers also drop down.  I have found Score Four to be much more interesting to play than 3-D Tic-Tac-Toe.

Since there are at most 16 possible moves in Score-Four, of which only about 4 to 8 are "good moves", it should not be hard to get a computer program to play an almost unbeatable game by using a standard min-max strategy with alpha-beta pruning.
