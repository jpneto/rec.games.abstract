The space X := Q \cap [0;1], of all
rational numbers between 0 and 1, is
especially educationally attractive.
For small children you may consider
fractions with their denominator
bounded by an additional constant
parameter, like 9 or 12...

It's much better for children to play
this kind of games then to practice
the operations on fractions by doing
repetitive calculations out of any context.
Here, during the dispersion game, they'd
do a lot of fraction comparisons and
subtractions without even realizing that
they are performing a tiring task behind
the scene.
