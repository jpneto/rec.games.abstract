 >Yes, moving in the center wins in 7x7.  Jing Yang wrote out a

 >The 2-2 and 3-3 points (B6 and C5) on the short diagonal win too.
 >My program can play perfectly starting from any of those three moves.

 >o evaluate a borderline move (e.g. A4) then a strong move like those.
 >I haven't tried 8x8 or 9x9 yet.  I wonder if on larger boards
 >there are more or fewer winning first moves.

Isn't it possible to prove, in a more general way, that playing to the
center of an NxN board (N odd) is always a win for the first player?

It cannot be a draw, and having the first move cannot be a disadvantage
for the first player (since the game is monotonically increasing), so
it is a win.  All that needs to be shown is that playing to the center
hex is at least as good as any other move, which seems obvious, at
least intuitively.  Hex is a fairly well studied game, has any work
been done on this?
