One note about voting.
After discussing the 100 point method for voting with various
potential judges I've concluded that it's probably not such a good
idea in this instance.  If one judge really likes one game he can give
that game 100 points just to make sure it wins.  This really sucks if
the other judges try to distribute their points in a fair way.  I can
add this to the ballot side by side with a different voting system if
anyone is truly attached to this method.

The method that I think suits the contest best is simply writing the
games in order from favorite to least favorite.  This reflects also
the sentiment that people are picking their "favorite game" and not
one which meets a 'stacking criteria' or some other fabricated
standards.  A list also forces a logical distribution of point value
without having to fiddle with numbers as a voter.
