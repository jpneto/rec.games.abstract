 i have been holding this post "unread" for awhile.
 Pentominoes really struck a major amount of chords
 in me when i was young.  there was something so
 cool about them, like the 12 gates to the city or
 something.  as i got familiar with them, they each
 had bad moments and good moments - they each had a
 unique personality.

 unfortunately for me i was 1st exposed to them in an
 alternate game called Pan-Kai.  in Pan-Kai, each
 player had a complete set of the 12 pentominoes and
 the board was 10x10 instead of Golumb's 8x8.  thus
 you had the excellent variation of walling off a piece
 that your opponent had played already.  BUT this version
 still heavily favored the 1st player.

 this could have been a perfect game.

 it was *so* close.  or so it seemed to my youthful eye.
 i got sucked in.  i got swept away in this, the most
 beautiful of Distractions, the world of Abstract Games.

 see the FAQ for details, but let it be known that as far
 as i am concerned all these role playing games and avalon
 simulations are so tiny in the whole deal here.  games
 of cards can be cool, scrabble, monopoly, all manner of
 very fine games in that genre have made their appearance.

 there were all these games but the one that seemed to
 stand tall in my boyhood was Chess.  i hated Chess because
 is was very quickly made obvious to me that the strength
 of a chess was very dependent on the ability to remamber
 move sequences of games played 10's 20's 30's and even as
 far back as 100's of years ago.  all the winning players
 knew most of these games by heart.

 so them when my father gave us this Pentominoe game one
 christmas or so, it was a Door.  i was wrong of course.
 but it seemed like Chess didnt have to be King.  then i
 saw an article about Go in our local newspaper.  there
 was a photo of a position and a writeup.  i was curious
 as hell.  i remember my dad cautioning me that "you could
 get your whole life lost in Go...."

 fortunately for me, after a few years of delving into it,
 i saw, again, the cooked book openings.  but even more, i
 saw how the 1st move still had the advantage like it did in
 Chess and Pentominoes.  still i had to give a big round of
 applause to Go for being what i thought was the Perfect Game.
 there were even Conclusions about Life and Death according
 to the shape of eye space, most notably Pentominoe shapes!

 since then, i have seen in my mind a weird thing.  in Go
 there is a Ko rule.  there are various rules by various
 official organizations about multiple Ko rules.  there is
 a contingent that says these rules are all saying the same
 thing eventually.  i regard the fact that they needed to make
 a rule about the Ko as a DEFECT in the game.  most games i
 have met have many more defects, often hundreds.  so when
 i say Go has 1 defect, that is still impressive.  but then
 i thought about the 1st move advantage & now think Go has
 2 defects.  HOWEVER!!!!!!!!

 let me state right here that the Ko rule in Go has created
 some of the world's most amazing sequences of play ever
 recorded in the history of mankind.

 for me, if the Rule uses any language like "except", then
 it is perilously close to a Defect Exposed & Dealt With via
 this Rule.

 this would naturally assume that the Perfect Game has no such
 defects....LOL.  so maybe that isnt True?

 so i spent a lot of time getting into Go and feeling smug
 about my 1st impressions of Chess and so on.

 but when Bobby Fisher played Boris Spassky on PBS and Shelby
 Lyman had all those wonderful guests....wow - see, most
 sports fans talk about action games like football or basketball
 or baseball....but for me I WAS ON THE EDGE OF MY SEAT for
 each move, even though this was no longer my Standard of
 Abstract Games.

 if it had been a Pentominoes Championship on TV...........

 2001: A Space Odyssey originally tried to imagine that in
 the future they would be playing Pentominoes instead of
 Chess or Go and that was how they originally had HAL
 scripted, but after the trial balloons, they sadly fell
 back to the western world's comfortable Chess.

 so no....Pentominoes had a great opportunity, but not
 the follow-through.

 it's a damn shame.  now we have a computer-generated
 solution saying the 8x8 game is solved as a win for the
 1st player.......yeah.  not really a surprise....


 cheers, and great to see another Pentominoe fan.
