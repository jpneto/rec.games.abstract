On 30 Set, 13:24, Matteo Perlini <epicuru...@gmail.com> wrote:

In general I don't like multyplayer abstract games, but someone ask me
whether Ecalper can be played with 3 player...

There are two ways to extend Ecalper in this direction:

1. the player replaces 1 of the previous opponent stones and places 3
stones;

2. the player place 4 stone, the other two players replace one stone
each.

The first one is the obvious extension to a tree-players variant. I
dont like it, because a player can have a big advantage relative to
his previous player if his next player is weak.

The second option looks much better, even if I suppose it can cause
some brain bursting.

I havent tried those variants yet, but if someone will do let us know
how it goes.
