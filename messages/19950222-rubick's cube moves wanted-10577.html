When I learned how to solve the Rubick's cube, there were two main
strategies going around.  Some solved it starting with the corners
and working inward.  The method I studied used the bottom layer, middle
layer, top layer strategy.  The book I used had a notation for "moves"
that looked like this:

To exchange the FRONT-LEFT and the BACK-RIGHT blocks on the top layer:
  U F U R U- R- F-

Where U=upper face, D=down face, F=front face, B=back face, L=left face
and R=right face.  Single letters meant a clockwise turn of that face.
A minus sign meant a counterclockwise turn and a 2 meant a double turn.

I have most of the moves memorized (still?) and I recently decided to
write them down for future reference.  Still, there are one or two moves
that escape me.  If this sounds like the method you used to solve the
cube, could you send me a quick e-mail?  I'd like to find these remaining
"moves".  Thanks.
