Hello!

 From what i have heard and read in the beligan tournaments, there is
no good standard answer on this. I think that 4 Gipf pieces is
considered balanced play. However, 3-5 pieces is common too.

There have also been successful attempts, earlier, to play only with
gipf pieces for a long time (anywhere from 8-20) remembering that you
are allowed to keep playing Gipf pieces even if you have to start
removing your own. This is the way I usually like to play, simply
because setting up a row with 4 Gipf pieces, or a block of them near
the center, is very powerful

On gipf.com, it was also mentioned that one of the tied winner from a
recent tournament played with only 1. To me, this would appear to be a
mistake, but I haven't played enough(because it appears as there is
more freedom with 2,3,4, and often you are able to choose which one to
remove).

In potential games it is a somewhat different matter, since you want
to start playing potential pieces as soon as possible (especially the
Tamsk potential).
