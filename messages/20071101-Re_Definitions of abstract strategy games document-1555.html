Rich Hutnik wrote:

Let's look at the NULL again for a minute:
[Null attributes]
* Types:
- Void: Pieces into Null never return to game (eliminated).
- Single space: Has capacity of one. Prior pieces sent to equivalent
of Void (described above)
- Pool: Pieces can enter back into play into play in any order
- Queue: Pieces enter back into play into a sequence.
- Multiple Queue: Each player has his own queue.

The reason has to do with exactly how games would leave or enter the
game.  It is a minutia difference, but important for games where there
is queuing, and how pieces enter the board.

Sure it's important to the individual game, but back at our Checkmate Championship we have Shogi with a pool of taken pieces to re-enter play, and we have [Western] Chess which has a potential pool from promotions, and I'm sure there are variants with all sorts of other flavours including a void, and so on, and yet they're all being played in the Checkmate Championship.  So my question remains, why do we need to know all that in the basic taxonomy document?

For your tournament taxonomy you only want it if you're using it to define the tournament parameters, or at least so I'd have thought.  As well as e.g. Chartrang plus descendants, do you envisage a tournament category of Null Multiple Queue games?  I'd have thought it's an entirely secondary characteristic for your taxonomical purposes?

I'd be putting that sort of detail in small print in a second tier document, rather than giving it to people to wade through at the off.
