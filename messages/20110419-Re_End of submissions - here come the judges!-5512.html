Thanks for the update Daniel, be assured I'm still interested.

I even ran into a new theme for a follow up contest: suicide. A friend
of mine with whom I'm working (or at least I am :) on an article about
the evolution of draughts variants, is rather fond of "misere"
variants in draughtstype games, variants in which the usual goal is
reversed. I am not. They're not called "misere" for nothing. In fact
it occured to me that no inventor I know has ever invented a game with
suicide as its goal. The "misere" people just take a given game and
reverse the goal. Blah.

If one is so fond of "losing to win", one should taylor a game around
that theme, I feel, not just stick it ad hoc on an existing game. It
need not to be suicide. Finishing last in a race game would qualify,
or grabbing the least amount of territory, or being the last to
connect something to anything.

However, I'll not participate again if the results are so "important"
that all fun is drained from it, and next so unimportant, apparently,
that you have to ask yourself if anyone cares.


Hey, I mentioned misere March 8th in this very thread!  Actually it
was META-misere, i think.

A game where you are forced to lose by killing your opponent - as a
concept in itself.  I could dig this.  Running into the corner where
the only legal moves left to your opponent are Murder.  This would be
different from, say, a game where you run quicker to the rooftop edge
& jump off before your opponent - because that is directly analogous
to a Success game where you attain the winning condition first.
Making your opponent have to do the deadly deed has it's extra
piquance.
