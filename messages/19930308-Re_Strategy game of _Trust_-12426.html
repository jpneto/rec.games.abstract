mmoss@ic.sunysb.edu (Matthew D Moss) writes:


 (b) After D minutes have passes, discussion MUST end. Each player then
	creates Z (=3) proposal(s), which can be of the following kinds:



		"I will give to <player> <#> points."
	    or  "I will take from <player> <#> points."



	[I couldn't think of any other forms at the time.]




I see a couple of problems, but the biggest one is if someone is coming
close to getting Y points. In such a situation, I imagine that no one will
vote for any proposals he makes, and no one will propose to give that person
any points. I can think of a few possibilities: (1) hide all points from
players except their own, (2) change # of votes a player has based upon
the amount of points he has (either proportionally or inversely). Neither
of those sounds terribly pleasing though.


Killing two birds with one stone...

Why not have one of the (possible) proposals be:  
  I get to see the score of player P

Sure, P will vote against it, but everyone else might vote for it (under
the agreement that you will reveal P's score next turn, or something).
