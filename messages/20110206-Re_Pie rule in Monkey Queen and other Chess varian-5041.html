As it happens Benedikt Rosenau and I are working on an e-book or app
called "On the Evolution of Draughts Variants" and there's a lot to
say about Draughts in this regard. First and foremost, Draughts
variants in general hardly suffer from any first move advantage. Dutch
10x10 grandmaster Hans Jansen [http://nl.wikipedia.org/wiki/
Hans_Jansen_(dammer)] is known for caring little about the general
measure of 'progress' of his position, as long as the tempo conditions
are in his favour: in Draughts one can have a huge advantage in
general progress and yet get stuck in a web of bad continuations.

Regarding boardsize, Malaysian, Sri Lankan and Canadian progressed to
12x12 and never made it to 'international'. Draughts is not
'infinitely scalable'. Playing it on 20x20 is about as interesting as
playing it on 4x4. That suggests a game of maximum interest, somewhere
in between.
The chronic illness of Draughts has a different source than bordsize.
