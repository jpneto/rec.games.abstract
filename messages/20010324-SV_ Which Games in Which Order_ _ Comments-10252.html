Octi


A fascinating game, but it has rather complicated rules (although the
producer claims the opposite).


This is a little like Go, you could describe the rules in 5 minutes, but
some special cases that require some care are needed later. I've got this
game played immediately with all (around 10) new players I tried it with.

Octi quick-rules.
1. At your turn, you may add a prong, move or jump a pod, or add a pod to
one of your own bases, or a base you occupy (some of the options are subject
to restriction, you have a limited number of pods and prongs)
2. Pieces move one space, or jump as in chinese checkers, but restricted to
the direction of the prongs. Several pieces may occupy the same square, but
you may only jump onto empty squares. You may move and/or jump one or more
of the pods on the same square during a turn. Capture is always optional.
3. The game ends when 1 player occupies 1/2/3 bases of the opponents,
depending on the rules.
(These rules are not complete, but simple to explain to anyone in 5
minutes.)
