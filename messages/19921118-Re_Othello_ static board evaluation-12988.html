 1) How many legal moves does the opponent have. A small number is

Be careful of this one! Remember, a good way of minimizing your opponent's
moves is to let him wipe you out (ie. he has 0 moves left). You should
try to also maximize your moves. This measure can be a difference
(ie. my moves - his moves) or a ratio (such as if I have more moves, the
score is (my moves + 1) / (his moves + 1), else if he has more moves, then
the score is -(his moves + 1) / (my moves + 1), else it is 0).

The second ratio method has the advantage that you having 2 moves and him
having 1 move is better than you having 4 moves and him having 3.
