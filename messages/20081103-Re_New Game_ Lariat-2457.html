Mark notes:


emphasize that Lariat is intended to be played on the surface
of a cylinder with one open end.


How is the closed end arranged?  Simplest would be to have
the "key point" connect to all the cells of the next row, like the
spokes
of an umbrella; this would be then an umbrella with
a big hexboard skirting.

This makes it essnetially a Hex game with one of the players
having side-to-side connected (cylindrical) borders, and
the other having regular Hexlike borders.  This doesn't sound
sufficiently different from Hex to be worthwhile; I daresay
that with different connections on the umbrellas part of it
the game could be made more funly different, but not greatly so.

That's why I think the (accicentally invented!) purely flat hexagonal
version is more different - enough to have a unique flavour of its
own!

OC it needs severe equalisation.
My plan of playing Unlur-style is one way,
and Nick's plan for having one player make all the initial moves is
another.

I think Nick's is game-theoretically superior, perhaps; but I think
mine is socially superior, as it doesn't require one player to do
ALL the initial work, but spreads it out evenly.

Nice to be part of an accidental-team game-construction gang!
