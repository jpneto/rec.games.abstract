   Another variant of chess (more an "ancestory") that I've seen rules for is
Shatranj, from India.  If I remember right, it's a four player game, with
each player having 8 pieces, 4 of which are pawns.  I don't think it's widely
played in this day and age, but you can find the rules in books on the
history of board games sometimes.
