No.  The "gi" in "shogi" means "chess" or a "chess-like" game.  Saying
"chessgi" would be like saying "chesschess" or "kigi"   :-)
... repetitive and redundent.  Should call it "shochess".


I don't think that "chess" in an inherent part of the denotative meaning 
of "-gi". I suspect a fairer translation would be "strategy game", so 
"chessgi" would mean "the king's game". This is opposed to "shogi", "the 
general's game"; "shochess" would be "the general's king", a silly name 
since there is no General in chessgi.

Also, of course, "chessgi" sounds better and more communicative, to my 
ears anyway.

Chessgi is a great game-- it's one of my favorite chess variants. The 
queen may be too powerful in chessgi, but that just speeds things up...
