Ecalper would be as complex as Oust on the same size board.  In

I didn't put that table for competing with other games. I did it just
for explaining Ecalper is a deep game, also with just 127 stones.

The reason why it has a so big game-tree complexity is because Ecalper
is (more or less) a three-move game.

I didn't study the game-tree complexity of Oust, so it's difficult to
say something relevant here, but, anyway, you should consider the
average number of moves for estimating the game-tree complexity.

How many possible moves in Oust does the player have in the second
turn? I think it's 120. In Ecalper, however, during the second turn
player has 930372 possible moves to choose from.

This, to me, is a strong clue Ecalper has a bigger game-tree
complexity than Outs (with a fair and meaningful number of turns).

But it would be really interesting whether someone can find out the
Outs' game-tree complexity.
