On Saturday, March 4th the second real-time Internet Othello Tournament
was held on the Internet Othello Server (to connect to IOS, telnet to
faust.uni-paderborn.de 5000).  Seventeen participants showed up, and to
keep the number of contestants even (so no byes were necessary), we
entered the computer player "ant" (1-ply logistello) as an 18th player.

After six rounds of Swiss tournament play, the results were as follows:

Here the SO column gives each player's strength of opposition number
(which is just the sum of the scores of all opponents, so the higher the
score the better), while the BQ column gives the Brightwell quotient
(6*SO+discs).  While ties were not officially broken, tied players are
listed in decreasing order of BQ.

Congratulations to Mark Masten and Igor Durdanovic for their well-deserved
tournament victory!
