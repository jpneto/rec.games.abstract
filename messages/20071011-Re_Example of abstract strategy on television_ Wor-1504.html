Rich Hutnik  <getrich@1upandup.com> wrote:

I notice an interesting backgammon clock.  You get so many seconds
to make a move.  If after that fails, then you start to eat into a
reserve clock.  Could something like this fit chess?  Set the base
time to 30 seconds a move, and then have a 30 minute reserve
possibly.


I believe this was invented by David Bronstein (challenger in the 1951
World Championship match) for use in chess.  It doesn't seem to have
been very popular but there's no fundamental reason it couldn't be
used.  Fischer's version, where the time given per move accumulates if
it's not used, is more common.
