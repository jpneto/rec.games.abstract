I recently came across a very brief description of a chess variant

called Alice

Chess, named in connection to Lewis Carrol's character from Wonderland.

It is

apparently based somewhat on ideas in Through The Looking Glass. The

game is

played on two boards: all pieces start on the same board, but after a

piece 

moves, it transferred to the corresponding square on the other board. 
	Anyway, I was wondering if anyone out there has heard of it, and knows 
any more about the rules. What I want to know in particular is: When you

move

to a square on one board which is occupied on the other, what happens?

Is the

move disallowed? Does the piece _not_ change boards? If the piece is an
opponents piece, is it taken? 


The move is disallowed.  Also, the concepts of check are not quite as you
describe:  if the player wishes to move his king, it must escape check on
its original board before transferring (ie the move must be legal *on the
board where it happens*, and then the transfer occurs).
However, interposition by transfer IS ok (the move is legal on its
original board, as the check is not there; and the transfer blocks the
check).

There is also Alice Chess II:  the players set up on different boards.
