dwboll@gr.hp.com (David Boll) writes:


I'm sure some of you have heard of the game where 2 players take turns
placing equal-sized disks on a square or circular board, and the first
player unable to place a new disk without overlapping a previously-placed
one loses...



On circular or square boards, this game is flawed by an EZ first-player
winning strategy: Open in the center, and mirror your opponant thereafter.


Mirror in the centre point, to be precise (or, equivalently, rotate
180 degrees around this)

This only works if the first piece can be placed rotationally
symmetrical around the center.

For example, if you place Nx1 rectangles on an 8x8 board, the strategy
won't work, as you can't place this rotationally symmetrical around
the center. In this particular example, the second player can win by
using the rotational strategy, but this need not be the case. For
example, if the piece is the T-shaped pentanomino, it can be placed in
such a way that it overlaps the middle but isn't rotationally
symmetrical around it.


I'm wondering if this could be fixed by playing on a triangular board...


I would guess so.
