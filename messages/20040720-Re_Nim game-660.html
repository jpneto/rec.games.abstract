Here the number <1,2|1,2> is surreal. It means that if left player
moves first he can leave a simple nim pile of size 1 or a simple nim
pile of size 2. The same goes for the right player.
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surreal_number


All of the numbers are surreal, you just happen to only be seeing the
surreality in the case you are confused about.  :)

The general rules of manipulating the surreal representations of
nonpartisan games are:
1. Both players always have the same options. (definition of nonpartisan)
2. The value of the position is the smallest value that is NOT available
as an option. (the Minimal EXcluded, or MEX rule)
3. Options higher than the MEX are superfluous to determining the
position's value. (the Sprague-Grundy Theorem)

Here are the surreal representations of the first three cases:

0 = < | >
1 = <0|0>
2 = <0,1|0,1>
3 = <0,1,2|0,1,2>

Now, decompose the final case:

(X X)    < (X .)  (X X)  (X .)  (. X) | (X .)  (X X)  (X .)  (. X) >
(X .) -> < (X .), (. .), (. .), (X .) | (X .), (. .), (. .), (X .) >

      =  <   2  ,   2  ,   1  , 1(+)1 |   2  ,   2  ,   2  , 1(+)1 >

      = <2,1,0|2,1,0>

      = 3

where "1(+)1" is the nim-sum of the two independent moves from that
position.
