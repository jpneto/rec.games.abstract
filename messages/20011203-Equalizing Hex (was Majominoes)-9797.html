So tempo is key.  I'd like to make the observation that the usual
proof of a win for Black (first mover) breaks down if White is given
a double-move at the end.  This is also true for Hex.  (For example,
consider a trivial 1x3 "majority wins" game -- White wins.)  However,
in practice few games of Hex (between humans?) fill up the board.
Or can it be true that under perfect play White can keep Black from
winning up to the point where only two cells remain (on an odd board
of sufficient size) -- thereby winning with a double-move?  It seems
highly unlikely but I'll propose this as a problem for the Hex
mathematician :-)

What is the smallest number Wn such that White wins nxn Hex if he is
allowed to make moves number n^2-Wn+1, ... , n^2?
