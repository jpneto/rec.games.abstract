I for one was glad to know about the new development in Chebache. My own
opinion is that the term "abstract game" is used in two ways, a strict sense
(as by game theorists) and a common sense (as by GAMES magazine, and even
perhaps Abstract Games magazine, though rather less so). I lean toward the
strict sense myself. But I think games like backgammon and Chebache would be
of interest to most of the people interested in "strict sense" abstract
games, though not all.
