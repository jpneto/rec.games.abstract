The board is composed of hexagons, arranged as follows:

       7 - - - - - - -                 7 - - - V - - -
      6 - - - - - - -                 6 - - - V - - -
     5 - - - - - - -                 5 H H - V - - -
    4 - - - - - - -                 4 - V H V - - -
   3 - - - - - - -                 3 - - H V H H V
  2 - - - - - - -                 2 - - H V H V H
 1 - - - - - - -                 1 - - V H H V -
  A B C D E F G                   A B C D E F G

Player H tries to connect side to side, and player V tries to connect
bottom to top, alternating stones as in go.  The sides are A1..A7, G1..G7
and top/bottom are A1..G1, A7..G7.  The second diagram shows a win for H.

The board size need not be 7x7, but for some reason odd sizes are popular.
I think the Occidental Math Field Day (popular when I was a kid) used 11x11.
Perhaps the experts can tell us whether the extremely strong center move is
outlawed in "tournament" play, and what sizes are used for the Olympiad.
