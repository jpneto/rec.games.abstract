  When you drop beans, you include the cups on the end as part of your
  move; if your last bean falls into your kalaha (?), you get one extra
  move.  (You can't keep getting moves -- you get at most one extra.)


In the version I had as a kid (a boardgame from Swedish game
manufacturer ALGA), the rules said you could get several free moves.
That meant from this position:

    X X X X X 6
  K             K
    X X X 3 2 1
          a b c

you could get 12 beans. (Play c, b, c, a, c, b)

[ More stuff deleted ]


  Here is the 'capture' move; if your last bean falls in an empty cup,
  you get your bean and the beans in the cup opposite the one you landed
  in (even if the one you landed in was on the opponent's side), and put
  them into your kalaha (?).


In my version, you only captured the beans in the opponent's cup if
your last bean was dropped in one of your own cups. However,
'wrap-around' was allowed, as in:
    
    3 X X X X X
  K             K
    0 X X X X 8

By playing c, you add 6 beans to your kalaha. Note that you don't ever
put a bean in your opponent's kalaha.


  Sorry if I'm fuzzy on rules --- it's been a while since I played.  :)
