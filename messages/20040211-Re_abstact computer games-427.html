onto their board per the computer-game rules?  Since there are
(I think) seven Tetris shapes (reflections count as different)

which player chooses and which divides, until each pair has

Hmmm...

The two players could start with a fixed set of shapes. Each player
gets to put up a shape for the other to play. When a shape falls
off the bottom in the filled lines, then it gets added to the
available shapes to be given to the other player. Though lines
are hard to do when they happen in the middle. A computer could
keep track of this pretty easily. So if part of a shape is still
on the board it's not available.

So is it really good strategy to keep throwing a particular kind
of shape at your opponent? Because if he fills rows with them he
gets them to throw back at you.  Are there combos that need to be
outlawed because they force holes to be left? How many pieces should
be in the starting set, and of which types? How many shapes are
required when the part-on-the-board thing is kept in mind? Can a
player run out of tiles and not have anything to put up for the
other player? Is that a win? A loss?
