"Cybraxis" <cybraxis@nomail.com> wrote in message news:...

It's more fun with a referee....believe me.

When 2 pieces combat the referee is the only one who gets to see exactly
what the two pieces are and decide who wins. This leaves the players with

a

little knowledge of what the opponent has and where, but not too much. If
there is no referee you have to compare pieces yourself and then you know
exactly what the opponent has and where....this is too much knowledge for
the game to remain very interesting. For example if my general takes a
soldier, with a referee present all the opponent would know is that my

piece

was something higher ranking than a soldier. But if there is no referee my
opponent knows where my general is and will go right after him with a
missile.

I have played the game both with and without referee. It's ok with no
referee, but takes a lot more skill and memory if there is a referee,

making

it simply a better game.
