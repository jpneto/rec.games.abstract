 I found some time to go back and work on the java2 applet.
 The upshot is no more blinkety blinking.  the downside is
 no other real progress.  looks like the apache/tomcat
 interface is the way to go.  But for now, it is ready for
 more test driving.  it might be slow in places...and you
 do have to be accurate with your mouse.

 quick review: Xong is an abstract game for 2 players that
 appears to have solved the First Move Advantage Problem by
 breaking up each move into 2 parts.  Like baseball, a Xong
 turn begins with the ball in the hands of the defense.  The
 first part of the turn is done by having the opponent design
 and give you the piece you have to play.  then you can play
 it anywhere it fits.  then you design your opponent's piece
 and give it to him.  since each piece has to be unique, they
 begin small and gradually get bigger until no more can fit.
 when that happens, 1 point is given instead of a piece.

 the applet will take a moment to load.  you should see a
 random board generated with no moves made yet.  Blue makes
 the first xoid (underwhelmingly the one-legged piece) and
 gives it to Red who then makes the first play.  this is done
 by Blue clicking the center of a leg and then the center of
 the GIVE hex.  Red can drag and click around the leg until
 he has it where he wants it.  then he hits the COMMIT hex
 to finsh the play.  now he designs the next xoid, usually
 the two-legger boomerang.  when building a larger piece
 you can click a leg a second time to throw it away.  the
 final built xoid must be all connected and made of vacant
 legs remaining on the board - the applet wont let you pick
 an occupied leg, but if you forget to connect them it will
 attempt to use only the upperrightmost part of it - which
 may then be a xoid already used, so you'll get a message in
 the browser status line.
