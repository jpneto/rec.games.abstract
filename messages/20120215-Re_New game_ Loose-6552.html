The moves that confused you are just a consequence of using SGF, a
format designed to store Go games, to play Loose. The move pairs 42-43
and 63-64 are actually single moves, but I had to enter them in two
steps because of the limitations of the SGF system. 73 is a complete
move in itself: white places at b3 (which would be illegal in Go) and
flips Black's group at once.

As for the rules question, see this example:

http://www.lifein19x19.com/forum/viewtopic.php?p=91293&sid=3b11ce6233....

Here, White has just one true liberty. When Black fills it, White's
group is flipped. At the end of the turn, Black's group (the only
group on the board) complies with the requirement of having at least
one true liberty (it has plenty of them), so Black's move was legal.

Note that not all true liberties are eyes, but all eyes are true
liberties. A group needs 2 eyes to live unconditionally, as in Go.- Hide quoted text -
