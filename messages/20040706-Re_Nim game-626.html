Well, the case for a full even board is still quite easy: The second
player always copies the first player's move on the symmetrically
opposite row/column.  This is always possible, as the board is kept
symmetric and there is no middle row/column, and it obviously makes a
second-player win.  This generalizes to all MxN boards where both M
and N are even and the board is symmetric both vertically and
horisontally or when it is 180 degrees rotationally symmetric (in
which case the second player copies the first player's moves rotated
180 degrees).

Like Martin, I believe an odd full board is a first-player win, but I
will need to think a bit more to find a proof.
