There have been a few articles on the game Nim recently.  So far as I know,
the first mention of Nim was by Bouton who completely analyzed both the
regular form and the misere form of the game in

  C. L. Bouton.  Nim, a game with a complete mathematical theory.  _Ann.
  Math._ (2), 3 (1901-02), 35-39.  FdM 32.225. 

His analysis is the usual one in terms of binary notation or powers of two.
He has no references in his article and only says Nim was a game the college
students were playing in Harvard at the time.

My question: does anyone know where the game Nim came from?  Any mention of
it in the popular press at the turn of the century or earlier?
