I suspect Oust will be much easier for an AI to play than

You're outstandingly wrong on that point, Graham.  Go absolutely has a
larger game tree.  But practically speaking, most of that tree never gets
used.  In Go you typically capture a handfull of singletons during the
course of a long, relatively tame game.  Oust, by comparison, is much more
aggressive.  Larger groups do get captured regularly.  Practically speaking,
the statement "It never plays the same way twice" holds truer in Oust than
in Go.  In Go, an expert player can remember all of the moves in the game. 
That'd be a lot more difficult in Oust.  I believe that the practical play
portion of Oust's tree is larger than Go's.

Add to that that Oust is counterintuitive.  You can get down to one stone
against tens of enemy stones and come back and win.  You often have to "lose
in order to win" in Oust.  Difficult as Go is to program, Oust will be *way*
harder.
