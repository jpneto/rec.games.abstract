An abstract game is never a game of physical dexterity.  Batik is
precluded on that basis alone.  Even if you were allowed to lower the
pieces into position, Batik would *still* not be an abstract game.

Hippos and Crocodiles is an abstract game that involves visual
dexterity.
http://www.iggamecenter.com/info/en/hippocroc.html

Said variant of Batik, on the other hand, is just a game of visual
dexterity.  Batik is a contest of visually estimating precise
distances - something that would not favor the visually impaired.

Think of an abstract game as something that would never disadvantage a
person based on physical, including visual, limitations.  It's
something for your mind - only.  The board and pieces are just tools
to help you visualize the state of the game - not something you should
have to grapple with.

Why is it offensive that Richard Hutnik, president of the

game?  When people see my Abstract Games table, I want them to think,
"Cool, abstract games."  I don't want them to think, "Oh, great.
