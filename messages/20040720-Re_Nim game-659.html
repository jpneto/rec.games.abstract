joc_kay@hotmail.com (Joe) writes:

One can generate 'irreducible' representations by:
1) removing empty rows and columns,
2) swap columns and rows such that a 'block-diagonal' configuration is
reached with the rows and columns in each block ordered by size, and
3) splitting the resulting configuration into 'non-interacting blocks'


This is very good!  I had been looking for a way to isolate
non-interacting subgames and had not found it myself.

I am unfamiliar with the term "block-diagonal".  Is this a standard
term?  I take it to mean that the grid can be divided into quadrants
     |
  A  | B
-----+---
  C  | D

such that there are no stones in quadrants B and C.  Then A and D are
independent games (which may be further segregated by this method).  Is
that correct, or are there other conditions?
