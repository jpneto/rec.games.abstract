There have been several requests for this information:

Chinook is running on a Silicon Graphics Challenge XL.
There are 16 processors, each running at 150 MHZ.  We have
10 GB of striped disk holding 5.6 GB of endgame database
information.  The machine has 1 GB of shared memory,
most of which is for disk i/o buffering.

On this hardware, Chinook is searching a *minimum* of 19 ply
deep, with lots of search extensions.  We seem to average
23 ply throughout a game.  Principal variations average
30 ply in length.  Of course, the database positions
can represent 100s of plys of search depth, making our
effective search depth even greater.

When you look at these numbers, it is amazing to think that
a human is even capable of competing on an even footing with
the machine.
