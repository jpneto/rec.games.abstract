Tinsley is the world champion in "straight" checkers, the version most
played in the United States among white players of English or Scottish
ancestry.  Pool checkers is more popular among black players and immigrants
from Russia and several other countries.  So is International (Polish)
checkers, played on a 10 X 10 board.  Finally Spanish checkers is different
from all of the above. 

Can somebody tell me the status of computers in the other kinds of
checkers?  I know a computer program was playing International checkers
(the 10 X 10 game) a decade ago, but I lost touch. 

Also, since the computer can memorize many positions (and so can a human) I
wonder if it would be at a relative advantage or a disadvantage in
11-man-ballot.  I used to prefer that version of straight checkers because
it threw off the book knowledge of my more experienced opponents.
