Off the top of my head:


1) The game "TRELLIS" is played on a checkered board. BLACK connects
diagonally across only DARK squares, WHITE only across LIGHT squares.


This allows black and white paths to cross, so it would violate the
principle of a filled board having only one winner.


2) BLACK connects on one diagonal direction, WHITE connects on the other.


Ditto.


3) The game "ONYX" suggests several methods that are *opposite* of first
come, first connect (aka Quickway aka Autoconnect, etc).



This suggests to me several ways that might work on a 4444:


3a) Play only on cells. No captures. Player who completes a 2x2 checkerboard
wins the connection. Vertex is marked to show this.


This could work, as does the first-come, first-connect.  I can't
really see if any of these will make a filled board have a winner (but
paths certainly can't cross).


3b) As above, but diagonal connections are automatic, immediate and good
until overridden.


Ditto.


3c) As (3a) with captures. No play whatsoever on vertices. ALL connections
are orthogonal.


This looks like it could create infinite play.


3d) As (3b)+(3c) Diagonal connections are immediate.


Ditto.


3e) Full-on Onyx rules. The only way to get a vertex and connect diagonally
is to play a stone on a vertex. This may ONLY be done if all four squares
around it are empty. Completing a 2x2 checkerboard captures both enemy
stones.


Capture look like potential infinite play.

However, I don't generally like having to explicitly play
vertices/corners.  This makes the board not really a square grid, but
a square/octagon grid.  I have a few suggestions for how to avoid
this:

 1. Use a checker board.  Only black-to-black corners connect
    (regardless of who plays them).  This makes black squares more
    valuable than white squares, but this is symmetrical for both
    players.  This topologically equivalent to a square/octagon grid,
    but avoids plays on corners.

 2. Allow only three out of four squares in a 2x2 to be filled.
    Diagonals automatically connect.  A board is "filled" when no more
    pieces can be played without completing a 2x2.

Option two seems most interesting to me.
