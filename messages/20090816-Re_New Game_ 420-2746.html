Fair comment. I must admit that one of the things I most like about
the abstract games that play is the simplicity of rules. Your one-page
games fit very well. Unfortunately, occasionally corner-cases get
discovered, and rules begin to expand, but that's only natural. (I've
sat on a rules subcommittee for a game played by pedants who love
finding contrived corner cases, and I've seen special cases become 
about 80% of the rules of an otherwise simple game.)


Generic game playing equipment is ideal on a number of
levels, but the most compelling thing about it is that it's exactly
what I need.  Exquisite, fundamental game parts shaped by thousands of
years of evolution.  What proprietary plastic part could I manufacture
that would bear comparison with the Go stone?  Or the checker, or the
Mancala set?  What folly it would be to try.
