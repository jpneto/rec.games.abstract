	Have you ever played either of the commercial games, Choiss or
Schach Plus? In each, you also get a box of 64 squares, so you have to
first set up an orthogonally connected board, after which you take turns
setting up the pieces. In Schach Plus, though, there is the problem of 
setting pieces up carefully, only to exchange nearly everything in the
first few moves - it kind of puts a damper on long-term strategy. Do you
find that Unachess players typically wait till the last minute to intro-
duce their kings on the board? Does the game have a problem with similar
frenzies of exchange?
