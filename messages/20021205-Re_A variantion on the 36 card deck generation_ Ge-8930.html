Heh heh, no you are completely correct. I was just being lazy and trying to avoid explaining that too, plus I didn't want to strain the credulity of young readers by trying to make them believe things were ever that primitive. We did indeed use a d6 and a d20 that was numbered from 0-9 twice. Eventually someone had the bright idea of using a china marker (what we used to write on glass and plastic before they invented AV markers [See! Now do you see how OLD that makes me look and feel? I hope you are happy!]) to color in one set of the numbers, which were then the 11-20 numbers.

Of course, before that we just used d6's from RISK and our SPI games. (Low-High = 1-10 or 11-20) plus (Low-High = 1-5/11-15 or 6-10/16-20) plus a last d6 roll from 1-5, reroll the 6's gave us a d20 roll. We usually rolled three different colored d6's at the same time, with pre-declared colors being used for the first, second, and third rolls. You want to believe we would curse out E.G.Gygax for inflicting this upon us while we ran through the labourious process over and over again.
