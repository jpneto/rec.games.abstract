To get back to the original question about games that are better
suited for humans than computers, there are several factors that can
be exploited to effect this:

 1) Make the search space large.  Most computer programs for games
    rely on (almost) exhaustive search of the next several moves.  If
    this is made unrealisable by having a huge number of possible (and
    not obviously bad) moves, a human may exploit his cognitive
    abilities to beat a computer.  Go makes the search space large by
    having a very large board with legal moves in every gridpoint.
    You can also increase the search space by adding randomness or
    hidden information (e.g. secret setup, as in Stratego).
