This is clearly very different from the other game. I don't know if I
like it as much, since there are an infinite (but countable) number of
distinct guesses between the lower bound and any other guess. The
"second smallest" rule does little to clarify what a winning strategy
might be. There's too much freedom in the guessing to allow for mind
games


There is plenty of room for mind games.  In fact, mind games are the
only way to have a good chance of winning.  Assuming that you're
really clever, and can think of a REALLY HUGE number, you and a
confederate can be pretty certain to win.  But you and your partner
can both betray the other.  (But has this happened?  I'm not saying ;-)

However, I see your point about the uncertainty.  It IS a crapshoot.
Just one where it might help to lie like a dog.
