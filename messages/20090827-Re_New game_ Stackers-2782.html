Torben, stop evading me.  This is the third time around on this
topic.  In Stackers you've got two opposing armies on opposite sides
of the board with pieces that can only move one square, not like Chess
where a bishop might fly three quarters of the way across the board in
one move, cutting through enemy troops in the process, and easily
facilitating the intermixing of armies.  Can we assume that the two
Stackers armies *might* slowly approach each other and make contact,
creating what may *appear* to be a temporary, impasse-like situation?
I'm going to take a leap of faith and assume you're still with me
here.  Neither army is "blocked" in the sense of having no legal
forward moves.  I know that.  BUT, if neither player wants to be the
first to form a mixed stack, AND neither player wants to open a
corridor allowing the enemy to pass through, you've got yourself a
full blown impasse - a lovely example of an unforced draw.

Is the preceding example like two retarded idiots, drooling and
sliding Chess rooks from side to side in place?  No!  In my case, I'm
not a drooling, retarded idiot.  I've played Chess many times and I've
always made some effort to get my pieces out into play.  Nobody had to
teach me that.  It just somehow seemed like the right thing to do.
But in the above described, impasse-like situation in Stackers, I
WOULD NOT WANT TO BE THE FIRST TO FORM A MIXED STACK.  If my opponent,
presumably also not a drooling, retarded idiot felt the same way, we'd
have to agree to a draw!
