Nope, b2 wins.  And some worse-looking moves win too.

My program and I just completed the analysis, 
and the following first moves win for Vertical:

        a b c d e f
     6 + - - - - - 6
    5 + + + + + - 5
   4 + + + + + + 4
  3 + + + + + + 3
 2 - + + + + + 2
1 - - - - - + 1
 a b c d e f 
(The hexes marked + win, and the hexes marked - lose).

I was surprised that a4 wins, and shocked that a3 wins.  The
analysis of a3 was by far the hardest, requiring millions of nodes
of search by the program along with dozens of suggested moves from
me (near the top of the tree).  Several times I thought I found
a refutation, but then found the one continuation for Vertical which
wins.

I can't write down a short proof that a3 (for example) wins.  If you
think you can beat it (or any of these other moves), we could play a
correspondence game. 

Determination of the set of winning moves for 7x7 is probably too hard
for me.
