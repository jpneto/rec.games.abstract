Can anyone advise me as to a cheap and foolproof way of making sets of
pentominoes that are accurate and dimensionally stable, based on squares
of about 25 mm each? Cutting them out of balsa wood or thick cardboard
is not 100 per cent reliable - at least, not in my hands.


Start with manufactured squares and weld-glue?
If you use appropriate materials (eg 'plastic') you should be able to keep
the glue layer to zero - else test and measure its thickness and then sand
the to-be-glued faces appropriately in advance.

Or

Find someone with a CAD-CAM machine who will produced them out of a solid
block of plastic. Such a machine is likely available at a well-equipped
Secondary school and I'm sure the kudos of designing and making the items
would enable a school to offer its services foc. [kids involved,
photographs, &c]
