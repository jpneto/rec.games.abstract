Othello Puzzle #4:
 . . o o o o o .
 x . o x o o . o
 . x x x x o o o
 o o x x x x o o
 o x . x x x x o
 o . x x x x o o

White (o) to play and win decisively ...


Here is the position with coordinates.

    a b c d e f g h
  1 . . o o o o o .
  2 x . o x o o . o
  3 . x x x x o o o
  4 o o x x x x o o
  5 o x . x x x x o
  6 o . x x x x o o
  7 o . x . x x . .
  8 . . . x . . . . 

Note that white must avoid c5 being filled before he has the left
edge, otherwise a black move to b1 or a3 will give him either the top
row or the left edge. If c5 is empty white can respond to b1 by a1 as
black is unable to play a3 to get the left edge. Black has one safe
move: b2, as white can't play the corner without losing the top row.
Nor can white play b1. Any white move must consider the position after
a black move to b2 in addition to the new openings.

Whites possibilities are b2, a3, c5, b6, b7, d7, b8, e8 and f8. B2
gives black two edges. A3 gives him one edge. C5, b6, b7, d7 and b8
either fills c5 or allows black to do so. This leaves e8 and f8. E8
yields

    a b c d e f g h
  1 . . o o o o o .
  2 x . o x o o . o
  3 . x x x o o o o
  4 o o x x o x o o
  5 o x . x o x x o
  6 o . x x o x o o
  7 o . x . o o . .
  8 . . . x o . . . 

Black can now play d7, f8 and g8 in addition to the moves from before.
D7 and g8 are safe, so this gives no advantage to white.
Furthermore, g7 is now safe for black, and g8 is no disaster.

So by exclusion F8 must be the winning move for white ;-).
