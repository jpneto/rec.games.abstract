Personally I like the risk. Chess variants are especially risky, but I
know 'Atlantis behaviour' inside out and all tools were available. The
only requirement, given the recycling mechanism as a new component,
was to select the right combination.
I came close enough to the mark to notice that I was slightly off.
When the game stretched a bit long, the pieces seemed increasingly
forced into roundabout ways towards the target, making the kings
relatively stronger.
It's a matter of taste, but once a reasonable number of tiles has
disappeared, I prefer the occasional blitz attack to succeed. If kings
become relatively safer, games might too often turn into a long and,
in my opinion, tiring hunt.

So I chose to use the 'tile taking' tool instead of 'piece only'
movement. I've seen it do an excellent job to the very same effect in
Caïssa.
