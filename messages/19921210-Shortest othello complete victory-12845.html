   A while back somebody was discussing this:
	What is the fewest number of moves in an Othello game that will leave
	one player with mo pieces on the board?
   Somebody else amnwered with (as I remember) an 18 move solution.
   I have found a solution involving 14 moves; 7 by each player.

		. . . . . . . .   O goes first
		. . . . . . . .
		. . d g i j . .
		. . c # O f . .
		. . h O # a . .
		. . l k e b . .
		. . . . . . m .
		. . . . . . . n

   Well, do I win?  Can anybody beat this?
   I expect that a computer could search all shorter combinations to see if
this is THE shortest.
