@ Mark:
M: Why 2 pieces?
D: Initially I thought to balance the powerful striking movement and
also speed up the game.  Secondly my flatmate had just mentioned that
he wanted to see a game where one either moves or places 2 pieces.
Also when choosing between placing and flipping, placing one piece
tended to feel like a pretty weak move even when forced (maybe that's
a good thing?) We tested it and it worked so I didn't give it too much
more thought.

M: How often do strikes occur in practice?
D: Quite often both times I played someone, but who knows.  It could
just be the styles of the people playing.

M: Is it possible that a play would be completed without any captures?

back to you.  At the end of all possible placements there would be
forced movement and movements can only happen with a capture.  This is
quite possibly a thorn in the game, but yeah... I'll check it out
again. *fingers crossed*
