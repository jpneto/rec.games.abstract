I am used to playing Monster chess where White actually starts with
*all* his pawns, but even then we found that Black could easily win 
by blocking or sacrificing minor pieces for several of superpawns, and
then pushing back the remaining king with his quen and two rooks (it
is really not that difficult).  

So, unless White is hurt by having 4 extra pawns, I think the version
you are discussing here is even easier for Black to win.
