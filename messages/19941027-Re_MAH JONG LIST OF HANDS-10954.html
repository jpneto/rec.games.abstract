5. PURITY HAND                 
Score: 3 DOUBLES PLUS EXTRAS
Any one suit.                                               
No Winds or Dragons.            
Pungs or Kongs and a Pair                
A Chow may be substituted for a Pung.         
If all tiles including the last are drawn from the wall the
    hand scores 2 extra doubles for concealed Mah Jong. 

6. LITTLE BROTHERS                        
Score: LIMIT
Any one suit.                                               
No Winds or Dragons. 
Three Pungs and a Pair.                                    
One Chow.                                      
Concealed (except last).     
FISHING: The ground score is doubled 3 times plus any doubles for Flowers. 

7. CHINESE ODDS                              
Score: LIMIT    
Any one suit.                                               
No Winds or Dragons.  
Pungs or Kongs of odd numbers.                             
Any Pair of odd numbers.          
Exposed.
FISHING: The ground score is doubled 3 times plus any doubles for Flowers.     
                                               


The list certainly need to be worked on.

1.  It is American Mah-jong, which is more Gin-Rummy than Mah-jong.
    Too many special (limit) hands which allows going out with
    other than "4 sets and a pair" pattern.  Also clumsy antique
    point-based scoring rules (most modern Asian rules are "doubles-
    based").  The numerous patterns do not have a strong consistency
    among themselves to be easily remembered.

2.  Almost all the patterns are "one suit"!  "Cleared suit"
    mah-jong is boring stuff!



15. GATES OF HEAVEN                      
Score: DOUBLE LIMIT
Any one suit.                                               
No Winds or Dragons.  
Run of 2-8. Any tile 2-8 paired.              
Pung of 1s. Pung of 9s.                                    
Concealed (except last).
FISHING: 500 points.

16. CONFUSED GATES                        
Score: LIMIT    
A Pung of 1s in any one suit.                               
No Winds or Dragons. 
A Pung of 9s in a second suit.                             
A Run of 2-8 in a third suit. Any tile 2-8 paired. 
Concealed (except last).
FISHING: 400 points.     


This kind of patterns (Confused Gates) adds more burden to the
players than enhance the game.  It is IMO an exploitation of
the relationships between suits.

Wow.  How many "run" patterns do we want.
A "run" isn't that hard.  "Cleared suit" is hard.  Shouldn't
give "run" hands too much, and shouldn't be designating
hordes of "run" special hands.  Burdens the player's
memory without enhancing the game.

Also, too many "concealed hand" patterns reduces player
interaction in the game and makes it less fun and exciting.

I see.  It is the "cleared suit" game.  Yawn.

IMHO, if anyone is wondering why Mah-jong has not become as popular
a game in America as it is in Asia, I can flatly tell you that it
is because of the American "adaptation".  They totally changed the
concept of the game and make it play like a modified version of
Gin Rummy.  Also, the scoring rules are more complex than those
commonly used in Asia, and the game is less playable.

The list IMO consists mainly of infrequent "limit patterns" which
have little impact of the game, but each one must be remembered
by the players and does not add to the flavor of the game.

"Cleared suit" mah-jong is IMO never as fun as the original game.
