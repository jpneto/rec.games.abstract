On a side note, all the Mind Ninja subgames with a scoring system (Product War, for instance) have implicitly a similar tie-breaker, since the blocker player only needs an equal score to win. The rules as written on igGC imply that ties are won by the first player instead of the second, but in the subgames with scoring I think it's better to have it the other way round, at least when played as independent games with the pie rule instead of the Mind Ninja opening protocol. If ties are won by the first player, he'll have to chose a slightly worse first move, and the number of available bad first moves is usually quite low already. Also, more often than not, he'll have one stone more than the opponent in the final position, so awarding ties to the latter should be a fair countermeasure.
