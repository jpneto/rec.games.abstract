The simultaneous move mechanism creates more problems than it solves.
Instead of the unknown of the next dice roll, you have the unknown of
your opponent's current, hidden move.  Neither dice nor simultaneous
move games can be considered perfect information games.  Randomizing
the opening position is also a major tradeoff.  While it deters
cataloging of opening plays, it imposes an element of luck.
