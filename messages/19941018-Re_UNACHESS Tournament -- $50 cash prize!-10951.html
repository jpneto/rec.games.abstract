Beginners, remember  --> You cannot capture and your pieces do not give 
check until you put your king down. This is the key rule that gives this 
game its flavor.



Here again is the email format:

                      RRBBNNQK 
                      PPPPPPPP 
 
 
Example: 
 
 
- - - k - - - r       Black's pieces in reserve 
- - - - - - - - 
- - - - - - - -       r-bbn--- 
- - - - - - - -       pppppppp 
- - - - - - - - 
- - - - - - - - 
- - - - - Q - -       White's pieces in reserve: 
- - - - - - K - 
                      RRBBNN-- 
                      PPPPPPPP 
 
 
1. K>h1   n>g2 !+ 
2. Q>g2   k>d8  +
3. Qxf2   r>h8  +
4. K-g1   q>h1  ++  
 
The '>' symbol means a piece is placed from the reserve. 
 
The '-' symbol represents a move of a piece already on the board. 
 
Using an 'x' as in Bxe2 represents a capture. 
 
Using a '+' as in B-e2 + represents check on the opponents king. 
 
Using a '++' or a '#' represents checkmate. 
 
Using a '!+' means 'not' check or virtual check. This means your king has 
not yet been placed so you cannot capture or give check yet, but one or 
more of your pieces is 'aiming' at the opponents king.
