Hi!



I suspect ervery one in this groups knows the game Reversi or
Othello.  Nonetheless I have a couple of qustions:



1.  Is there any sublte difference between Othello and Reversi?


Some people use the names interchangeably, but the original Reversi
rules had some differences from Othello:

 1) Instead of a fixed initial position, the first four moves were
    played in the central four squares, allowing an

    OO
    XX

    opening in addition to the Othello

    OX
    XO

    opening.

 2) In Reversi, if you pass, you still place a piece. I don't recall
    the exact rules for this, but I believe the new piece just has to
    touch an existing piece.


2.  I believed that there are tow names for trademark reasons.
    Is that true?
    Are the games (names) protected?


The name is trademarked by Mattel. The mechanics aren't, though, so
you can find a lot of games with the same rules (though expressed in
other words). The look-and-feel of the Mattel board is also protected,
so alternative versions use other colours than black+white on a green
board. I have seen many variants of this.



3.  If I'm not mistaken I read some time ago about a winning
    strategy.
    Does one exist?


Sinc this is a deterministic two-player game with full information,
perfect play is possible. As far as I know, it isn't know if perfect
play on both sides leads to white win, black win or tie. On a 6x6
board, perfect play has been determined to yield black win.


    If so, is it simlpe enough to spoil the game for humans?


No. But the best computer programs these days are so good that no
human stands a chance against them. Good programs can, for example,
play the last 16-18 moves perfectly.
