Bill Taylor wrote in recre.bills.gibberish:


Corners have always been a slight annoyance in
connection games; they are easily dealt with by
overlapping the edges, of course, but it seems
ever so slightly clumsy.

In Cornerless Y, the matter is solved by the
opening layout,

The game is Y, with PIE rule, played on a Y
board initialised thus:-


         x o
        o . x
       x . . o
      o . . . x
     x . . . . o
    o . . . . . x
   x . . . . . . o
  o . . . . . . . x
 x . . . . . . . . o
o . . . . . . . . . x
 x o x o x o x o x o
_____________________________________________________________


And that's it!  It's somewhat of a marvel that
this opening distribution guarantees a unique
winner!
