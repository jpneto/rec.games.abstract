Whether or not a game is *theoretically* a first person win or a draw
has little bearing on how good the game is. 


The rules of Pentagonal weren't explicitly stated in the original post
-- I assume that they are:

1) Players take turns placing pieces in the cells of a 5x5x5 cube.
2) The central cell is 'free', that is, it counts for *both* players.
3) 5 in a row wins.

I believe that players capable looking one move ahead will always play
to a draw. Even if the first player is allowed to place THREE PIECES
on her first play, the second player should STILL have no trouble
drawing the game. 5x5x5 is too small for 5-in-a-row (unless there are
a few more 'free' cells). 

Unlike 5-in-a-row games on 2 or 3D boards of larger width, forming a
'double-three' does not force a win. For example, let's ingore the
free cell for now and give 'X' an enormous head start:

      I           II         III          VI          V
  a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e 
1 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .
2 . . . . .   . x . . .   . x x . .   . . . . .   . . . . .
3 . . . . .   . x x . .   . . x . .   . . x x .   . . . . .
4 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . x x .   . . . x .   . . . . .
5 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .

'X' now has a quintuple-three, intersecting at IIIc1. On slightly
larger board, a simple double-three would be a win. But here, 'O'
doesn't even have to respond -- she can let 'X' move again and then
simply block each of the 4-in-a-rows, as they are all single threats:

      I           II         III          VI          V
  a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e 
1 x . . . .   . . . . .   x . x . .   . . . . .   . . . . .
2 . . . . .   . x . . .   . x x . .   . . . . .   . . . . .
3 x . x . .   . x x . .   . . x . .   . . x x .   . . o . o
4 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . x x .   . . . x .   . . . . .
5 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . o . o   . . . . .   . . . . o


For 'X' to win, she has to form a double-FOUR:

      I           II         III          VI          V
  a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e   a b c d e 
1 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . x   . . . . .
2 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . x .   . . . . .
3 . . x . .   . . x . .   . . x . .   . . . . .   . . . . .
4 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . x . . .   . . . . .
5 . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .   . . . . .

If 'X' move's VIc3, she forms a double-four and wins on the next move
-- but I don't see how this can happen unless 'O' goes out to the
kitchen for a snack.
