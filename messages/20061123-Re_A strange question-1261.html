Quoting Patrick's message of Tuesday:

I've been into chess off and on all my life, and right now I'm on an up
cycle.  Lately I've also gotten interested in checkers (draughts),
another recurrent interest.  But I have a thing about doing things in
threes--so I'm wondering what other game would naturally complete this
set.  If I were going to focus on just three abstract (perfect
information, randomizer-free) board games, which game would go best
with chess and checkers?

My first thought was to relax the randomiser-free criteria and go for backgammon, as there is so much gaming equipment to allow you to carry all three around in one box.  But if you want to be strict on that, then your later suggestion of nine men's morris is the obvious choice.  It's historical too.  In the past, these compendium boxes would be square, rather than rectangular, with chess/draughts on one outside surface, nine men's morris on the other and backgammon inside.

I'd still consider tablut/hnefatafl as an alternative, though you'd have to be careful which variant you choose, as some work better than others. One of the Icelandic sagas - I can't remember which at the moment - mentions a gaming board which was for chess and hnefatafl, so they were associated.
