How would a winner of the Hex game, connecting in the
(more or less) horizontal direction, also be a winner of
the Y game? Even with the assumption this player would also
have stones on the 'dense' nodes on the left and right,
how would this connect to the bottom side of the triangle?
If the player has to make additional moves besides playing
in the Hex grid, then this is not the same as Hex.


One can assume that the players will play the two bottom nodes first
(any time one is played, the opponent must take the other one) and
that they will then fill in the quadrangle - after one move there the
other three must be played, and the result will always be that opposite
sides of the Hex-board are connected to the same colour. (If a move is
played inside at once, the other player can "choose sides" for the
Hex-board, which could not be an advantage for the first player.)
