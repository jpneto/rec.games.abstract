I know you intended sarcasm but that kind of stuff is happening already. I
INTEL speed chess tourney they used a 6 minutes for white vs 5 minutes for
black tiebreak. White must win -- any draw counts as a win for black. Black
nearly always gets the point. So the results were being determined by a coin
toss. Better to eleminate as many draws as possible so that tiebreaks are less
needed. Of course, assigning different point values to the various possible
results would make ties even less likely.

The main point of this rebuttal is that I believe that the less likely we
can make ANY TYPE draw in chess, the better the game will be, and regardless
of whether the rules of the game are altered somewhat or just the scoring 
is changed, Kasparov will still be the champ. The world standings will 
most likely be unchanged. But there MAY be more interest in major chess
events by the lay press that MAY contribute to the overall welfare of the
game. Just my opinion with nothing but unscientific anecdotal evidence to 
back me up, but I think the public hates draws and games and sports that
end in draw to often are mocked.
