8x8 draughts (checkers) has not been solved - although the
number of possible legal positions has been calculated by the Chinook
team at Alberta University and 8-man tablebases exist.
See http://www.cs.ualberta.ca/~chinook/databases/databases.html

This number is 500,995,484,682,338,672,639
See http://www.cs.ualberta.ca/~chinook/databases/total.html
   
Given the symmetry of the draughts board & the starting position, I
find it surprising that this number is odd.

Let n = the number of pieces on the board, where 1 <= n <= 24

Then the number of possible legal positions is even, EXCEPT when n = 8

In fact, the positions with 4 checkers v. 4 checkers are alone responsible.
