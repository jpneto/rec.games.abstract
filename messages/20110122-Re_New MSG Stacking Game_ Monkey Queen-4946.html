I don't like to make major structural changes to my games.  I changed the
baby kings to baby queens in response to four suggested improvements, the
common subtext being that Monkey Queen could be made a little more exciting.

Now I've got something that works, and works well.  The 8x8 to 10x10 is
cosmetic.  MQ is scalable and presumed to be playable at various sizes.  I'm
just saying, "Beginners, don't play ten games on the 8x8 and then wish it
was a 10x10.  Just start with the 10x10."  Usually programmers want one
representative board size.  That size would be 10x10 for Monkey Queen.


On a general note: leaving the whole stack idea in favor

Monkey Queen's design certainly had arbitrary choices, as evidenced by the
change from baby kings to baby queens.  But there's an axiom at the heart of
MQ - one which generates a finite game with lots of freedom and variety of
movement.  It's not like Breakthrough (Dan Troyka) where you're marching
pawns from this side to that side.

In Monkey Queen you're moving babies closer to an enemy queen which can
suddenly move somewhere else, causing your babies to change direction and
follow it.  This can't go on indefinitely because the queen is allowed only
a finite number of non-capturing moves.  Lovely.  I call Monkey Queen my
first game-centric design, but its architecture is magnificent.
