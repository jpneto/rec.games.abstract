Email Nomic or regular Nomic?

For the regular variety, it is very helpful to write (or xerox and
paste) each rule on a 3"x5" card, and provide a pack of blank cards for
new rules.  This way you can move rules about, put an amendment on top
of its referent, reinstate old rules easily, and generally manage the
game a lot better than if you wrote them on paper.  You will also end up
with a permanent record of the game, which can be amusing.

Props can be helpful; a recent game involved chess pieces (representing
votes), Scrabble tiles and polyhedral dice.

Four or five players seems good, from what I've heard.

It's also helpful to agree beforehand roughly how pedantic you're going
to be; do you use the spirit of the rule when in doubt, or the letter?
Disagreement on this point makes the game much less pleasant.
