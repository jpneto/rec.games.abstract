How it works?

Player blue place the first token in an empty hex.

He placed 4 then he has to place the second one. He is allowed to place it at any distance equal to 4 hexes starting from the first one so he chooses to place 2. His turn is over.

Now the red player chooses to play his first token the 3 which at 2 hexes from the blue 2. Then he must add the second one 5. Red 5 it at 3 hexes from red 3.

Once finished placing the red player HAS to flip the 2 previous blue token to avoid confusion. So each time a player has to play he has to do it and only the 2 last token will be visible to the 2 players. I could not do it in my diagrams unless creating 3 diagrams. The red turn is finshed

And so on.

The more the board is full players will land on occupied hexes.

They have to remove the occupier and give it back to the owner (if red the red player will take it and reuse it further if blue same process as red)..



The game is not tactical one at all.

Is deeply strategic.

To have an idea on how the game behave as a programmer to make a program with random moves (you have to respect the rules above)


Distances between tokens are allowed diagonaly and horizontaly.
