AFAIK, P-KB3 is f3 if it's a move of white pieces. Black has P-KB3 too, but it is f6 in algebraic notation. So, if you name the board squares, the KB3 square for white is KB6 for black. I mean, I don't know about descriptive notation in USA, but in Brazil it was that way.

This way, the first Ruy López (Spanish Game) moves are:

In algebraic:

1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bb5 a6

In descriptive:

1. P-K4 P-K4
2. N-KB3 N-QB3
3. B-QN5 P-QR3
