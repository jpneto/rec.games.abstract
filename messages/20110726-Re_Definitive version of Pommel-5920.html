On Jul 26, 11:45 am, Nick Bentley <nickobe...@gmail.com> wrote:

Not having much of a feel for checkers, I can't really contribute, but
question: why a hex board and not a square board?


Ah, that's because of the movement of the promoted piece.  In an
orthogonal or diagonal version, its movement is more constrained, to
the point where it can be a disadvantage to promote.  The game works
on all three geometries, but I have settled on hexagonal as less
quirky and more strategic.  I will leave the diagonal version in the
mix, though, since a small version of it can readily be played with a
checkers set.
