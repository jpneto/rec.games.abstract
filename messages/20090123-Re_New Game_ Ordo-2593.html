I gather from figure 6 that the ordo move is more limited than the
description makes it sound: pieces connected in a horizontal line may
only move vertically (and still only forward), and pieces connected
vertically may only move horizontally.  Otherwise the vertically
connected pair on the right could be extended into a threesome and
all three could then shift up one space.  (There'd also be a move by
the horizontal pair just below the vertical pair, which could shift
left one space.)  Figure 8 seems to say this also, but again, it says
"pieces may only move sideways" where the text above says "they may
[move] forward or sideways", and nowhere does it come out and say that
horizontal sets move only forward while vertical sets move only sideways.
