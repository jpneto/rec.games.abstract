Zertz does play very differently with experienced players than with
beginners, but that shouldn't stop you from having fun!  As for
Panther's comment:

For experienced players Zertz will eventually only consist of an
opening and an endgame phase.

... this tends to be true when one player is more experienced than the
other.  Any mistake can be fatal, but if both players are patient, then
the middle game can be present, and very tense.



Zertz is a new game, and you can't expect to find Zertz players which are
really good. But imagine, the game would be played for a few hundred years
by some millions of people among them a few hundred Zertz professionals (this
is just an imaginary voyage). What would happen to the game?

Don't misunderstand me. I think that Zertz is a game with a big potential.
But I also think, that Zertz should be played with slightly different
rules on a much larger board. This would guarantee that Zertz would
remain as deep and "mysterious" as Go. Another comparison: Zertz is like
playing Go on a 9 x 9 board. If that would be the standard way to play Go,
it would be a shame.
