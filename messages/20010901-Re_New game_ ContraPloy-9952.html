Hidden information means that there is luck involved. A game without

I meant: "abstract strategy game"

J. M. Thompson wrote about them:

"Abstract strategy games furthermore minimize the
element of chance.  It is essential to their
definition that such games have perfect
information:  each player, when deciding his move,
must have complete information about the current
position of the board (...)"

http://www.thegamesjournal.com/articles/DefiningtheAbstract.shtml



This definition is illogical, since the word "strategy" has no 
connotation of perfect information.  All reasonable people should reject 
it, and specify "of perfect information" when referring to the relevant 
sub-category of abstract strategy games.
