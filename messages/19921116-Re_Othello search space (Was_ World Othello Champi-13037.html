I seem to remember 7.5 as an average, from Rosenbloom.  This would give
~10^50 instead of 10^60.


But in any case, this is estimating the number of legal games, while "nodes"
suggests to me the number of legal *positions*. This is trivially bounded by
3^64 (each square is empty, white, or black) < 3.5*10^30.


You're right about the bound.  My original statement was misleading.  But
"search space" usually refers to the space through which a program searches as
defined by its operators (in this case, the Othello move), rather than the
graph of unique legal positions.

In any case, the original conclusion still holds: even at 10^30 nodes, it
probably won't be solved.
