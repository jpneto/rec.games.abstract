Geoff Summerhayes wrote:

comparing games on their tactical depth.


There is more than one single possible definition for tactical depth,
game complexity, or whatever you would choose to call it.

1) It is the greatest depth of a legal path in the game's tree,
provided the game can be represented by a game tree. [Problem: a
game of infinite depth could be trivial.]
2) It is its class of complexity in theoretical informatics. 
[Problem: you need quite some maths to prove anything.]
3) It is the shortest time the currently supposedly strongest player
needs to find, understand, and apply a perfect solution. [Problem:
games might require much more than just a lifetime.]

Further problems exist. E.g., how do you measure tactical complexity
of social competence or of infinitely many states of a ball in a ball
game [if we are kind enough to simplify physics]?

If you compare games, then first ask yourself about your objectives.
If your objective is to claim greatest depth, then choose a 
definition so that depth is great and argue in a way that nobody else
notices any shortcomings. If your objective is being objective, then 
explain everything...
