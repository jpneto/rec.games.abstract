Edward D. Collins <ed@edcollins.com> wrote:

This is how I would break down chess:

+----------+-------------+-----------+
| game     | strategy %  | tactics % |
+----------+-------------+-----------+
| chess    |     01      |    99     |
+----------+-------------+-----------+


This probably varies with the level of chess played. I'm not a very good
chess player, but I've seen my mental model of the game shift as I've
gotten better. And my discussions with better players have led me to
believe that they think about the game in ways that I can barely comprehend
at my level.

It's similar to gimmick car rallyes. Beginners just drive the route and try
to catch all the gimmicks along the way. As this approach develops, you can
start to anticipate more gimmicks before you leave the start. But at some
point, experts start to take a different approach, analyzing and
understanding the entire rallye rather than just following the route and
catching gimmicks.
