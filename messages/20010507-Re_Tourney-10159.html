Tourney is a very interesting new game invented by Chris Huntoon. You can
find the rules at:

http://www.zillionsofgames.com/games/tourney.html

The main final goal is to reach your own start row with a "Charger" that
has been promoted to a "Champion" at the other end of the board. There are
also two other possible ways to win, but i think, in good play they will

not

happen often. Huntoon's idea is quite new.


There is a similar idea presented at

http://www.chessvariants.com/diffobjective.dir/utchess.html

which is called Capture The Flag (CTF) Chess

Here are the rules:

--------------------------
CTF Chess

1.The FIDE rules apply except in the following:
2.Before the game starts, each player defines a square (the Base) and drops
its Flag (a special piece) on it.
3.A player wins if he can reach the enemy Base, catch the Flag and return it
to its own Base.
4.To catch the Flag, a piece must move into the enemy Base. Then,
automatically, the Flag is attached to it.
5.A piece can drop the Flag where it stands (counts as a move). If it is not
in a Base, the Flag can be caught by a friendly piece (and returns
automatically to its own Base) or by another enemy piece (and is attached to
it).
6.The King in a non royal piece, so there is no checkmate, stalemate or
castling.

Notation: use [ ] to identify the piece carrying the Flag (e.g.., [R]:h4 )

--------------------
