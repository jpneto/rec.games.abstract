Noughts and Crosses? (Tic-Tac-Toe)



Actually it could well be one of the large number of regional variations
on Mancala/Wari that are played on a non-"standard" board. Murray's
"History of Board Games other than Chess" Illustrates an example from
Benin that has a board with depressions in a 3 x 4 pattern. He also
illustrates varients with 3 x 6 patterns of bowls, and 4x6  and one that
is 4x28


That was an obvious possibility but 3 x 3 is both odd and small - surely an
extreme version unless it's a bent 2 x 4 board with a single staorage area
in the centre.
