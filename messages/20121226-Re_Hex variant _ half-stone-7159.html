El divendres 21 de desembre de 2012 4:14:40 UTC+1, Mark Steere va escriure:


Also, all the cells you've filled are divided left-right.  If that's a rule, that creates an asymmetric, unequal game for Blue and Orange.  If the cells can be divided in any of the three orientations, it's symmetric, but you still have the connectivity problem of the preceding paragraph.


2 ideas:

1) Break the hexagons by the middle of the edges instead of by the vertex.

2) Allow any possible orientation but forbid that 2 adjacent hexagons share the same orientation. That could make some cells unplayable during the game (which is arguably worst than the draws we are trying to avoid).  One possible solution could be to paint in three colors the cells of the board and to assign every color to a given orientation.  That's equivalent to play hex in another board topology. In fact this is equivalent to play Pex (http://www.iggamecenter.com/info/en/pex.html) with a nicer board.
