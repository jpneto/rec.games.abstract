I declared that the goal of "chess" variants is to capture some DEFINED

Draughts belongs to a different group of games where you capture all
stones. This group is further divided into many "families", for
instance, there are games in which you capture only your
opponent's pieces (this group is further subdivided) and games in which

your point of view) when you start the game (Mancala variants - this group

The goal in Octi is not to  capture pieces but to control certain squares.
Capturing pieces is just a means to reach a goal as in Go connections
are a means to make territory (but Go isn't a connection game like Y or
Twixt). You do constantly confuse the goals with the means.

There are only two possible classifications systems of abstract board games:

1rst: a historical approach (doesn't work well with modern games)
2nd: a logic approach based on ONE common feature of all games: I decided
to chose the goal (object) of the game. Every game has a goal, a way
to win or to lose. Usually people mix all these systems together and
get an arbitrary artificial system which is neither historically nor
logically consistent. My system is logically sharp as a knife, but it is
a-historic.
