The game shown in R & G are D is *not* Questions.  It is a mind blisteringly
similar game called "Verbal Tennis" (thus the tennis court).  As with
Questions, the could of the game is to bandy questions back and forth,
without repeating or making a statement.  The conditions of relevancy must
be observed, and unanswerable questions (rhetoric and philosphy) are banned.

Scoring is not exactly like tennis.  The match consists of the best 2 of 3
games rather than 3 of 5 sets of first to win 6 games each (the latter would
wear the novelty rather thin, eh?).  There is no "set", but that doesn't
prevent use of the line "game set and match" to announce victory.

The game pre-dates R & G are D ... it appeared in a novel with a name
like "The Fox in the Air" (memory fails as to whether that is the exact
title, but I think it's close)
