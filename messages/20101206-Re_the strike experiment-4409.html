On Dec 6, 5:11 am, "Daniel S." <antiw...@me.com> wrote:

On your turn:
Place 2 stones or Strike with a stone in play.

Place - 2 stones anywhere on the board not adjacent to your own color

Strike - Slide an already placed stone of your color X number of
spaces in a straight line to an empty cell directly adjacent to a
group of size X enemy stones.  Exchange all of this groups stones with
stones of your color.  A stone may not strike a group it's already
connected to.


Mark already mentioned this: why two. It shifts play from the
strategical to the tactical and it speeds up the game. Both might be
ok, but I'd make it part of the playtesting (possibly you've already
done that and reached the conclusion that two is better).

There's also the possibility that a striking stone connects two groups
in the strike: one of a size equal to the lenghts of the strike, the
other not necessarily (and mostly not). Theoretically the stroking
stone could even end up touching three enemy groups.

Then: the striking stone might connect the enemy target group to one
of the player himself (or even two). The resulting group might easily
be 'oversized'.


The first player to create a size 10 group wins.

Depending on boardsize, but a hardwired number is arbitrary just the
same. Nick's own experiments led to 'the largest group' in
'Byg' (second largest if equal and so on). That's not arbitrary and I
find it more elegant.


The strike is the mechanic which I find intriguing.  The rest is filler..
