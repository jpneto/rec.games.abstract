1. What is rec.games.abstract?			[Paraphrased from the RFD]

	rec.games.abstract is intended for discussion of abstract games
	where strategy, tactics and player skill are paramount.  This
	includes the major category of perfect information, pure-strategy
	(deterministic) games, i.e. those with no element of chance (e.g.
	no dice), and where all information is available (unlike card games
	and Scrabble, for example, where players' hands are hidden).  The
	games under consideration are characterised by a simple board and
	pieces, with a small number of rules.

	Discussions about other games are welcome provided they don't deviate
	too far from this category and provided there is no more suitable
	newsgroup.

	is maintained at ftp.eece.unm.edu [129.24.24.119], in directory

	archive are relative to /pub/rec.games.abstract.

6. Rules for abstract games

	The directory games/ on the FTP archive is organised with a
	subdirectory for each game.  The file `rules' in each directory
	contains rules for the game, usually culled from discussion on
	rec.games.abstract and elsewhere.

	Currently rules are available for: footsteps, shogi, trax, xiangqi,
	pente, sprouts and twixt.

7. Metagame workbench

	Metagame is a workbench for developing and playing metagame
	programs developed by Barney Pell (Barney.Pell@cl.cam.ac.uk).
	The workbench also allows users to enter and modify the
	rules of chess-variants, and test them out against other humans or
	programs.  Features include:

		o	Generator for Symmetric Chess-like Games
		o	Definitions for chess, checkers, shogi and others
		o	Text interface for generating and playing games
		o	Legal move generator
		o	Simple legal players and sophisticated player

	Metagame can be obtained via anonymous ftp from ftp.cl.cam.ac.uk
	[128.232.0.56] in /users/bdp/metagame2.0.tar.Z

	In the same directory are three papers:

	metagame.ps.Z	Metagame: A New Challenge for Games and Learning
	chess-like.ps.Z	Metagame in Symmetric Chess-Like Games
	lpar.ps.Z	Logic Programming for General Game-Playing
