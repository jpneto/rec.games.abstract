The question is which rule has precedence: the one about winning by jumping on or over the goal line (also out of bounds) or the one about not going out of bounds.  The way it's worded in _Winning Ways_ (as far as I know the primary document for Phutball rules) you can only go out of bounds as the last step of move over the goal line, which seems to allow the move in question.

I've always played it as... if the move would take you out of bounds (side to
side) it's illegal.  If it takes you out of bounds (goal to goal) it's
a winning move for the player over whose goal is stepped.

If you step *on* the goal line, but immediately move off... it's a non-winning
move.

But the side-to-side out of bounds takes precedence.

And that's the way it's implemented on my PBeM server <pbmserv@gamerz.net>
