Tony Brown wrote:

> ah hey, abstract is abstract


 ;-)  on one level, all games are abstract...but
 in this case, this group, rec.games.abstract, has
 chosen to narrow their subject down to the kind of
 games like go or chess or othello or even tic-tac-toe
 variations.  while go & chess have plenty of discussion
 within their own groups, some other abstract games
 are not given a group of their own.  that is what this
 group is for.  when a new abstract game hits the scene,
 this group can then dissect it.  it is a good clearing
 house for finding where to get these games, what is
 cool about them, what is defective, what is stolen and
 transmogrified, and so on.

 there are no elements of chance in abstract games.
 there is no hidden information in abstract games.

 also, the very term "abstract" is colored with the
 notion that the playing board and/or pieces are
 reduced to skeletal abstractions of whatever reality
 they may have been inspired by.

 role-playing games, particularily the gazillions of
 games inspired by the Tolkein books, do the reverse.
 they attempt to reconstruct a sense of reality of
 being in the story yourself - a fine goal, but not
 applicable to rec.games.abstract.
