I see your point, Hazen, that extra dimensions arbitrarily and unnecessarily add complexity to, in this case, an already complex game, Chess.  And in general I'm pretty much in agreement with you. Another drawback is the explosion in board size.  Even with the tiny basis of length 4, 4^^4=256.  That's huge.  A 5D board, if perfectly symmetric, would have 1024 cells.  It'd take a month to play.  

What I find interesting in Hyperchess4 is mainly the board.  I find its geometry and connectivity appealing on some level.  I was wondering what a honeycomb 4D board would be like, and if a Hex-like connection could be played on it.  Probably not.  Connection games really need to be 2D.  Otherwise enemy paths can go around one another.
