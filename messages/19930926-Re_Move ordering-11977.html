I announced a perfect play sequence for 6x6 Othello with the standard
starting position in this newsgroup earlier this year (the second player
wins 20-16). 8x8 Othello is another matter!

Counting legal moves for both sides in Othello is certainly expensive
(unless you have special hardware), so I use one of the many crude 
approximations available: counting the number of empty squares next to
the opponent's pieces. The more such empty squares there are,
the more potential moves you have. Not only is this much faster to 
calculate than the number of legal moves, but it also appears to be
a better evaluation function. But I think it is the balance between
positional features and corner evaluation which is most interesting.
Whether I have this approximately right will be revealed by my
(8x8) program's performance in the Paderborn computer Othello tournament
in October.
