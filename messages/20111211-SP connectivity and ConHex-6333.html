Hi,
Here is the connectivity graph of the visible balls in a fully packed
5x5 square pyramid:
http://www.boardgamegeek.com/image/1174727/shibumi

This graph is trivalent, i.e. no more than three balls meet around any
intersection. This property allows the formation of connections
without fear of deadlock, much as connection games on the hexagonal
grid (e.g. Hex and Y) avoid deadlock.

The dual of this graph is equivalent to the ConHex board!
http://www.boardgamegeek.com/image/1174726/shibumi

This appears to be one of those happy mathematical coincidences.
