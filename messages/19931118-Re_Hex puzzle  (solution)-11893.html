 With all the talk about Hex lately, I thought I'd post a
 hex puzzle that occured in a recent email game of mine:

                    A B C D E F G H I J K

                   - - - - - - H H H - -   1
                  - - - - - - - v v H v   2
                 - - v H H H H v H H v   3
                - - - v v v v - v H v   4
               - - H - v H - - - - v   5
              - - - - - v - - H - -   6
             - - - - - - - - - - -   7
            - - - - - - - - - - -   8
           - - - - - - - - - - -   9
          - - - - - - - - - - -   10
         - - - - - - - - - - -   11

 H to play and win (H=horizontal, v=vertical)
 Enjoy! 



  The winning move for H is B1.

  In a nutshell, a C2 response by v will end up laddering up to
  g1, for example: B1-C2-C1-D2-D1-E2-E1-F2-F1. Now, if v moves G2,
  we reply J5, and if v moves J5, we reply H4.

  A similar, but shorter, ladder occurs on the west side, laddering
  over to the H piece at C5. Ex:B1-A1-B2-A2-B3, and H is connected
  on the west.

  Depending on v's responses, these two move sequences could be
  intermingled (ex: B1-A1-B2-C2-C1, etc), but the outcome is still
  the same.

I may be missing something, but I think you're wrong.
H-B1 is met by V-H4, after which H must block at J5
and V can connect her big group to the top with any
move in the area of D1.

                     A B C D E F G H I J K
                    - 1 - 4 - - H H H - -   1
                   - - - - - - - v v H v   2
                  - - v H H H H v H H v   3
                 - - - v v v v 2 v H v   4
                - - H - v H - - - 3 v   5
               - - - - - v - - H - -   6
              - - - - - - - - - - -   7
             - - - - - - - - - - -   8
            - - - - - - - - - - -   9
           - - - - - - - - - - -   10
          - - - - - - - - - - -   11

But it still might be possible for H to block v on the bottom.
I don't think so, but I'm not sure, which is why I didn't
follow up until now.  If I were H I'd play J5 first off,
which threatens to make the B1 move work.  V will have
to respond somewhere at the top; B3 is probably the strongest
reply she has (it solidifies the connection at the top while
helping the stuff at the bottom a little bit).  Then H
has lots of choices, none of which seem very appealing.
How about F9?  It probably doesn't work, but it seems pretty
complicated.  Thus we'd have this position:
