You say on the page that the pattern is topologiclly distinct from a
hexagonal grid.  This isn't quite true, as you get a hexagonal grid if
you combine each yellow pentagon with its blue neighbour.

It could be interesting to use Penrose tilings (see, e.g.,
http://library.thinkquest.org/16661/advanced.math/3.html)
for a Hex-like game.  Such tilings do have points where more than
three tiles meet (in fact, up to 10 can meet in a point).  Another
problem is that the board can't be made symmetric except by 5-way
symmetry, so you can't have a symmetric quadrilateral board.

I wonder if it is possible to make a variant Penrose tiling such that
only three tiles meet at any point?  You would need at least one of
the tiles to have more than 4 edges.
