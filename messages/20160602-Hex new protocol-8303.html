Any game requiring to place 1 stone could be replaced by a new protocol : you remove one of your opponent stone and you place 2 stones. Same protocol for your opponent.
If you apply this new protocol to the classical hex game will you then avoid the pie rule?
You will give to the second player an advantage in closing the game.
First player places 2 stones on board
Second player remove one opponent stone and places 2 new stones etc...
until one of them connects his 2 sides.
I`m just wondering what will be the effect on the game play?
Will it change it?
Or the game will be the same?
This new protocol could be used in other games.
So the problem is : is there equivalence between :
- placing one stone
- and removing 1 and placing 2?
