OK, I'll give it a try.
Keeping your marbles connected is important, but getting a good
center position with a group of marbles also is.
The basic offensive strategy is trying to break up your opponents
marbles in at least two groups, and then keep the smaller group
separated and pushed aside with as large a group of your marbles
as you can.
I believe the position of your marbles (more to the center = better)
is even more important than keeping them connected.
And yes, when both players follow a defensive strategy,
the games can get slow.
