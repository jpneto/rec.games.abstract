in my opinion, you have hit upon what i think is the most undesirable
aspect of chess.  the huge number of drawn games in proportion to the
decisions is tiresome at best.  it is, however, a nasty problem to fix.
so nasty it might be better off left alone.  in the recent fischer-
spassky match, with the new fischer clock in place and with fischer
known to hate draws, there were still 15 out of 30 games drawn - and
that is a lower ratio...one of those karpov-kasparov matches had a
huge number of draws due to the change fischer brought about by not
having draws count for 1/2 points anymore.  it is the smallness of the
board and the limitations on the piece's movements that creates a
balance of power that makes it easier to defend, relatively, than to
kill.  thus to reduce the draws, the game would need drastic alterations
that may make it unrecognizable to its former incarnation.

go would not be improved by a whole integer komi or perpetual check
draw, again, in my opinion.  hey, this is another thing basketball
has over hockey.  i just dont like draws in general.  others may
disagree.
