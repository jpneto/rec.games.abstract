I have encountered, recently, a rumor that an algorithm for playing

I think this might be referring to the following: Go endgames sometimes
break up into (almost) independent subpositions, so that the game is then
a composition of smaller games.  There is a mathematical theory of something
like this composition in WINNING WAYS (Berlekamp, Conway & Guy) and this is 
applied to Go in Berlekamp & Wolfe's new book MATHEMATICAL GO.  This contains 
many artificial endgame problems which a properly programmed computer can win
but a human Go expert who doesn't know the theory can't.
