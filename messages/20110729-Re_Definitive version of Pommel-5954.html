On 26 jul, 17:35, fizzy <ewo...@aol.com> wrote:

When not capturing, a captain may traverse any number of
empty hexagons but must stop on a hexagon that is in an unblocked
horizontal or vertical line from an opposing piece.


Insead, if a captain, when not capturing, had to stop on a hexagon
where it can be captured (i.e. with an empty cell behind it or behind
the line of men it belongs to, if the oppossing piece is a captain,
and also adjacent to it, if it's a soldier), and stalemate were a
loss, then Pommel would be, needless to say, categorically drawless. I
guess the rule I've quoted doesn't mean that.

On the other hand, according to the same rule, captains must stop in
hexagons where they can "see" _any_ oppossing piece, soldier or
captain. If that's so, many trivial draws are possible. Imagine a
position where each player has one captain on any square and one
soldier on a corner cell. Players could move their respective captains
always in line of sight of the oppossing soldier, but never actually
capturing it. Am I right?
