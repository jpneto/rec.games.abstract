Ah, Life, by John Conway. Excellent, intriguing, fantastically complex game,
with simple rules. They go like this:

The game is played on an infinite grid. Each cell has eight neighbors.
Each cell is designated as either "alive" or "empty".

1) A living cell with exactly one or zero living neighbors dies of
   isolation.

2) A living cell with four or more living neighbors dies of overcrowding.

3) An empty cell with exactly three living neighbors becomes alive.

Some interesting formations:

               X                  X
                X             X    X
              XXX              XXXXX

            GLIDER           SPACESHIP

The glider traves diagonally one cell per four generations. The spaceship
traves horizontally one cell per two generations.

I recommend Martin Gardner's _Wheels,_Life,_and_Other_Mathematical_Amusements_.
