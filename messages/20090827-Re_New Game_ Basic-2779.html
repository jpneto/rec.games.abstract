Quite a neat little game, especially considering the design
specifications.

As a strategic game, though, I suspect not one of the greats.
Nowhere near the excellent complexity-generating simplicity
of Atol, for example.

The strategic ideas seem to be (AFAICS) quite simple -
try to make piles with oppo-stones on the bottom, as
far up the board as possible; otherwise common sense
applies, to a largely parity-based substrate.

BTW, it could be made even simpler, or at least "clean-lined",
by using 16 checkers each, with the board starting full.
Though this would not allow a checkers set to be used, OC.
Some backgammon or Othello checkers wiould have to be added in...
